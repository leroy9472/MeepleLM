### 1. Lore & Objective
In Dungeoneer, you play as a hero exploring a dangerous realm, completing quests to gain experience and power. The game blends cooperative map-building with competitive card play.

**Victory Conditions:**
- **As a Hero:** Complete 3 quests, return to the Entrance, and exit the dungeon.
- **As the Dungeonlord (in multiplayer):** Defeat all other heroes.
- **In Solo Play:** Complete 3 quests, defeat the dungeon boss (optional advanced rule), return to the Entrance, and spend 2 Movement points to exit.

### 2. Components
Each Dungeoneer set includes:
- **Adventure Cards (55 total):**
  - 31 Adventure cards (Boons, Treasures, Banes, Encounters)
  - 10 Map cards
  - 7 Quest cards
  - 3 Hero cards
  - 3 Tracker cards
  - 1 Cut-out card (hero and marker tokens)
- **Tokens:**
  - Hero tokens (standees)
  - Marker tokens (for item locations)
  - Glory and Peril tokens (e.g., colored beads or coins)
  - Wound and health tokens
- **Other:**
  - 6-sided dice (2 per player recommended)
  - Player mats (optional, for tracking)

Decks can be separated by card back or category:
- **Glory Deck:** Boons and Treasures (blue and gold borders)
- **Peril Deck:** Banes and Encounters (gray and red borders)
- **Quest Deck:** Purple cards
- **Map Deck:** Black (Dungeon) or white (Wilderness) bordered cards

### 3. Setup
1. **Prepare Tokens:** Cut out and assemble hero and marker tokens.
2. **Sort & Shuffle Decks:** Separate cards by type and shuffle each deck.
3. **Roll for Initiative:** Highest die roll goes first.
4. **Assign Heroes:** Each player takes one Hero card. Place a die showing "6" over "Level: 1" to indicate 6 Life.
5. **Assign Quests:** Deal 2 Personal Quests to each player. Reveal 1 Global Quest.
6. **Start the Map:**
   - Place the Entrance Map card in the center.
   - Attach one random passage card to each side of the Entrance.
   - Place all hero tokens on the Entrance.
7. **Track Resources:**
   - Each player places Glory and Peril tokens on "1" of their Tracker card.
   - Receive 1 Glory and 1 Peril for starting at the Entrance.
8. **Draw Starting Hand:**
   - **Multiplayer:** Draw 5 Adventure cards.
   - **Solo:** Draw 3 cards from the Glory deck.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
A full turn consists of five phases:

#### 1. Reset Phase
- Reset all once-per-turn abilities.
- Cards with upkeep costs must be paid for or return to hand.
- Reset Movement points to match hero's Speed.

#### 2. Dungeonlord Phase
- Use **opponents' Peril** (not your own) to play Banes and Encounters.
- **Encounters:**
  - Play all cards that can be funded by target's Peril.
  - Choose attack form (Melee, Magic, Speed) for combat cards.
  - Resolve in order: Attack → Response → Resolution.
- **Banes:** Can be played at any time during this phase.
- **Threats:** Not attacks. Roll die + relevant stat vs. target number. Succeed or fail based on result.

#### 3. Build Phase
- Draw one Map card.
- Place it in a legal, connected position (adjacent to existing map, parallel to Entrance).
- Must maintain continuous, accessible map.

#### 4. Hero Phase
Spend Movement and Glory to:
- **Move:** 1 Movement per space. Collect Glory/Peril on entry.
- **Explore:** Spend 1 Movement to draw and place a Map card.
- **Attempt Quest:** Meet criteria (e.g., Slay, Escort, Threat). First attempt per turn is free.
- **Play Cards:** Spend Glory to play Boons and Treasures into Inventory.
- **Special Actions:**
  - **Challenge:** Spend 1 Movement to initiate combat with a monster or hero.
  - **Untip Hero:** Spend 1 Movement if immobilized.
  - **Escape:** If surrounded, roll Speed Threat to flee (3 + fastest monster's Speed +1 per extra monster +2 if you attacked).

#### 5. Discard/Draw Phase
- **Must discard one card** if you have any in hand (from hand, Inventory, or Pack).
- Draw up to 5 cards (multiplayer) or 3 cards (solo).
- If deck is empty, reshuffle discard pile.

### 5. Core Mechanics
#### Glory & Peril
- **Glory:** Used to play Boons and Treasures. Gained by wounding monsters.
- **Peril:** Used by others to play Banes/Encounters against you. Gained when your monster wounds a hero.

#### Combat
- **Standard Attack (●):** Can initiate or counter-attack.
- **Counter-Attack (◆):** Can only respond to an attack; deals 1 wound if successful.
- **Roll:** Attacker and defender each roll 1d6 + relevant stat (Melee, Magic, Speed).
- **Hit:** Higher total wins. Tie = no damage.
- **Hit Effect:** Starburst symbol indicates special effect instead of default wound.

#### Map Cards
- **Entries:** Can be Open, Locked, Trapped, or Wall.
  - **Open:** Free movement.
  - **Locked:** Threat roll; failure costs 1 Movement.
  - **Trapped:** Threat roll; failure causes 1 wound.
  - **Wall:** Impassable.
- **Obstacles:** Activated when entering a space:
  - **Pit:** Fail Threat → hero tipped.
  - **Spikes:** Fail Threat → take 1 wound.
  - **Portal:** Spend 1 Movement to switch between Dungeon and Wilderness maps.

#### Adventure Cards
- **Boons (Blue):** Skills/spells. Limited per hero.
- **Treasures (Gold):** Items. Limited per hero. Can be stored in Storage Items.
- **Banes (Gray):** Negative effects. Played using others' Peril.
- **Encounters (Red):** Monsters/Traps. Played on a hero's space.

#### Quest Types
- **Slay:** Defeat a monster.
- **Escort:** Transport an item between locations.
- **Threat:** Pass a Threat roll.
- **Sacrifice:** Pay cost (e.g., Life) for reward.
- **Search:** Find hidden objective via Threat roll, then complete quest.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
- **Winning:**
  - Complete 3 quests.
  - Return to Entrance.
  - Spend 2 Movement points to exit (solo) or 1 (some variants).
- **Losing:** Hero is defeated (0 Life).
- **Quest Rewards:** Level up, gain Life, Glory, or Peril.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Can I move through a space I’ve already visited this turn?** No. Glory/Peril only gained once per space per turn.
- **Can I play a Response card during my own turn?** Only if attacked by another player.
- **What happens to a monster that survives combat?** It goes to your Pack (max 3) or back to hand.
- **Can I use a card that says "opponent" in solo play?** Yes — "opponent" refers to you.
- **Can I camp?** Only in variants that include the Camp action (costs max Movement + 5 Glory; gain 2 Life, increase Peril).
- **How is Peril increased in solo play?**
  - Level 1/4/7: +0
  - Level 2/5/8: +1
  - Level 3/6/9: +3
  - Level 4/7/10: +5
