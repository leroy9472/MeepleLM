# Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
In the world of Hyboria, players control kingdoms vying for dominance through military conquest, political intrigue, and control over Conan the Cimmerian. The goal is to earn the most Empire Points (EPs) through territorial control, completing objectives, and dominating adventure token categories.

## 2. Components
- Kingdom boards and cards (Aquilonia, Turan, Hyperborea, Stygia)
- Plastic army units, emissaries, Conan figure
- Gold and sorcery tokens
- Adventure tokens (monsters, treasures, women)
- Strategy cards and bidding tokens
- Objective cards, artifact cards, Conan bonus card
- Fate dice (7 total)
- Control markers (forts, towers, cities)
- Raider tokens, "Crom, Count the Dead!" tokens
- Adventure and destination markers

## 3. Setup
1. Each player selects a kingdom and takes:
   - 5 army units and 4 emissaries (Aquilonia, Turan)
   - 4 army units, 4 emissaries, and 2 sorcery tokens (Hyperborea, Stygia)
   - 3 gold, bid tokens, kingdom cards
2. Place starting armies in home province.
3. Shuffle objective cards; place face-up equal to player count.
4. Each player draws 2 kingdom cards and 2 strategy cards.
5. Randomly distribute artifact cards and Conan bonus card.
6. Place Conan in Cimmeria.
7. Shuffle 4 adventure cards to form the adventure deck; reveal top card.
8. Place destination marker in province shown on revealed adventure card.
9. Draw adventure tokens equal to adventure length; place face-up as track.

## 4. Gameplay Flow
The game consists of 3 Ages. Each Age has 4 adventures. Per turn:
1. Conan Player may move Conan
2. Play/refresh play-on-the-table cards
3. Select and resolve a Fate Die action
4. End of turn: if adventure track empty, resolve adventure, draw new card, bid for Conan
5. After 4 adventures, conduct Age Change Phase

## 5. Core Mechanics

### Bid for Conan
1. Each player draws 1 strategy card.
2. Simultaneously reveal 1 strategy card and bid token.
3. Total = strategy card adventure rating + bid token value.
4. Highest total wins; ties broken by:
   - Fewest adventure tokens
   - Nearest home to Conan
   - Random
5. Discard played cards; keep bid tokens (except 0 token holder regains all used tokens).
6. Conan Player receives first-player marker.

### Fate Die Actions
Players choose one die from pool (7 dice). Actions:

**COURT + CONAN**
- Conan Player: Move Conan + place raider token
- Non-Conan: Take leftmost adventure token
- Then perform Court action

**COURT Options:**
- Draw 1 kingdom + 1 strategy card
- Draw 2 strategy cards
- Play kingdom event card

**MILITARY Options:**
- Redeploy armies twice
- Redeploy once, then attack
- Place 1 army in up to 2 friendly provinces

**INTRIGUE Options:**
- Move up to 2 emissaries
- Move 1 emissary + start intrigue contest
- Move 1 emissary + remove from enemy province to gain gold = rating
- Build 1 emissary in friendly province

**MILITARY/INTRIGUE**: Choose Military or Intrigue action
**WILD**: Choose Military, Intrigue, or Court action

### Movement
- Armies: Move through adjacent friendly provinces; max 5 per province (except home)
- Emissaries: Move to adjacent province; may continue through friendly provinces or other emissaries

### Military Contests
**Campaign (Neutral Province):**
- Attacker: 1 die per army unit (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Defender: Province rating dice (+1 if Conan present and not CP)
- Raider tokens: Defender counts Axe as hits
- Attacker wins: Advance on track
- Attacker loses: Remove 1 army unit
- Forced march: Remove 1 unit to continue immediately

**Siege (Enemy Province):**
- Attacker: 1 die per army unit (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Defender: Max(province rating, army units) dice (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Attacker wins: Remove enemy unit or control marker
- Defender wins: Remove attacking unit
- Winner gets "Crom, Count the Dead!" token

**Battle (Two Players in Neutral):**
- Attacker: 1 die per army unit (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Defender: 1 die per army unit (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Standard retreat rules apply
- Winner gets "Crom, Count the Dead!" token

### Intrigue Contests
**Convert Neutral to Ally:**
- Attacker: 1 + 1 per adjacent friendly province/emissary (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Defender: Province rating (+1 if Conan present and not CP)
- Raider tokens: Defender counts Axe as hits
- Win: Replace emissary with tower, gain gold = rating

**Convert Enemy Ally to Neutral:**
- Attacker: 1 + 1 per adjacent friendly province/emissary (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Defender: Province rating + 1 (+1 if CP and Conan present)
- Win: Remove enemy tower, emissary remains
- Lose: Remove emissary

### Conan Mechanics
- Conan Player adds +1 die (max 6 total) and Hit+Conan counts as 2 successes when in same province
- Non-Conan attacking neutral with Conan: Defender gets +1 die and Hit+Conan as 2 successes
- Two non-Conan players: Conan has no effect

## 6. Scoring & End Game
Game ends when:
- Fourth adventure of third Age completes, OR
- Any player attempts to crown Conan in third Age

**Final Scoring:**
1. Resolve raids: -1 EP per raider token; remove by sacrificing army unit
2. Gold: Gain amount equal to ratings of provinces with towers/cities
3. EPs: Gain rating of provinces with forts/cities
4. Objectives: Score for completed objectives
5. Bonuses:
   - Richest Kingdom: +3 EP (tie: +1 each)
   - Most "Crom, Count the Dead!": +3 EP (tie: +1 each)
   - Highest in each adventure token category: +5 EP (tie: +2 each)

**Crowning Conan (Third Age Only):**
- If Conan in CP's home province, CP may attempt to crown
- All reveal tokens in one category
- CP wins with highest total: +3 EP, only CP can score token dominance
- CP fails: CP eliminated, game ends

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Ties in Artifact Bidding**: Artifact not awarded
- **Bid Token 0**: Player regains all used tokens after bid
- **Max Dice**: Normally 5, can be 6 with Conan's presence
- **Contest Resolution**: Defender wins ties
- **Play-on-Table Cards**: Refreshed automatically during Age Change Phase
- **Multiple Contests**: Each new roll requires new strategy cards and sorcery use
