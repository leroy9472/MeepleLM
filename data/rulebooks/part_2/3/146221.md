## Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
In the world of Forge War, the wild lands grow restless and threaten the kingdom. As leaders of adventurer guilds, players must gather resources, forge weapons, and complete quests to earn prestige from the king. The objective is to amass the most prestige by the end of the game through successful quest completion, resource management, and strategic use of adventurers and weapons.

2. Components  
- 37 double-sided interlocking mine pieces (6 each of Stage I, II, III central and border hexes + 1 central tile)  
- 180 resources:  
  - 40 copper ingots (orange)  
  - 40 iron ingots (black)  
  - 40 mithril ingots (blue)  
  - 20 emeralds (green)  
  - 20 rubies (red)  
  - 20 diamonds (white)  
- 82 quest cards:  
  - 18 green Stage I quests (plus 2 epic-only)  
  - 9 purple Stage I endgame quests  
  - 20 grey Stage II quests  
  - 9 purple Stage II endgame quests  
  - 12 red Stage III quests  
  - 12 purple Stage III endgame quests  
- 108 market cards:  
  - Weapon cards (3 types across stages)  
  - Bonus cards (market stalls and savvy cards)  
  - Single-use cards  
- 8 starting weapon designs:  
  - "Copper Dagger" ×4  
  - "Copper Sword" ×4  
- Player elements per player:  
  - 1 player mat (2-sided: normal/epic)  
  - 3–4 adventurer tiles with level 1 tokens  
  - 3 overseer meeples  
  - 10 worker pickaxes  
  - 5 disk markers  
  - 6 claim cubes  
  - 5 white quest tracker cubes  
  - 4 randomized Stage I starting wealth cards (normal) or 4 fast-forward wealth cards (variant)  
- Additional for epic game:  
  - 20 champion title tokens per player  
- Other components:  
  - Round tracker  
  - 15 bonus tiles (optional)  
  - "Started Quest" tokens  
  - "+1 weapon power" tokens  
  - Gold coins (value 5 and 1)  
  - 100/200 prestige tokens  

3. Setup  
- Each player collects:  
  - 2 starting weapon designs ("Copper Dagger", "Copper Sword")  
  - 3 overseers, 10 workers, 5 disk markers, 6 claim cubes, 5 white tracker cubes  
  - 5 white quest tracker cubes  
  - 4 randomized Stage I starting wealth cards (choose 2)  
  - 1 player mat (correct side up)  
  - 3 adventurers with level 1 tokens (normal) or 4 (epic)  
  - Champion tokens (epic only)  

- Place components:  
  - Disk marker on 0 of prestige track  
  - Disk markers on turn order track (random order)  
  - 2 disk markers on merchant (buy/sell tracking)  
  - Round tracker on Stage I, Round 1  

- Bonus Tiles (epic only, optional):  
  - Shuffle 15 bonus tiles  
  - Place 1 on Rounds 4 and 5 of Stage I  
  - Place 2 each on Rounds 1–2 and 4–5 of Stages II and III  
  - If using bonus tiles, start with 1 fewer adventurer (3 total)  

- Quest Deck:  
  - Normal: 18 green Stage I quests (excluding "Investigate the Kidnappings" and "The Cursed Keep") + 9 purple Stage I endgame quests (green on top)  
  - Epic: All green (20), grey (20), red (12), and purple (12) Stage III quests stacked in order (green→grey→red→purple)  

- Market Setup:  
  - Separate green market cards by type (weapon, bonus, single-use)  
  - Weapon deck → weapon space  
  - Split bonus deck into 2 equal stacks (6 cards each) → bonus spaces  
  - Split single-use deck similarly → single-use spaces  
  - Reveal top card of each stack  
  - Place 4 public starting weapon designs below board:  
    - "Copper Spear", "Copper Mace", "Copper Axe", "Iron Sword"  

- Supply:  
  - Place all resources, extra adventurers, level markers, and coins next to board  

- Mine Construction:  
  - Randomly assemble 6 Stage I central hexes + 1 center tile into hexagonal array (side length 4)  
  - Surround with 6 Stage I border tiles  
  - Orientation based on player count:  
    - 4 players: all border tiles resource-side up  
    - 3 players: 3 contiguous border tiles (2 long, 1 short) resource-side down  
    - 2 players: all border tiles resource-side down  

- Starting Wealth:  
  - Each player chooses 2 of 4 starting wealth cards and collects bonuses  

- Overseer Placement:  
  - In turn order, players place 1 overseer per round until all 3 are placed on unoccupied hexes  

4. Gameplay Flow  
Each round consists of 3 phases: Mine, Market, Quest.  
- Start of Round:  
  - Replenish market (reveal top card of each deck)  
  - Replenish quest offer (move cards forward, refill empty spots)  

- Mine Phase:  
  - In turn order, each player moves one overseer  
  - Optional: First player may take second move (locks into last turn order)  
  - After movement:  
    - Generate worker on starting hex (same color)  
    - Workers passed through change color:  
      - Opponent → current player’s color  
      - Own color → defect to player with fewest workers (tie: earliest in turn order)  
    - All affected workers generate 1 resource of their hex type for their owner  
  - Check for strikes: remove any group of 5+ same-colored workers  
  - Rearrange turn order:  
    - Order by ascending worker count  
    - Ties: reverse previous order  
    - Exception: player who took second move is locked into last place  

- Market Phase:  
  - Players take one action in turn order (or pass for 1 gold):  
    - Blacksmith (closed): Buy face-up unique weapon design  
    - Bazaar (closed): Buy one face-up bonus or single-use card  
    - Barracks (closed): Hire level 1 adventurer (4 gold) and/or level up adventurer (cost = current level in gold)  
    - Clerk (open): Claim public weapon design (pay card cost + 1 gold per existing claim)  
    - Road (open): Move one quest tracker forward 1 step (free; Adventurer’s Savvy adds +1 per card)  
  - After all actions:  
    - Unpurchased market cards go to discard pile  
    - Return player disks  

- Quest Phase:  
  - Quest Acquisition:  
    - In turn order, pay to acquire one quest:  
      - First 2 slots: free  
      - Next 3: 1 gold  
      - Last 3: 2 gold  
    - Place tracker on first step  
  - Quest Management:  
    - For each active quest:  
      - Equip adventurers with weapons (resource cost paid, weapon power = sum)  
      - Total weapon power must meet or exceed current leg difficulty  
      - If required weapon type not met, difficulty +3 per missing type  
  - Quest Rewards:  
    - Advance all quest trackers 1 step  
    - If leg completed: collect rewards (resources, gold, adventurers, movements, market cards)  
    - If quest completed:  
      - Gain full prestige  
      - Return adventurers to pool (weapons lost)  
      - Level up adventurers (1 level per leg, max 1 per adventurer)  
      - Champions may gain title-specific rewards  

- End of Round:  
  - Move round tracker forward  
  - Remove frontmost quest(s) based on player count:  
    - 4 players: 1 slot  
    - 3 players: 2 slots  
    - 2 players: 3 slots  
  - In normal game, after Round 6: remove all green quests, leave only purple endgame quests  
  - In epic game, after Round 12 and 18: end of stage procedures  

5. Core Mechanics  
- Adventurers:  
  - Start at level 1, max level 4  
  - Level 1: can equip swords/daggers  
  - Level 2: any public weapon  
  - Level 3+: can equip unique weapons  
  - Level up via quest completion, Training card, Barracks, or wealth cards  

- Weapons:  
  - Weapon designs allow forging by spending resources  
  - Public designs: claim with cube, shared  
  - Unique designs: buy and keep  
  - Weapon types: Axe (⧖), Mace (⧗), Spear (⧘)  
  - Required types on quests: penalty +3 per missing type  

- Market Cards:  
  - Weapon: enables forging  
  - Bonus:  
    - Market stalls: exchange resources (1:2 or 2:1) once per round  
    - Savvy cards:  
      - Buyer’s: +1 buy from merchant  
      - Seller’s: +1 gold when selling  
      - Trader’s: +1 use of market stall per round  
      - Adventurer’s: +1 quest progress per Road action  
  - Single-use: immediate effect (e.g., hire, train, move)  

- Merchant:  
  - Buy: pay gold for resources (price chart)  
  - Sell: gain gold for resources  
  - Savvy cards modify buy/sell amounts  
  - Actions can be taken anytime during round  

- Champions (epic game only):  
  - Level 4 adventurers become champions  
  - Gain title with special ability (e.g., Opportunistic, Valorous)  
  - Titles grant ongoing or completion bonuses  

6. Scoring & End Game  
- Normal Game: ends after Round 7  
- Epic Game: ends after Round 6 of Stage III  
- Final Scoring:  
  - Active quests immediately fail (gain prestige from last completed leg)  
  - Prestige from:  
    - Adventurers: +1 per level, +1 extra for level 4 (total 5)  
    - Market cards: sum of prestige values (unique weapons, bonus cards, claimed public weapons)  
    - Normal game only:  
      - Blacksmith bonus: 2 prestige per unique weapon, 1 per claimed public weapon (excl. starting)  
      - Bazaar bonuses:  
        - 1 per completed quest  
        - 1 per employed adventurer  
        - 2 per market bonus card  
        - 1 per leftover resource  
    - Sell all leftover resources (no Seller’s Savvy), gain 1 prestige per 4 gold  
  - Highest prestige wins; tie: earlier in turn order  

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- Can I change a weapon on an adventurer mid-quest?  
  Yes, but original resources are lost to supply.  
- Can I move an adventurer from one quest to another?  
  Yes, but weapon is lost and new weapon must be forged.  
- What happens if I fail a quest on the same round I acquired it?  
  Lose 3 prestige for disappointing the king.  
- Can I use multiple Savvy cards?  
  Yes, except for market stalls (only one copy usable).  
- How are bonus tiles resolved?  
  At end of Rounds 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, 18 (epic only), after quest phase, before new round.  
- Can I use merchant actions during another player’s turn?  
  Yes, at any time during a round.  
- What happens to market stalls when I use them?  
  Rotate card to indicate used; resets next round.  
- Can I use the Road action to complete a quest?  
  No, Road only advances by 1 step; quest completion happens during rewards phase.  
- Can I use a weapon with no power (e.g., Ward of Gods)?  
  Yes, but it contributes 0 base power (may gain power via champion).
