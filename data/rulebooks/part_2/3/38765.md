# Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective
In *Ground Floor*, players assume the role of entrepreneurs building a startup company from the ground up. As CEOs, they must strategically manage time, money, information, and employees to grow their business, improve their office, and increase popularity. The goal is to become the most prestigious company by earning the most Prestige Points through business expansion, remodeling, and innovation.

### 2. Components
- 1 Main Game Board
- 5 Player Boards (one per player)
- Player-specific components per player:
  - 1 CEO Marker
  - 2 Player Markers
  - 25 Time Markers (in player color)
  - 1 Player Aid
  - 1 Specialty Tile
  - 1 Matching Remodeled Room Tile
- General Stock:
  - Money Tokens (1, 3, 5 denominations)
  - Information Tokens (1, 3, 5 denominations)
  - Supply Cubes
  - Untrained Tokens
  - Remodeled Room Tiles (6 per player)
  - 13 Tenant Improvement (T.I.) Tiles
  - 31 Floor Improvement Tiles
  - 2 Status Markers (Rounds Track, Job Market)
  - 20 Economic Forecast Cards (Depression, Recession, Stable, Boom)
  - 10 Player Markers (2 per player color)

### 3. Setup
1. Place the main board in the center.
2. Each player chooses a color and takes corresponding CEO marker, 2 player markers, and 25 time markers.
3. Each player receives:
   - 1 Player Board
   - 1 Player Aid
   - 7 Info Tokens
   - 1 Supply Cube
   - 1 random Specialty Tile
   - Matching Remodeled Room Tile (place over corresponding room on Player Board)
4. Place remaining components in the general stock.
5. Place Status Markers:
   - One on space 1 of the Rounds Track.
   - One on space marked 5 (5 Money + 5 Info) of the Job Market Track.
6. Shuffle Economic Forecast cards into four piles (Depression, Recession, Stable, Boom). Discard half from each pile randomly. Shuffle remaining 10 cards into a deck, place face down on "Future" space. Move top card face down to "Current" space.
   - **Important**: First card must not be Depression or Boom. Reshuffle if needed.
7. Stack one player marker from each player randomly on space 1 of the Popularity Track. Top marker determines first player.
8. Place one Supply Cube on each space in the Warehouse.
9. Place Stage I Floor and Tenant Improvement tiles next to the Construction Company. Keep Stage II and III tiles off to the side.
10. In 2- or 3-player games: Return extra copies of each Improvement tile to the box (only one copy available per tile).
11. Place all player markers on the "No Bonus" space of the Marketing Bonus track.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
The game is divided into **3 Stages**, each with **3 Rounds**, for a total of 9 rounds. Each round has **5 Phases**:

#### Phase 1: Receive Income
- Players receive money based on CEO position on Employee Track.
- Some Improvements provide additional Money or Info.
- If a player has 5 or more employees, they must pay $3. If unable, they must fire one employee (discard 3 time markers, move CEO down one space).

#### Phase 2: Hire Employees
- Players hire in Popularity order (most popular first).
- Each hire costs current Job Market price (Money + Info).
- After each hire, move Job Market marker one space right (max 7 + 7).
- After all players pass, the most popular player may hire again—continues until all pass.
- For each new hire:
  - Move CEO up one space on Employee Track.
  - Place an Untrained Token on that employee’s space.
- **Note**: Time markers for new employees are not available until after training in a future round.

#### Phase 3: Schedule Business
- Players act in Popularity order.
- Assign Time Markers from Employee Track to tasks on Player Board or Main Board.
- CEO provides 4 Time Markers; each additional employee provides 3.
- Player Board tasks resolve immediately.
- Main Board tasks are scheduled but resolved in next phase.
- **Ground Floor Rooms** can be used unlimited times per phase.
- **Office Remodel**: At any time, spend $3 and 3 Info to remodel any Ground Floor room. Take Remodeled Room tile from stock and place over corresponding room. Benefit is immediate.

#### Phase 4: Conduct Business
Resolve areas on Main Board left to right:
- **A. Consulting Firm**:
  - Left column: Pay cost to place Time Marker.
  - Right column: Players receive 10 Info only if corresponding left space is occupied.
  - After resolution: Right column markers return to general stock; left column markers slide to right.
- **B. Advertising Agency**:
  - Schedule: Pay $1 and 1 Info to place in first available space.
  - Conduct: Resolve in reverse order (last to place chooses first).
  - Choose one of:
    - **Networking**: Move marker to Networking box + add 1 free Time Marker from stock.
    - **Print**: Move marker to Print box + pay $1 and 1 Info.
    - **Broadcast**: Move marker to Broadcast box + pay $2 and 2 Info.
  - Resolve each type in Popularity order:
    - Networking: +1 Popularity per 3 markers.
    - Print: +1 Popularity per 2 markers.
    - Broadcast: +1 Popularity per 1 marker.
    - +1 additional Popularity if player has sole majority in a type.
    - Markers that contributed to Popularity advance go to general stock; others remain.
- **C. Marketing Bonus**:
  - Not used in Schedule phase.
  - In Conduct phase, players (except least popular) choose one bonus in Popularity order:
    - 1 Time Marker for next round
    - 2 Info
    - 2 Money
    - 1 Supply Cube
    - Remodel 1 Room ($1 + 1 Info)
  - Each bonus can be chosen only once.
  - **Note**: Not used in 2-player games.
- **D. Warehouse**:
  - Schedule: Pay Money or Info (not both) to place Time Marker on any space.
  - Conduct: Collect Supply Cubes adjacent to your Time Markers.
  - Must have storage space (Storage Closet); otherwise, discard.
- **E. Factory**:
  - Schedule: Pay $2, 2 Info, and 1 Supply Cube to place Time Marker in first available space.
  - Conduct: In numerical order, move markers to Retail Outlets and set price (6, 8, 12, or Liquidation).
  - If Retail Outlets are full, place in Liquidation (immediate $3).
- **F. Economic Forecast**:
  - Not used in Schedule phase.
  - Conduct: After Factory resolution, reveal the Current Economic Forecast card.
- **G. Retail Outlets**:
  - Not used in Schedule phase.
  - Conduct: Based on Forecast card, a number of consumers buy products.
  - Consumers buy from lowest price bracket first, and within bracket, from most popular.
  - After all consumers buy or sell out: Unsold products drop to lower brackets (including Liquidation), from lowest up, in Popularity order.
  - Players choose new price within bracket.
  - Any product in Liquidation sells immediately for $3.
- **H. Construction Company**:
  - Schedule: Pay $4 and 4 Info to place Time Marker in first available space.
  - Conduct: In numerical order, players may buy an Improvement:
    - **Tenant Improvement (T.I.)**: $1 + 1 Info
    - **Floor**: $2 + 2 Info + $2 + 2 Info per existing floor
  - Place Floors above previous ones (Specialty Tile on top).
  - T.I.s replace a Ground Floor room or go on an Empty Floor.
  - Cannot buy an Improvement already owned.
  - If unable or unwilling, move Time Marker to general stock.
  - **Stage Availability**:
    - Rounds 1–3: Stage I only
    - Rounds 4–6: Stage I and II
    - Rounds 7–9: Stage I, II, and III
  - At end of Round 3, add Stage II Improvements.
  - At end of Round 6, add Stage III Improvements.

#### Phase 5: Reorganize
1. Move all Time Markers from Player Boards to general stock.
2. In Popularity order, players may fire employees (move CEO down, discard 3 Time Markers per employee).
3. Replenish Time Markers based on Employee Track (CEO: 4, others: 3 each).
4. Reset all Marketing Bonus markers to "No Bonus".
5. Add 1 Supply Cube to the most expensive empty space in Warehouse (if full, skip).
6. Move all Popularity markers one space left (in reverse order, stacked markers stay intact).
7. Move Job Market marker left according to "Unemployed" value on current Forecast card (minimum 2 + 2).
8. Move top card of Future Forecast deck face down to Current space.
9. Advance Round marker one space right.

### 5. Core Mechanics
- **Time Management**: Players assign limited Time Markers to actions.
- **Popularity Order**: Determines turn order and consumer purchasing priority.
- **Employee Hiring & Training**: New hires require training (via Training room) before Time Markers are usable.
- **Remodeling**: Upgrade Ground Floor rooms for better abilities.
- **Improvement Purchases**: Expand vertically with Floors or enhance with T.I.s.
- **Economic Forecast**: Drives consumer behavior and Job Market adjustments.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
The game ends **before** the Reorganize phase of the round in which **either**:
- The 9th round’s Conduct Business phase is completed, or
- A player buys their 5th Floor Improvement (6th floor including Ground Floor).

**Scoring**:
- Remodeled Rooms: 2 PP each
- Tenant Improvements: 3 PP each (except T.I. Achievement: 1 PP + bonus)
- Floors: 2–8 PP each (as printed)
- Achievements: Bonus PP as described
- **Money & Info Bonus**: 1 PP for every group of $3 and 3 Info (combined)

**Final Scoring Bonuses**:
- **T.I. Achievement**: +1 PP per T.I. owned (including itself)
- **Improvement Achievement**: +1 PP per Remodeled Room
- **Retention Achievement**: +1 PP per employee
- **Production Achievement**: +3 PP per Supply Cube in Storage
- **Marketing Achievement**: +2 PP per Popularity level attained
- **Executive Achievement**: +2 PP per "Achievement" Floor owned
- **Skyline Achievement**: +1 PP per Floor owned

**Winner**: Player with most Prestige Points.  
**Tiebreaker**: Most popular player among tied players.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Q**: Can I use a Ground Floor room more than once per Schedule phase?  
  **A**: Yes, unlimited uses.
- **Q**: Can I remodel a room that already has a Remodeled tile?  
  **A**: No. Only one remodel per room.
- **Q**: What happens to Time Markers in Advertising Agency if they don’t contribute to Popularity?  
  **A**: They stay in the Agency for future use.
- **Q**: Can I place multiple Time Markers on one task?  
  **A**: Yes, if the task allows (e.g., multiple spaces).
- **Q**: Can I hire employees if I can’t afford the Job Market cost?  
  **A**: No. You may only hire if you can pay.
- **Q**: In 2-player games, are Marketing Bonuses used?  
  **A**: No, unless using the variant rule where the most popular player decides.
