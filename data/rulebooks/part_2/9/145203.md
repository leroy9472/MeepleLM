## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
Prosperity is a strategic board game set across seven decades of technological development, from 1970 to 2030. Players represent nations striving to balance economic growth, energy production, ecological responsibility, and technological advancement. The goal is to earn the most Victory Points (VP) by the end of the game through effective management of resources, pollution, research, and technology adoption.

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
- Play proceeds clockwise over a total of 36 turns (7 decades).  
- Each turn consists of two phases:  
  1. **Draw and Score Phase** (triggered by drawing a Technology tile).  
  2. **Action Phase** (active player takes 2 actions).  
- Game ends after the 36th turn (when last Technology tile is drawn).

4. **Research**:  
   - Move Research markers forward equal to number of Research symbols on player board (split between tracks).  
   - Bonus VP:  
     - **+3 VP** to highest on each research track.  
     - **+1 VP** to second-highest.  
     - **Tie for 1st**: both get **+2 VP**, no 2nd place awarded.  
     - **Tie for 2nd**: **no VP** awarded for 2nd place.  

5. **Prosperity**:

   - **Only if** there are **no pollution discs** in the top triangle space.  

**Winning**: Player with most VP wins.  
**Tiebreaker**: Most leftover money.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Pollution Track Capacity**: All spaces hold one disc except the top triangle, which can hold unlimited.  
- **Scoring Symbols**: Only the white-bordered symbol on the drawn tile is scored this turn.  
- **Tile Placement**: Tiles must match color of space. Cannot move or rotate tiles.  
- **Covered Symbols**: When a tile is covered, its symbols no longer count.  
- **Unusable Blue Spaces**: Only become active when the "X" transport tile of same color is placed.  
- **Money Handling**: All money is public. No hidden funds.  
- **Score Track Overflow**: VP can exceed 50; wrap around and remember to add 50 in final count.  
- **Research Track Limits**: No maximum; players can exceed printed numbers.
