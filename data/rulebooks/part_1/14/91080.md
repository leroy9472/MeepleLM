# Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective

Andean Abyss simulates the complex conflict in Colombia during the 1990s–2000s involving the Government, the Marxist FARC, the right-wing AUC paramilitaries, and the drug-trafficking Cartels. Each faction pursues distinct political, military, and economic goals using asymmetric strategies.

**Victory Conditions**:
- **Government**: Total Support exceeds 60.
- **FARC**: Total Opposition plus FARC Bases exceeds 25.
- **AUC**: AUC has more Bases than FARC.
- **Cartels**: More than 10 Bases and more than 40 Resources.
- If no faction meets its condition by game end, the closest to victory wins.

## 2. Components

- Game board of Colombia with Departments, Cities, and Lines of Communication (LoCs).
- Wooden pieces: Troops (dark blue), Police (light blue), Guerrillas (red/yellow/green), Bases (red/yellow/green/blue).
- Markers: Terror, Sabotage, Control, Support/Opposition.
- 76-card deck (72 Event cards, 4 Propaganda cards).
- 6-sided dice (red, yellow, green).
- Cylinders (black/white) for tracking Resources and Aid.
- Sequence of Play aid sheet.
- 4 Faction player aid foldouts.
- 8 pawns (6 black, 6 white) for marking selected spaces.

## 3. Setup

### General Setup
- Choose Standard or Open Deployment.
- Shuffle 72 Event cards, remove 12 randomly, then split into 4 piles.
- Add 1 Propaganda card to each pile, stack to form draw deck.
- Reveal top card.

### Standard Deployment
- **Aid**: 9
- **Resources**: Govt 40; FARC, AUC, Cartels 10
- **Total Support**: 50
- **Opposition + Bases**: 20
- **El Presidente**: Samper
- **Control Markers**:
  - Active Support: Atlántico, Santander, all Cities except Cali
  - Active Opposition: Chocó, Arauca, Meta East/West, Guaviare, Putumayo, Nariño
- **Forces**:
  - Troops: 3 each in Bogotá, Medellín, Cali, Santander
  - Police: 2 in Bogotá; 1 in each other City
  - Govt Base: 1 in Santander
  - FARC: Guerrillas in 9 Depts (1–2 each), Bases in 6 Depts
  - AUC: Guerrillas in 6 spaces, Base in Antioquia
  - Cartels: Guerrillas in Cali, Putumayo; Bases in 5 spaces (2 in Putumayo)

### Open Deployment
- Set non-player forces per Standard.
- Players deploy in order: Cartels (2 Guerrillas, 6 Bases), AUC (6 Guerrillas, 1 Base), FARC (12 Guerrillas, 6 Bases), Govt (12 Troops, 12 Police, 1 Base), all in legal spaces.

## 4. Gameplay Flow

### Sequence of Play
1. **Event Card Phase**:
   - Reveal top card.
   - Factions are Eligible if they did not act on the previous card.
   - First and Second Eligible Factions may:
     - Execute an Operation (with or without Special Activity)
     - Execute the Event
     - Pass (gain Resources: +1, +3 if Govt)
   - Limited Operations (1 space, no Special Activity) allowed under certain conditions.
   - Ship a Shipment to gain a free Limited Operation.
   - Adjust Eligibility: Acting factions become Ineligible.

2. **Propaganda Card Phase** (when Propaganda card is drawn):
   - Victory Check: If any faction meets its condition, game ends.
   - Control Phase: Mark Govt and FARC Control; Sabotage LoCs under FARC influence.
   - Resources Phase: Add Resources and resolve Shipments.
   - Support Phase: Civic Action (Govt), Agitation (FARC), Election (El Presidente).
   - Redeploy Phase: Govt moves forces.
   - Reset Phase: All factions become Eligible; remove markers; flip Guerrillas Underground.

3. **Final Propaganda**: After 4th Propaganda (or 3rd if short game), determine winner by victory margin.

## 5. Core Mechanics

### Operations

#### Government Operations
- **Train** (Cost: 3 per space):
  - Place up to 6 cubes (Troops/Police) in selected Cities/Depts with Govt Base or in Cities.
  - Optionally: Replace 3 cubes with 1 Base or conduct Civic Action (requires Troops, Police, Control).
- **Patrol** (Cost: 3 total):
  - Move cubes along LoCs/Cities, stopping at Guerrillas.
  - Activate 1 Guerrilla per cube in each LoC.
  - Optional free Assault in 1 LoC.
- **Sweep** (Cost: 3 per space):
  - Move Troops into selected spaces (via adjacent LoCs if clear).
  - Activate 1 Guerrilla per cube (1 per 2 in Forest).
- **Assault** (Cost: 3 per space):
  - Remove 1 Active Guerrilla per Troop (1 per 2 in Mountain).
  - Remove 1 enemy piece per Police in City/LoC.
  - Bases removed only after Guerrillas/cubes.
  - Drug Bust: +6 Aid per Shipment removed.

#### Insurgent Operations
- **Rally** (Cost: 1 per space):
  - Place 1 Guerrilla or replace 2 Guerrillas with 1 Base.
  - If Base already present: Place Guerrillas up to Pop + Bases, or move Guerrillas there and flip all Underground.
  - FARC: Only in Neutral/Opposition.
  - AUC: Only in Neutral/Support.
- **March** (Cost: 1 per destination City/Dept, 0 for LoC):
  - Move Guerrillas to adjacent spaces.
  - Activate if destination has Support/Opposition/LoC and total forces >3.
- **Attack** (Cost: 1 per space):
  - Activate all Guerrillas.
  - Roll die: ≤ number of Guerrillas removes 2 enemy pieces (Bases last).
  - On "1": Place 1 Guerrilla.
  - Take any removed Shipment.
- **Terror** (Cost: 1 per City/Dept, 0 for LoC):
  - Activate 1 Underground Guerrilla per space.
  - City/Dept: Place Terror, shift Support/Opposition toward Neutral (FARC: toward Active Opposition).
  - LoC: Place Sabotage.
  - AUC: Aid -3 (1 space), -5 (2+ spaces).

### Special Activities
- **Accompanying Operation Required** unless granted by Event.
- Only one Special Activity per Operation.

#### Government
- **Air Lift**: Move up to 3 Troops from 1 space to another.
- **Air Strike** (with Patrol/Sweep/Assault): Remove 1 Active Guerrilla or Base (1 space).
- **Eradicate**: +4 Aid; remove all Cartel Bases in 1 Dept; shift Dept/adjacent toward Active Opposition or place FARC Guerrilla.

#### FARC
- **Extort**: In spaces with Underground FARC Guerrilla and Control, gain 1 Resource per space, Activate 1 Guerrilla.
- **Ambush** (with Attack): In 1 space, auto-succeed, remove 2 enemy, place 1 FARC Guerrilla.
- **Kidnap** (with Terror, ≤3 spaces): Roll die; gain Resources from Govt/Cartels; on "6", AUC places 1 piece.

#### AUC
- **Extort**: Same as FARC, using AUC forces.
- **Ambush**: Same as FARC.
- **Assassinate** (with Terror, ≤3 spaces): Remove 1 enemy piece per space.

#### Cartels
- **Cultivate** (with Rally/March): Move 1 Base to new space or place 1 if Rallying.
- **Process** (with Rally/March): Place 1–2 Shipments or remove Base for +3 Resources.
- **Bribe** (≤3 spaces): Spend -3 Resources to remove/flip Guerrillas, remove cubes, or remove Base.

### Key Rules
- **Stacking**: Max 2 Bases per Dept/City; Bases not on LoCs.
- **Control**: Govt if more Govt than total Insurgent pieces; FARC if more FARC than others.
- **Guerrillas**: Start Underground; flip to Active via Operations/Events.
- **Shipment**: Marker under Guerrilla; transferable; removed during Drug Profits for Base or +6 Resources.
- **Eligibility**: Acts render faction Ineligible next turn; Passing keeps Eligible.

## 6. Scoring & End Game

### Victory Conditions
- **Government**: Total Support > 60
- **FARC**: Total Opposition + FARC Bases > 25
- **AUC**: AUC Bases > FARC Bases
- **Cartels**: >10 Bases and >40 Resources

### Victory Margin (Final Game)
- Government: Total Support - 60
- FARC: Total Opposition + FARC Bases - 25
- AUC: AUC Bases - FARC Bases
- Cartels: min(Cartels Bases - 10, Cartels Resources - 40)

### Game End
- Immediate win if any faction meets condition during Propaganda.
- After final Propaganda, highest victory margin wins.
- Tiebreak: Non-players > Cartels > AUC > FARC.

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases

- **Free Operations**: Via Events or Shipment removal; do not affect Eligibility.
- **Operations with 0 Resources**: Can Extort/Kidnap/Process during Operation to gain Resources mid-execution.
- **FARC Zones**: Govt cannot enter; remove Bases, Redeploy cubes when placed.
- **Dual-Use Events**: Player chooses shaded or unshaded effect.
- **Insurgent Momentum**: Shaded effects last until next Propaganda Reset.
- **Govt Capabilities**: Permanent effects from marked Events.
- **Negotiation**: Open; only Resources and Shipments can be transferred.
- **Non-player Factions**: Follow AI priorities (e.g., AUC Marches toward FARC Base).
