## Vanuatu – Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
Vanuatu is a strategic board game set in the South Pacific archipelago, where players take on roles of traders and explorers developing islands, managing resources, and attracting tourists. The goal is to earn the most Victory Points (VP) over 8 rounds through resource management, stall construction, tourism, and efficient use of character abilities.

---

### 2. Components  
- Game board depicting a grid for tile placement  
- 18 Archipelago tiles (3 unlettered start tiles + 15 lettered A–F)  
- Fish tiles (values 1–3)  
- Treasure tiles (values 1–3)  
- Demand tiles (for exporting goods)  
- Tourist tiles (with 0, 2, or 4 tourists)  
- 4 Rest tokens (face-down bonuses)  
- Fish price marker (starts at $3)  
- Turn track (8 spaces)  
- 10 Character tiles  
- Player materials per player:  
  - 1 boat  
  - 8 stalls  
  - 5 action markers  
  - 3 player discs (for VP, Bank, Prosperity track)  
- Goods cubes: Kava ($1), Copra ($2), Beef ($3)  
- Fish discs, treasure discs, tourist pawns  

---

### 3. Setup  
**Board Setup:**  
- Randomly place the 3 unlettered Archipelago tiles face-up near the starting island.  
- Create two stacks from remaining tiles (A to F), combine into one deck, and place face-down. Reveal the top 2 tiles.  
- Set fish price marker to $3.  
- Place initial goods: 1 cube each of Kava, Copra, and Beef on the starting island.  
- Place 3 fish discs and 2 treasure discs on starting sea tiles as shown.  
- Place initial tourists: Return one tourist tile based on player count:  
  - 3 players: remove value-4 tile  
  - 4 players: remove value-2 tile  
  - 5 players: remove value-0 tile  
- Shuffle remaining tourist tiles; place face-down stack. Flip top tile to Tourism Office and place corresponding tourists on it.  
- Place 3 demand tiles face-up in the Chamber of Foreign Trade.  
- Place rest tokens face-down on "Welcome to Vanuatu" sign.  
- Lay out 10 character tiles face-up above the board.  

**Player Setup:**  
- Each player chooses a color and takes:  
  - 1 boat (placed on empty starting sea tile)  
  - 8 stalls  
  - 5 action markers  
  - 3 player discs:  
    - 1 on 0 VP (Prosperity track)  
    - 1 on $3 (Bank track)  
- First player receives first-player marker.  

---

### 4. Gameplay Flow (8 Rounds)  
Each round has 5 phases:

#### Phase I: Turn Setup (Skip Round 1)  
1. Advance turn marker.  
2. Remove previous tourist tile and tourists; draw new tourist tile and place indicated tourists.  
3. Remove filled demand tiles, shift remaining up, refill to 3 face-up.  
4. Replenish goods on any island with 0 cubes.  
5. Reset fish price to $3.  
6. First player places the two revealed Archipelago tiles:  
   - Each must touch **two** already-placed tiles.  
   - Island tiles cannot touch other islands.  
   - Sea tiles must touch at least one island.  
   - Add fish, treasure, and goods as shown.  
7. Reveal next 2 Archipelago tiles.  

#### Phase II: Character Selection  
- In turn order, each player:  
  - Draws a new character tile.  
  - Returns the one from the previous round.  
- Character ability may be used **once per round** when performing the corresponding action.  
- After use, flip character face-down.  

#### Phase III: Action Planning  
- In turn order, players place **5 action markers** in two rounds:  
  1. Each places **2 markers** (on same or different action spaces).  
  2. Each places **2 more markers**.  
  3. Each places **1 final marker**.  
- Cannot plan an action that is currently impossible (e.g., sail to unreachable tile).  
- To chain actions, must plan in execution order.  

#### Phase IV: Perform Actions  
- In turn order:  
  - Select an action space where you have **plurality (simple majority)** of markers.  
  - In case of tie, earlier player in turn order has majority.  
  - Perform the action if possible, then remove all your markers from that space.  
  - Continue until no markers remain.  
- If you have no majorities:  
  - Remove markers from **one** space without performing the action.  
- If you have majority but cannot perform:  
  - Still remove markers (no action taken).  
- Must perform possible planned actions.  

#### Phase V: Resolve Rest Tokens  
- Players who took a rest token reveal it and gain bonus:  
  - Gain first-player marker  
  - Gain $1 and 1 VP  
  - Gain $1  
  - Gain 1 VP  
- Return used rest tokens face-down to board.  

---

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Actions (Available During Phase IV)  
Players may perform actions only if they have majority on the space.  

1. **Sail**  
   - Pay $1 per space (max $3), move boat 1–3 sea spaces.  
   - *Navigator*: Sail 1–3 spaces for free.  

2. **Build Stall**  
   - Pay $3 to place stall on adjacent island (if space).  
   - *Builder*: Pay $1 instead.  

3. **Explore Treasure**  
   - Take treasure tile worth number of treasure discs on current sea tile.  
   - Remove one treasure disc.  
   - *Diver*: Gain $ equal to treasure tile value (1–3).  

4. **Fish**  
   - Take fish tile worth number of fish discs on current sea tile.  
   - Remove one fish disc.  
   - *Fisherman*: Gain VP equal to fish tile value (1–3).  

5. **Sell Fish**  
   - Sell one or more fish tiles.  
   - Revenue = sum of fish values × current fish price.  
   - Reduce fish price by $1 per sale.  
   - *Merchant*: Can sell even if not adjacent to own stall.  

6. **Buy Good**  
   - Buy 1 good (Kava $1, Copra $2, Beef $3).  
   - Place on topmost available demand ship.  
   - Score VP as shown (1–3).  
   - +2 VP if last good on ship.  
   - *Buyer*: Can export a second good of same type for free.  

7. **Draw (Turtle)**  
   - Claim turtle drawing spot on adjacent island.  
   - Score 3 VP.  
   - *Artist*: Score 5 VP instead.  

8. **Transport Tourist**  
   - Move 1 tourist to adjacent island (with space).  
   - Earn $1 per stall **on that island** (any player).  
   - *Guide*: +2 VP per completed turtle drawing **on that island**.  

9. **Rest**  
   - Take all remaining rest tokens, choose one, keep face-down until end of phase.  

#### Character Abilities (Summary)  
| Character | Action | Bonus |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Navigator | Sail | Free movement (1–3 spaces) |
| Builder | Build | Pay $1 instead of $3 |
| Diver | Explore | Gain $ = treasure value |
| Fisherman | Fish | Gain VP = fish value |
| Merchant | Sell | No stall adjacency required |
| Buyer | Buy | Free second good of same type |
| Artist | Draw | 5 VP instead of 3 |
| Guide | Transport | +2 VP per completed drawing |
| Beggar | Any time | Convert VP to $ at 1:1 (up to 3 VP per turn) |
| Preacher | Any | Perform 1 action without majority (only if no majority on any action) |

#### Money & VP Conversion  
- Any time a player reaches **$10**, immediately convert to **5 VP**.  
  - Move VP marker up 5, reset $ marker to $0.  
  - If over $10, continue converting in $10 chunks.  
- Treasure tiles can be sold **at any time** for $ equal to face value.  
- Beggar allows conversion of VP → $ at 1:1 during turn (max 3 VP).  

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
Game ends after Round 8. Final scoring in order:

1. **Fish Value**: $ equal to total value of held fish tiles.  
   - Convert $10 → 5 VP as needed.  
2. **First Player Bonus**: +3 VP.  
3. **Cash Bonus**: +1 VP per $3 (remainder kept, used for tiebreak).  
4. **Treasure**: +2 VP per point of treasure tile value.  
5. **Tourism**: +2 VP for each of your stalls × number of tourists on same island.  
   - e.g., 2 stalls + 3 tourists = 2 × 3 × 2 = 12 VP.  

**Winning**: Highest VP wins.  
**Tiebreakers**:  
1. Most stalls built.  
2. Most $ remaining.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I use character ability without performing action?**  
  No. Ability triggers only when performing the corresponding action.  

- **Can I perform multiple actions in one turn?**  
  Yes, if you have majority on multiple action spaces, you may perform them in turn order.  

- **Can I sell fish at $0 price?**  
  Yes, but revenue will be zero. Fish price can drop to $0.  

- **Can I build a stall on an island with no space?**  
  No. Only one stall per player per island.  

- **What if I can't perform an action I have majority on?**  
  You must still remove your markers. No action is taken.  

- **Can I transport a tourist to a full island?**  
  No. Island must have space (unfilled tourist capacity).  

- **Do I have to use Beggar conversion?**  
  No. Optional, but limited to 3 VP per turn.  

- **Can Preacher use an action with neutral markers (2-player variant)?**  
  Yes, if no majority on any action, Preacher can perform one action with just 1 marker, even on guarded actions.
