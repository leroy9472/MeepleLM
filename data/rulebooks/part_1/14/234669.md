## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
Legacy of Dragonholt is a narrative-driven cooperative adventure game set in a fantasy world. Players take on the roles of heroes completing quests, developing skills, and influencing the fate of villages and regions. The core objective is to progress through story entries by advancing time and completing challenges, earning experience, fame, and skills to grow stronger and shape the story.

Progress is tracked across multiple categories such as Heroism, Academic Study, Combat Training, and others. Reaching certain thresholds unlocks rewards like experience points, skill acquisition, and increased fame. The story unfolds based on time passed and progress made, with key story entries triggered at specific points.

---

### 2. Components  
- Hero tracking sheets (for stamina, skills, experience, fame, gold)  
- Story tracking sheets (for time, progress, entries read)  
- List of skills grouped by category  
- Reference to story entries (e.g., entry 3877, 9330, etc.)  
- Progress trackers (Progress, Heroism, Peace, War, etc.)  
- Time trackers (per day or location)  

---

### 3. Setup  
1. **Create Heroes**:  
   - Choose 5–8 skills.  
     - Two skills must be from your race’s list.  
     - Two skills must be from your class’s list.  
   - Base Stamina is 14.  
     - Subtract 2 Stamina for each skill beyond 5.  
     - If playing with 1 hero: +4 Stamina.  
     - If playing with 2 heroes: +2 Stamina.  
   - Record chosen skills and adjusted Stamina.

2. **Prepare Tracking Sheets**:  
   - Use the appropriate tracking sheet for the current location (e.g., Dragonholt Village).  
   - Reset Time, Progress, and Entries Read trackers.  
   - Note starting FAME and GOLD (if applicable).

3. **Begin Story**:  
   - Start reading from the beginning of the current story section (e.g., Dragonholt Village).  
   - Track each story entry read.

---

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
1. **Advance Time**:  
   - As the story progresses, mark time passing (e.g., "When eight time has passed in day one...").  
   - When time thresholds are met, read the corresponding story entry (e.g., entry 3877).

2. **Mark Progress**:  
   - Certain actions or events allow players to mark progress in categories:  
     - Heroism  
     - Academic Study  
     - Combat Training  
     - Physical Training  
     - Social Practice  
     - Spiritual Meditation  
     - Peace / War (context-dependent)  

3. **Trigger Rewards**:  
   - When progress thresholds are reached, apply rewards (experience, fame, stamina, skill access).

4. **Learn Skills**:  
   - Once the required progress is marked in a category, players may spend 1 experience to learn any skill in that category at any time.

5. **Resolve Story Beats**:  
   - Follow narrative prompts, make choices, and continue reading entries as time and progress dictate.

6. **Repeat** until the current arc concludes or a major event (e.g., masquerade ball) is triggered.

---

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Progress & Rewards  
- **Heroism**  
  - At 8 progress: +1 XP, FAME +1  
  - At 16 progress: +1 XP, MAX STAMINA +2, FAME +1  
  - At 24 progress: +1 XP, FAME +1  

- **Academic Study (6 progress marked)**  
  - Unlock skills: Alchemy, Arcana, History, Reasoning, Runes, Survival  
  - Cost: 1 XP per skill  

- **Combat Training (6 progress marked)**  
  - Unlock skills: Archery, Brawling, Dueling, Military  
  - Cost: 1 XP per skill  

- **Physical Training (6 progress marked)**  
  - Unlock skills: Agility, Athletics, Endurance, Stealth  
  - Cost: 1 XP per skill  

- **Social Practice (6 progress marked)**  
  - Unlock skills: Deception, Empathy, Performance, Persuasion, Streetwise  
  - Cost: 1 XP per skill  

- **Spiritual Meditation (5 progress marked)**  
  - Unlock skills: Awareness, Devotion, Willpower  
  - Cost: 1 XP per skill  

> Note: Skill learning can occur at any time once prerequisites are met.

#### Time Tracking  
- Time advances narratively based on in-game events.  
- Key story entries are triggered when specific time thresholds are met in each day/location:  
  - **Day One**: 8 time → read entry 3877  
  - **Day Two**: 8 time → read entry 9330  
  - **Day Three**: 6 time → read entry 5352  
  - **Day Four**: 8 time → read entry 1501  
  - **Day Five**: 8 time → read entry 5035  
  - **Day Six**: 8 time → read entry 4093  
  - **Day Seven**: 6 time → read entry 6882 (masquerade ball)

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
- There is no traditional "end game" or final score.  
- Success is measured by narrative progression, character development, and reaching key story milestones (e.g., the masquerade ball on Day Seven).  
- Character growth via experience, skills, stamina, and fame is the primary metric of advancement.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
**Q: Can I learn multiple skills at once?**  
A: Yes, as long as you have enough XP and the progress requirement is met for the category.

**Q: Do progress markers reset?**  
A: Not specified in the source. Assume progress is cumulative and does not reset unless stated by a story event.

**Q: What happens when both Peace and War progress are tracked?**  
A: Context-dependent (e.g., in "Paper Faces"). The story likely resolves based on which reaches a threshold first or comparative values. Specific rules not provided.

**Q: Can I regain spent experience?**  
A: No rule allows refunding or regaining spent XP. Manage XP carefully.

**Q: Is there a maximum fame or XP?**  
A: Not specified. No cap indicated.

**Q: What triggers time advancement?**  
A: Narrative events or player actions as directed by the story text. The GM or group decides when to mark time based on story flow.
