## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
In *New Frontiers*, players build space empires by exploring unknown worlds, settling colonies, developing technologies, producing and trading goods, and achieving strategic goals. The objective is to score the most Victory Points (VP) by the time the game ends.

The game ends when any of the following conditions are met:  
- A player’s developments cover more than 10 spaces on their empire mat.  
- A player has more than 7 colonies (including their home colony).  
- Fewer than 5 colonists remain in the supply after a Settle action.  
- The initial VP chip supply is exhausted.  

When any condition is triggered, finish the current round, then proceed to final scoring.

### 2. Components  
- **Empire Mats (1 per player)**: Each has a home colony and spaces for developments and colonies.  
- **Development Mats** (Large and Small): Central boards holding available developments.  
  - 8 Large Developments (square tiles)  
  - 48 Small Development tiles (double-sided, grouped into 16 types)  
- **Action Tiles (7)**:  
  - Retreat Into Isolation  
  - Develop  
  - Explore  
  - Settle  
  - Produce  
  - Trade, Consume  
  - Send Diplomatic Envoys  
  - (Optional) Chart Galactic Goals (replaces Retreat Into Isolation)  
- **World Tiles (60 round tiles)**: Represent planets to explore and settle.  
- **Goal Tiles (8)**: Used with Galactic Goals variant.  
- **Stockpile Mats (1 per player)**: Track stockpiled goods, credits, or exploration.  
- **VP Chips**: 1-, 5-, and 10-point denominations.  
- **Credit Chips (white)**: Unlimited supply.  
- **Goods Cubes**: 4 types:  
  - Novelty (cyan)  
  - Rare Elements (brown)  
  - Genes (green)  
  - Alien Technology (yellow)  
- **Colonists**: 12 per player; placed on mat and colonies.  
- **Player Disks (2 per player)**: Track priority and player identity.  
- **Priority Track**: Determines turn order.  
- **Colonist Supply Mat**: Holds colonist pool.  
- **Expansion Components (Starry Rift - SR)**:  
  - Xeno worlds (with purple defense value)  
  - Penalty chips (negative VP)  
  - Special development and world tiles with blue dot  

### 3. Setup  
#### General Setup (2–5 players):  
1. **Development Mats**:  
   - Place 8 Large Developments on the Large Development Mat.  
   - For Small Developments:  
     - 2 players: 1 tile per type  
     - 3–4 players: 2 tiles per type  
     - 5 players: 3 tiles per type  
   - Randomize visible side: flip a coin for each stack; white square or non-square side up. Return unused tiles to box.  

2. **Action Tiles**:  
   - Lay out 7 Action Tiles in a row.  
   - Use **Retreat Into Isolation** side for base game.  
   - Use **Chart Galactic Goals** side for Galactic Goals (GG) variant.  

3. **Goal Tiles (GG only)**:  
   - Shuffle 8 Goal Tiles and place face down under Chart Galactic Goals tile.  
   - Give each player a Stockpile Mat.  

4. **VP Supply**:  
   - 12 VP per player (using 1- and 5-point chips).  
   - Set aside 10-point chips as reserve.  

5. **Colonist Supply**:  
   - 12 colonists per player.  
   - Place 5 colonists in the top section of the colonist mat as a visual reminder for endgame trigger.  

6. **Supply Pools**:  
   - Form common supply of credits and goods.  

7. **World Tiles**:  
   - Place all 60 in bag and shuffle thoroughly.  
   - (SR) Use only Galactic Trendsetters with blue dot.  

8. **Player Setup**:  
   - Randomly determine turn order.  
   - Place one player disk per player on the Priority Track, leaving space 1 empty.  
   - 2-player game: Each player adds a second disk behind their first, repeating order.  
   - Each player:  
     - Takes 1 empire mat (randomly dealt), chooses side to play.  
     - Places 1 colonist from supply on home colony.  
     - If home colony is Windfall (colored halo), place matching good on it.  
   - Starting player:  
     - Receives 3 credits.  
     - Last player: +1 credit.  
     - 4–5 players: Penultimate player also +1 credit.  

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
Each round proceeds in the following steps:  

1. **Active Player Selection**:  
   - Leftmost player on the Priority Track slides their disk **½ space left** to indicate they are active.  

2. **Action Selection**:  
   - Active player selects one unused Action Tile (not yet slid down).  
   - Slides it down to lock it for the round.  

3. **Action Execution**:  
   - Active player receives the **bonus**.  
   - All players (including active) perform the **action**, in priority track order.  
   - Active player **does not** get bonus again when their turn comes in execution order.  

4. **Priority Adjustment**:  
   - Active player slides their disk the remaining ½ space to occupy the **first space** on the Priority Track.  
   - All other disks shift right to fill gaps.  

5. **Next Player**:  
   - The next leftmost player (now with disk at edge) becomes active.  
   - Repeat steps 1–4 until all players have selected an action.  
   - 2-player game: Each player selects 2 actions per round.  

6. **End of Round**:  
   - If **Produce** was not selected, add **1 credit** to its tile.  
   - Slide all Action Tiles back up.  
   - Slide all Priority Disks **one space right** on the track (resetting for next round).  
   - Begin next round.  

**Turn Order Changes**: Only **Send Diplomatic Envoys** and **Chart Galactic Goals** alter priority order during play.

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Action Tile Details  

**Retreat Into Isolation**  
- Bonus: Gain 2 credits.  
- No action for other players.  

**Develop**  
- Action: Buy 1 Development.  
- Bonus: -1 cost (minimum 0).  
- Cannot buy duplicate of existing development.  
- Developments placed on empire mat; arrangement may be changed freely.  
- 9-cost developments occupy 2 spaces.  
- Red spaces may not be covered until all 10 blue spaces are (or would be) covered.  

**Explore**  
- Active player draws 7 world tiles (SR: 8), places face down or gray-side-up.  
- Each player (starting with active) picks one.  
- Active player picks a second after all others have picked one.  
- Return unselected tiles to bag.  
- Explored worlds placed on right side of empire mat (gray side up).  
- (SR) Xeno world may be returned to bag immediately if selected.  
- Max 9 explored + settled worlds per player. Excess must be returned.  
- Powers (e.g. Survey Team) trigger after selection, before return.  

**Settle**  
- Bonus: Gain 1 colonist.  
- Action: Either  
  - Gain 2 colonists, or  
  - Settle 1 explored world (flip to colony side).  
- Must have:  
  - Required colonists (1 or 2),  
  - Pay credit cost (non-military), or  
  - Match/exceed military strength (military worlds).  
- Cannot mix credit and military.  
- (SR) Xeno worlds require matching/exceeding **purple** xenos strength.  
- After settling:  
  - Flip tile to colony side.  
  - Place colonists.  
  - Windfall: place matching good.  
- **Terraforming Engineers**: Replace non-military colony with same-color colony costing 0–3 more. Not a Settle action. May require additional colonists.  
- **Doomed Colony**: If new world requires more colonists, must supply them. Excess returned to mat.  

**Produce**  
- Action: Produce 1 good on each production colony without a good.  
- Bonus: Collect all credits on Produce tile; produce on one Windfall colony without a good.  
- Grey colonies do not produce.  
- Cannot hold more than 1 good per colony.  
- If not selected: +1 credit on tile at round end.  

**Trade, Consume**  
- Bonus: Gain 1 VP.  
- **Trade**: Sell 1 good for:  
  - Blue (Novelty): 1 credit  
  - Brown (Rare Elements): 2  
  - Green (Genes): 3  
  - Yellow (Alien Tech): 4  
- Trade Powers increase value.  
- **Consume**: Must use each Consume power once (player’s choice of order) until goods or powers are exhausted.  
  - Must return exact number unless "up to" is stated.  
  - Cannot partially use a power.  
  - Prosperous Economy: gains 1 VP per good consumed (not a consume power itself).  
- If VP supply runs out: use 10-point reserve; game ends after round.  

**Send Diplomatic Envoys**  
- Bonus: Gain 1 VP and move to first position on Priority Track (others shift back).  
- No action for others.  

**Chart Galactic Goals (GG only)**  
- Bonus:  
  - +1 position on Priority Track  
  - Gain 1 credit  
  - Draw 3 goals, keep 1 face down, return 2 to bottom of stack.  
  - (SR: draw 5, return 4)  
- Flip over one previously face-down goal (any player).  
- No action for others.  
- After selection, any player with face-down stockpile goal may reveal it to enable stockpiling.  
- Stockpile goals: Goods, Credits, Exploration – may stockpile during Explore or Settle only.  

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
When end condition is met, **complete the round**, then score:  

1. **Remove all explored worlds** (do not score).  
2. **Owner of Alien Oort Cloud Factory chooses its kind** (for scoring and powers).  
3. **Flip all face-down goals face up**.  
4. **Score the following**:  
   - VP chips in possession  
   - VP values in hexagons on developments and **settled colonies** (including home colony)  
   - "?" bonuses on 9-cost developments and colonies (conditional VPs)  
   - All **Galactic Goals in play** (each player scores all goals, not just their own)  
   - (SR) Penalty chips: -1 VP each for each explored Xeno world not settled  

**Tiebreaker**:  
- Most credits and goods **in empire** (not on stockpile mats).  
- If still tied, players share victory.  

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Credits**: Unlimited.  
- **Settle when colonists run out**: Gain as many as available. Not required to settle.  
- **Trading with players**: Not allowed.  
- **Windfall Colony**: Has colored halo; immediately receives matching good when settled. Not necessarily a production world.  
- **Alien Oort Cloud Factory**:  
  - Owner selects kind each action.  
  - Affects its own powers and scoring.  
  - Good on it cannot be traded.  
- **Produce with insufficient goods**: Perform in player order; later players may get fewer.  
- **Consume with no goods**: Cannot use Consume powers.  
- **Terraforming Engineers**:  
  - "Kind" includes grey worlds.  
  - Cannot set Alien Oort Factory to grey.  
  - Return replaced world and its good to supply.  
- **Stockpile Goals**:  
  - May stockpile only during Explore (1 world) or Settle (1 world).  
  - Goods/credits/worlds may run out; resolve in player order.  
- **Xeno Worlds (SR)**:  
  - Count as keyword for Fiction Tolerance goal.  
  - Cannot be stockpiled.
