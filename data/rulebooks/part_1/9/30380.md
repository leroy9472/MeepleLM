## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective
In 1950s Cuba, village leaders strive for wealth and influence amid political turmoil. Players represent village heads aiming to maximize prosperity through trade, construction, and legislative influence.

**Objective:** Have the most Victory Points (VPs) at the end of 6 rounds.  
VPs are earned by:
- Shipping merchandise (products/goods) on trade ships
- Erecting and using buildings
- Complying with tax, duty, and subsidy laws
- End-game scoring for buildings

**Tiebreaker:** The player with the most money wins.

---

### 2. Components
- **Game Board:** Central board with ship docks, statute spaces, VP track, and action spaces.
- **Player Sets (5 colors):**
  - 5 Character Cards (Worker, Tradeswoman, Architect, Foreman, Mayor)
  - 5 Workers (mi bois)
  - 1 Plantation Board (12-field grid with fixed layout per side)
  - 1 Score Marker
- **Buildings:**
  - 25 Building Tiles (e.g., Distillery, Bank, Church)
- **Merchandise:**
  - 54 Product Barrels: 18 Sugar, 18 Tobacco, 18 Citrus
  - 30 Goods: 15 Rum, 15 Cigars
- **Resources:**
  - 45 Resource Cubes: 15 Wood, 15 Stone, 15 Water
- **Currency:**
  - 60 Peso Coins: 45×1, 25×3, 15×5
- **Statutes:**
  - 24 Statute Cards (6 each for Tax, Duty, Subsidy, Other)
- **Ships:**
  - 15 Ship Cards (varying VP values and cargo requirements)
- **Special Tiles:**
  - 1 Start Player Card
  - 1 Vote Tile (Town Hall)
  - 1 Veto Tile (Church)
  - 6 Black Alternate Action Discs

---

### 3. Setup
**General Setup:**
- Place game board centrally.
- Place 25 building tiles face-up near the board in any arrangement.
- Shuffle and place 4 statute decks (Tax, Duty, Subsidy, Other) on their board spaces.
- Shuffle ship deck; place:
  - 1 ship at sea (edge of board)
  - 2 ships face-up on upper docks (1VP and 2VP docks)
  - 3rd dock left empty
- Place 6 black discs: 2 at Market, 2 at Architect, 2 at Mayor action spaces.

**Player Setup (per player):**
- Choose a color and take:
  - Plantation board (decide collectively whether to use identical or unique sides)
  - 5 character cards
  - 5 workers
  - 1 score marker
- Place score marker on Lighthouse (start of VP track).
- Place worker in own Warehouse.
- Receive 10 pesos (publicly).
- Take any 2 resource cubes and any 2 product barrels (place in Lot).
- Choose starting player (give Start Player Card).

**Market Setup:**
- Place at Market:
  - 3 Tobacco, 3 Sugar, 3 Citrus (priced at 6, 5, 4 respectively)
  - 2 Rum, 2 Cigars (priced at 6, 5 respectively)

### 4. Gameplay Flow
The game lasts **6 rounds**. Each round has **5 phases**:

#### A) New Bills Phase
- Reveal the top card of each of the 4 statute decks.
- These 4 cards are now proposed bills (not yet laws).

#### B) Action Phase
- Starting with current start player, players take turns:
  - Play 1 character card face-up
  - Perform its action
- Continue until each player has played **4 cards**.
- Save the 5th card for Parliament Phase.
- After 4th cards are played:
  - New start player is determined: player whose 4th card has highest vote value.
  - Tie: the player who played their 4th card later wins initiative.

**Alternate Actions:**  
Tradeswoman, Architect, and Mayor have alternate actions. When used, cover the matching board space with a black disc — only one player may use each alternate per round.

#### C) Parliament Phase
- All players reveal their 5th (saved) character card — vote value = number of votes.
- **Town Hall Bonus:** If player owns and activated Town Hall with Foreman this turn, gain +2 votes.
- **Vote Buying:** Players may buy additional votes for 1 peso each.
  - All players simultaneously reveal hidden pesos.
  - All money paid goes to bank.
  - **Exception:** If Corruption Act (IV) is in force, no vote buying allowed.
- Player with most total votes wins the bid.
  - If tie: re-bid once; if still tied, player closest to start player (clockwise) wins.
- Winner **must** select exactly **2** of the 4 proposed bills to become laws.
- Selected laws are placed on board (I–IV); unused bills are discarded.

#### D) Statute Phase
Enact all 4 current laws in order: I → II → III → IV.

**I. Tax Act**  
- Pay specified amount (pesos) to gain **2VP**.
- May only pay once per round.
- One variant: pay 1 peso per building to gain 2VP.

**II. Duty Act**  
- Pay specified item(s) to gain **2VP**.
- Examples: 1 citrus, 1 tobacco, 1 water, 2 resources, 2 products, etc.
- May only pay once per round.
- **Bonus:** If paid both Tax and Duty, gain **+1VP** (total 5VP).

**III. Subsidy Act**  
- Gain **1VP** per:
  - Building
  - Empty resource field
  - Empty product field
  - Water cube (max 7VP)
  - Vote on 5th character card (not Town Hall votes or bought votes)
  - 3 pesos (max 7VP)
  - Rum or cigar (in some versions)

**IV. Other Acts**  
- **Market Acts:** +2 or -2 of each product at market.
- **Drought Act:** Worker can activate only 1 product field (water can be spent to activate more).
- **Building Act:** Architect must pay +2 pesos to erect building.
- **Harbor Act:** Fully loaded ships leave immediately; others shift down.
- **Corruption Act:** No vote buying in Parliament.
- **Export Act:** Ship deliveries worth 2VP each.
- **Control Act:** Alternate actions cannot be used.

#### E) Round End Phase
- **Reset Alternate Actions:** Remove all black discs.
- **Remove Vote Tile:** If Town Hall was used, remove Vote Tile.
- **Spoilage:** Return all un-stored **products** (tobacco, sugar, citrus) from Lot to stock.  
  Resources and goods may remain in Lot.
- **Ships:**
  - Fully loaded ships leave harbor (cards go to bottom of deck).
  - Ship in 3rd dock leaves (even if not full).
  - Remaining ships move down one dock.
  - Fill empty docks from deck; place new "ship at sea".
- **Church Veto:** If Church was used to veto, place Veto Tile on that statute deck.  
  Remove Veto Tile next turn if no veto used.

### 5. Core Mechanics

#### Plantation Board
- 12 fields: Warehouse, Lake, 2 Mountains, 2 Forests, 2 Tobacco, 2 Sugar, 2 Citrus.
- Worker moves to any field; activates entire row and column.
- Max **2 product fields** activated unless water is spent (1 water = 1 extra product field).
- Buildings block field production.

#### Worker
- Move to any field.
- Activate row and column.
- Collect resources and max 2 products (choose which).
- Water can be spent to activate additional product fields.
- Affected by Drought Act (limits to 1 product field unless water spent).

#### Tradeswoman
- Buy/sell merchandise (products/goods) at market.
  - Buy: Pay price shown; take from market or stock (7 pesos if market empty).
  - Sell: Place on market (earn price) or to bank (1 peso for product, 3 for good).
- **Alternate Action:** Take 1 free item:
  - 1 resource OR
  - 1 product with lowest market price (tie: choose)
  - Place black disc on Market space.

#### Architect
- Choose any available building, pay cost (resources), place on any non-building field.
- **Alternate Action:** Earn 2VP (first user) or 1VP (second user). Place black disc.

#### Foreman
- Two options:
  1. Activate **all buildings** in same row/column as worker
  2. Activate **any one building** (regardless of worker position)
- **Warehouse:** Use to store all products from Lot (prevents spoilage).

#### Mayor
- Supply **one ship** with any amount of merchandise.
  - Each item earns VP based on ship’s dock: 1VP (top), 2VP (middle), 3VP (bottom).
  - Ship must have matching cargo slots.
- **Alternate Action:** Earn 4 pesos (first) or 2 pesos (second). Place black disc.

#### Church
- During Foreman action, may **veto one proposed bill**.
- Cannot veto same statute type (I–IV) two turns in a row.
- Discard bill; place Veto Tile on that deck.
- Veto Tile removed next turn if no veto used.

#### Buildings (Summary)
| Type | Function |
|------|---------|
| **Victory Points** | Convert resources/products to VPs (e.g., Cement Factory: 1 stone → 1VP) |
| **Factories** | Convert products → goods (e.g., Distillery: 1 sugar → 1 rum) |
| **Branch Offices** | Supply ships (Small: 1 item, Large: up to 2 of same) |
| **Income** | Earn pesos (e.g., Bank: +2 or +4 pesos) |
| **Power** | Town Hall (+2 votes), Church (veto), Lighthouse (swap ships) |
| **Storage** | Warehouse (store products to avoid spoilage) |
| **Black Market** | Convert 1 merchandise ↔ 1 other merchandise |

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game
**Game ends after Round 6.**

**Final Scoring:**
- **+2VP per building** owned.
- All other VPs from shipping, statutes, and building use already scored.

**Winner:** Player with most total VPs.  
**Tiebreaker:** Most money.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Stock Exhaustion:** If an item is unavailable in general stock, player cannot obtain it.
- **Trading:** No player-to-player trading allowed.
- **Building Overlap:** Cannot build over existing buildings or Warehouse.
- **Worker on Building:** Allowed. Only unobstructed fields produce.
- **Alternate Actions:** Only two players total can use alternate actions for Architect/Mayor; Tradeswoman alternate is first-come, first-served.
- **Church Veto:** May only be used during Foreman action; cannot veto same type twice consecutively.
- **Lighthouse:** Swap "ship at sea" with any ship in deck; rest of deck order preserved.
- **Spoilage:** Only **products** spoil; resources and goods remain in Lot.
- **Harbor Act:** Fully loaded ships leave **immediately when loaded**, not during Statute Phase.
- **Vote Buying Ties:** Re-bid once; if still tied, player closest to start player wins.
- **New Start Player:** Determined before Parliament Phase, affects turn order for voting.
