## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective
In *The Networks*, players take on the role of television network executives competing to build the most successful lineup of shows over five seasons. The objective is to amass the most Viewers by strategically developing shows, signing stars, landing ads, and leveraging network cards. Success is measured in Viewers, with money serving as a secondary resource and tiebreaker.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
- The game spans **5 Seasons**.
- Each Season, players take turns in order, performing **one action per turn**.
- Actions include: Develop Show, Sign Star, Land Ad, Take Network Card, Attach Star or Ad, Drop and Budget.
- A player may **Drop and Budget** to end their participation in the Season.
- When all players have dropped, proceed to **End of Season**.
- After Season 5, perform final scoring.

### 5. Core Mechanics

#### Player Actions
1. **Develop Show**:
   - Pay Development Cost and meet prerequisites (icons on Show Card).
   - Take a face-up Show from the Scoring Track.
   - Cancel a show in a time slot: move to Rerun area (rotated 180¬∞), discard attached Stars/Ads (starter cards to box).
   - Place new Show in vacant slot. Attach required Stars/Ads from Green Room.
   - Place Scoring Cube on top Viewer row (always, regardless of season).
   - If Show is in preferred time slot and in first season, score higher Viewer value.
   - May cancel a Show in the same season it was developed, but only by replacing it.

2. **Sign Star**:
   - Take a face-up Star from Scoring Track.
   - Pay Signing Cost.
   - Place in Green Room (not on a Show).
   - No replacement card drawn.

3. **Land Ad**:
   - Take a face-up Ad from Scoring Track.
   - Collect Landing Bonus (immediate income).
   - Place in Green Room.
   - No replacement card drawn.
   - **Promos**: Ads that give Viewers; require payment when attached or rotate to gray side.

4. **Take Network Card**:
   - Take a face-up Network Card.
   - No cost.
   - 4 Types:
     - **Instant Use**: Use power, discard to bottom.
     - **One-Time Use**: Keep, use once during game, then discard.
     - **Endgame Only**: Keep; scores at end of game.
     - **Advanced Only (lasting effect)**: Keep; may discard once for bonus, losing effect.
   - Cards in Green Room do not affect income/Viewers until used or scored.
   - If a Network Card contradicts a rule, it takes precedence.

5. **Attach Star or Ad**:
   - Attach one Star or Ad from Green Room to a Show.
   - If Show has space (per prerequisites), slide under.
   - If at capacity, discard one Star/Ad to make room.
   - Evaluate conditions at time of attachment; do not rotate later.

6. **Drop and Budget**:
   - Move Turn Order Disc to leftmost available space on Drop and Budget track.
   - Collect reward from that space:
     - Season 1: money only.
     - Seasons 2‚Äì5: choose money or Viewers.
   - End your Season (no more actions).
   - When all players drop, proceed to End of Season.

#### Burning Actions (1‚Äì2 Players Only)
- Occurs at specific points (every 3 turns in 2p; every turn in 1p unless Drop and Budget).
- Reveal top Network Card; follow Burn Icons on bottom:
  - Burn leftmost Show, Star, Ad, or Network Card (to bottom of deck).
  - Burn leftmost Drop and Budget space (block with cube).
- Discard Network Card to bottom after burn.
- In 1p, failing a burn places a cube on Turn Order track; 5 cubes = immediate loss.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
**End of Each Season (except after Season 5 final scoring)**:
1. **Income / Expenses**:
   - Income: from attached Ads.
   - Expenses: upkeep from Shows and Stars in Lineup (not Reruns/Archives).
   - Net positive: collect money.
   - Net negative: pay from money first; if insufficient, pay remainder in Viewers (cannot go below 0).

2. **Score Viewers from Lineup and Reruns**:
   - Lineup: Score current Viewer row of each Show.
     - First-season Show in preferred slot: higher value.
     - Otherwise: lower value.
   - Add Viewers from attached Stars and Promo Ads.
   - Add Rerun value (‚í∏) of shows in Rerun area.
   - Do not count unattached Stars/Promos or Archives.
   - Update score on Scoring Tracks; claim 100/200/300 tokens as appropriate.

3. **Age Shows**:
   - Move Scoring Cube down one Viewer row on each Show.
   - If at last row, remains there.
   - Done after Season 5 as well (for final scoring).

4. **Set Up Next Season** (skip after Season 5):
   - Move Reruns to Archives (genres still count for bonuses).
   - Discard all unused face-up cards to bottoms of decks.
   - Draw new face-up cards (number per Right Scoring Track) from correct Season decks.
   - **New Turn Order**: Reverse order of Viewer totals (lowest first). Tie: earlier Drop and Budget goes first.
   - **Flip Right Scoring Track**: Only after Season 1.
   - Advance Season Marker.

**Final Scoring (after Season 5)**:
1. Score Lineup one final time (after aging).
2. **Do not** collect income, pay expenses, or score Reruns.
3. Score 1 Viewer per Star in Green Room.
4. Score endgame Network Cards.
5. **Winning**: Most Viewers wins. Tie: most money. Remaining ties stand.
6. **1-Player Win Condition**: Reach 265 Viewers by end of game.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Can I hold a Show without putting it in my Lineup?** No. Shows must be placed immediately when developed.
- **Can I cancel a Show without replacing it?** No. Canceling only occurs when replacing with a new Show.
- **When do I evaluate Star/Ad conditions?** Only when attaching to a Show. Changes later do not affect card side.
- **Can I attach a Star/Ad to a Rerun?** No. Only active Lineup shows can have Stars/Ads.
- **Do Genre Bonuses trigger multiple times?** Yes. You score when reaching 3rd and 5th Show of a genre.
- **Can I convert money to Viewers anytime?** Only when scoring the 5th Show of a genre (4M = 3 Viewers).
- **What happens if no face-up cards are available for an action?** You cannot perform that action until next Season.
- **Can Network Cards be used out of turn?** Only if the card specifies (e.g., reaction, end of turn).
- **In 1-player, what if I can‚Äôt perform a Burn?** Place a cube on Turn Order track. Five cubes = lose.
- **In 2-player, what happens when a player Drops?** Block a Turn Order space; remaining player gets only 2 turns between Burns.
