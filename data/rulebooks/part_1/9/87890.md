### 1. Lore & Objective
In *Pret-√†-Porter*, players are fashion company owners competing to create the most prestigious and profitable clothing collections over a year (12 rounds). The goal is to succeed in the competitive world of fashion by designing collections, preparing for fashion shows, earning prestige, and selling designs for maximum profit. Victory is determined by combining total cash and victory points, with the highest score winning.

### 3. Setup
1. Place the board in the center.  
2. **Contracts, Buildings, Employees**:  
   - Remove cards with ‚ÄúLast Quarter‚Äù (X, XI, XII) backs.  
   - Shuffle remaining cards and place decks face-up (undeveloped side up).  
   - Deal 3 cards from the bottom of each deck to their board locations.  
   - Cover deck tops with the removed ‚ÄúLast Quarter‚Äù cards (face-down).  
3. **Designs**:  
   - Shuffle deck, deal 2 starting Design cards per player (different styles, no T, Q, or * icons).  
   - Reveal 4 face-up Design cards on the board.  
4. **Player Setup**:  
   - Each player gets $40, 3 Action pawns, 2 Upkeep markers, 1 VP marker (on 0).  
   - Randomly draw 1 Clothing Type token; take matching starting Design cards; place token on player board.  
   - Place Upkeep marker on -3 (cost of 3 unskilled workers).  
5. **Fashion Shows**:  
   - Shuffle non-final Fashion Show tiles and place 1, 2, 3 in months III, VI, IX.  
   - Place final quarter tiles (X, XI, XII backs) face-up in month XII.  
6. **Last Minute Preparations**: Place 2 Q, 1 PR, 1 T, and $5 on location 9.  
7. **Order**: Randomly determine first player.  
8. Place Calendar marker on "I".

### 4. Gameplay Flow
The game spans 12 rounds, grouped into 4 quarters. Each quarter:  
- **Rounds I & II, IV & V, VII & VIII, X & XI**: Working Rounds (Preparation)  
- **Rounds III, VI, IX, XII**: Exhibition Rounds (Fashion Shows)  

#### Working Round Phases:
1. **Planning Phase**: Players place 3 Action pawns on board locations in turn order.  
2. **Action Phase**: Resolve locations 1‚Äì9 in order. Resolve card effects (no pawn required) first, then pawn actions.  
3. **Growth Phase**: In turn order, each player may train 1 Employee and/or upgrade 1 Building. Pay cost, flip card, adjust upkeep.  
4. **Upkeep Phase**: Pay monthly upkeep. If unable, take $20 Loan(s). Discard unused board cards. Advance calendar.

#### Exhibition Round Phases:
1. **Prestige Exchange Phase**: Exchange prestige from *previous* show into VP (1:1, 2:1, 3:1 in 2/3/4p).  
2. **Show Phase**: Each player chooses 1 Collection (same Style, finished designs). Place materials on designs. Gain T tokens for matching Clothing Type.  
3. **Award Phase**: Compare Collections across cities. Award prestige based on Q, T, PR, Quantity.  
4. **Sales Phase**:  
   - **Prestige Effect**: Gain $ = (Collection size) √ó (Prestige earned this round).  
   - **Collection Sale**: Gain $ from design card values. Discard designs and used materials.  
5. **Upkeep Phase**:  
   - Pay upkeep.  
   - Repay all Credits and Loans in full. If unable, take new Loan (get $18 instead of $20).  
   - Flip Contracts (extend or discard).  
   - Advance calendar.

### 5. Core Mechanics
- **Collections**: Group of finished designs (same Style). Must have both required materials.  
- **Finishing Designs**: Place both required material types on a design card.  
- **Action Resolution Order**:  
  1. Bank  
  2. Contracts  
  3. Buildings  
  4. Employees  
  5. Designs  
  6. Local Manufacturer  
  7. Warehouse  
  8. Import  
  9. Last Minute Preparations (reverse order)  
- **Pawn Limits**: 2 per location (2‚Äì3p), 3 per location (4p), unlimited for Bank.

  - **Exclamation (‚ùó)**: Immediate, one-time effect.

- **Credit**:  
  - Max = $10 √ó largest potential collection size.  
  - Interest = 10% of credit value (added to upkeep).  
  - Must repay in full at next Exhibition Upkeep.  
- **Loan**:  
  - Forced if cannot pay upkeep.  
  - $20 received, +$2 upkeep (10% interest).  
  - Repay $28 at next Exhibition Upkeep.  
- **Penalty**: Failure to repay credit/loan in Exhibition Upkeep results in -$10 golden bill (end-game penalty).  
- **Final Quarter (Rounds X‚ÄìXII)**:  
  - Replace Contracts, Buildings, Employees with final quarter decks.  
  - Final cards cannot be upgraded or extended.

  - 2 players tie for 1st ‚Üí both treated as 2nd.  
  - 3+ tie for 1st ‚Üí no award.  
  - 2+ tie for 2nd ‚Üí no award.  

### 6. Scoring & End Game
- After Round XII:  
  1. Conduct Prestige Exchange Phase for prestige earned in XII.  
  2. If any player has unpaid loans/credits they cannot repay, they are bankrupt (score = 0).  
  3. Final score = Total Cash + Victory Points.  
- **Tiebreaker**: Player with more VP on track wins.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Design Bonuses**: * = +$2 profit; Q or T = gain token when in collection.  
- **Clothing Type Match**: Gain 1 T token per matching design in collection (one-time, not per round).  
- **Permanent vs. Temporary Tokens**: Only tokens from buildings/employees/contracts are permanent. Others discarded after show.  
- **Card Limits**: Only 1 Employee trained and 1 Building upgraded per Growth Phase.  
- **Severance**: Firing Employee costs 2√ó upkeep. Does not apply to unskilled workers.  
- **Transparent Play**: All information (cash, tokens, designs) is public.  
- **Advanced Variant**: Turn order based on score after each Exhibition Round.
