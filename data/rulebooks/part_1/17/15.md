## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective
Cosmic Encounter is a strategic board game of diplomacy, bluffing, and shifting alliances. Players take on unique Alien Powers with special abilities, competing to establish colonies on planets outside their home system. The primary objective is to be the first to establish four bases on planets in other players' systems.

### 2. Components
- Alien Power Cards (each with unique abilities)
- Challenge Cards (Attack, Compromise, War, Defend)
- Destiny Pile (color-coded cards determining challenge targets)
- Hyperspace Cone (challenge token holder)
- Player Boards (Star Disks)
- Tokens (in various colors for each player)
- Reinforcement Cards
- Flare Cards (Wild and Super versions)
- Edict Cards
- Lucre (currency counters)
- Warp (token repository)
- Rewards Deck
- Hazard Deck
- Special game elements: Blue Moons, Reverse Hexes, Theurgy cards, Daggers

### 3. Setup
1. Each player selects or randomly receives an Alien Power
2. Players place four tokens on their home planet
3. Each player receives four Challenge Cards
4. Destiny Pile is shuffled with one card per player color
5. Challenge Deck, Rewards Deck, and Hazard Deck are shuffled
6. Lucre distribution: Player 1 gets 4, Player 2 gets 5, etc. (position + 3)
7. Place Hyperspace Cone in center

### 4. Gameplay Flow
1. **Start Turn**: Take 3 lucre + 1 per home base
2. **Retrieve Token**: Draw one token from Warp
3. **Flip Destiny**: Reveal top card of Destiny Pile
4. **Position Cone**: Place cone on target system, point at planet/moon
5. **Play Reinforcements**: Players may play Reinforcement cards
6. **Main Players Reveal**: Simultaneously reveal Challenge Cards
7. **Invoke Powers**: Use Alien Powers, Flares, Edicts
8. **Kickers**: Play Kicker cards
9. **Allies Join**: Players commit to sides
10. **Main Players Invite**: Invite specific allies
11. **Place Tokens**: Commit tokens to cone
12. **Calculate Total**: Determine challenge winner
13. **Resolve Outcome**: Winner gains base, loser loses tokens
14. **Collect Rewards**: Winner draws cards, gains lucre
15. **Consolation**: Loser may collect consolation
16. **End Turn**

### 5. Core Mechanics

**Challenge Resolution**:
- Attack vs. Attack: Higher total wins
- Attack vs. Compromise: Attack wins, Compromise gets consolation
- War: Wins against Attack, loses against Compromise
- Defend: Loses tokens OR gains no consolation

**Alien Powers**:
Each player has unique abilities that modify standard rules:
- Menagerie: Collect prisoner tokens from Warp
- Outrage: Command players holding your prisoners
- Philosopher: Convert challenge prisoner status
- Eagle: Immune to imprisonment
- Bastille: Free allied prisoners when winning
- Brawl: Force opponents to assign guard tokens
- Entrall: Add prisoner count to challenge total
- Feline: Capture losing tokens for negotiation
- Hogan: Free imprisoned tokens
- Houdini: Escape from prisons at start of turn
- Inquisitor: Draw from opponent's hand based on prisoners held
- Knife: Sacrifice prisoners for cards
- Vendetta: Take consolation as prisoners
- Slayer: Trade prisoners for lucre
- Insurgent: Kidnap winner's tokens when losing

**Lucre System**:
- Starting amounts: Position + 3
- Income: 3 + 1 per home base per turn
- Uses:
  - Move tokens between bases: 2 lucre
  - Re-flip Destiny: 10 lucre
  - Replace Alien Power: 25 lucre
  - Add Alien Power: 50 lucre
  - Hire Rogues
  - Retain played Flares: 10 lucre
  - Purchase Dagger Cards
  - Buy tokens from main player: 1-2 lucre
  - Add to challenge total: 1 lucre = 3 points

**Special Challenge Types**:
- **Reverse Cone**: Allies receive swapped rewards
- **Inverse Cone**: Attacker becomes defender
- **Phase Cone**: Tokens can shift allegiance before reveal
- **Continuum Cone**: Challenge proceeds in reverse order

**Blue Moons**:
- Require alliances when challenged
- Special effects when occupied:
  - Commit Moon: Allies must bring equal tokens
  - Bonus Moon: +1 per additional player
  - Deal Moon: Others included in deals
  - Hazard Moon: No Hazard draw on second challenge
  - Hulk Moon: Single token counts as two
  - Loner Moon: No ally invitations allowed
  - Moon Base Epsilon: Counts as victory base
  - Moon Unit Zapper: Remove token when zapped
  - Re-Kicker Moon: Keep played Kickers
  - Rewards Moon: Take cards instead of Warp tokens
  - Threeforce Moon: Reinforcements Ã—3
  - Unfair Moon: No loss on failed deal

**Daggers Expansion**:
- Purchase at turn start: Set 1 (3 lucre), Set 2 (7 lucre), Set 3 (13 lucre)
- One dagger per challenge
- Effects range from reinforcements to system destruction
- Riposte cards can steal played daggers

**Cosmic Dune Variant**:
- Lucre = Spice
- 5-card hand limit (Harkonnen: 7)
- Separate Edict/Flare deck auctioned each interphase
- Spice uses: Buy cards, enhance tokens (3 spice/token), see top deck (5 spice), buy Warp tokens (2 spice/token)
- Unique powers: Atreides, Bene Gesserit, Emperor, Fremen, Guild, Harkonnen

**Hazard System**:
- Secret and public hazards
- Effects: Forced challenges, restrictions, special conditions
- Examples: "Win with lowest Attack Card", "Exactly 12 tokens required", "Only home base tokens allowed"

**Theurgy Cards**:
- Replay one Flare or Edict
- Extra cone token
- Lose one fewer token
- Reduced lucre costs
- Free token from Warp
- Fewer home bases needed for power
- Re-land during challenge

### 6. Scoring & End Game
- **Victory Condition**: First player to establish four bases outside home system
- **Alternative Wins**:
  - Cosmic Dune: Specific power conditions
  - Hazard "Can Win for Losing": No powers, no external bases, exactly 13 tokens in Warp
  - Hazard "Victory in Death": Base per 4 Warp tokens when another wins

**Scoring Elements**:
- Base establishment: Primary victory path
- Lucre: Used for transactions, not direct scoring
- Cards: Resource for challenges
- Prisoners: Negotiation leverage

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
**Timing Conflicts**: Offensive player acts before defensive player in each phase.

**Tie Resolution**: 
- Attack vs. Attack tie: Both lose tokens
- Compromise vs. Compromise: Both get consolation
- War vs. Attack: War wins
- War vs. Compromise: War loses

**Prisoner Rules**:
- Taken when challenge type specifies
- Can be traded, freed, or used for power effects
- Some powers prevent imprisonment

**Alliance Rules**:
- Allies commit tokens to cone
- Share in victory/loss
- Receive appropriate rewards
- Can be zapped or affected by powers

**Token Limits**:
- Maximum 4 tokens per player on cone
- Some powers/moons modify this

**Hand Management**:
- Maximum hand size varies by power
- Excess cards must be played/discarded immediately
- Consolation can temporarily exceed limit

**Power Interactions**:
- Cosmic Zaps stop power usage
- Edicts modify game rules
- Flares provide one-time effects
- Some powers counter others

**Special Situations**:
- No tokens in Warp: Cannot retrieve
- No cards: Cannot play challenge
- Multiple powers: Allowed with sufficient lucre
- Dead players: Tokens remain, powers inactive
