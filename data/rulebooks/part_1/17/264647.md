# Age of Civilization — Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
In Age of Civilization, you lead a civilization through six historical eras, making strategic decisions to grow your people, gain technologies, and construct wonders. The goal is to accumulate the most victory points by the end of round 6. Victory is determined by scoring points from actions, technologies, civilizations, and end-game abilities. In case of a tie: most workers breaks the tie; if still tied, most coins; if still tied, players share victory.

2. Components  
- 4 Technology Cards (1 per player)  
- 2 Scoring Track Cards (form a single scoring track)  
- 1 Permanent Action Card (Wonder, Research, Exploit)  
- 8 Action Cards (form the time track)  
- 48 Civilization Cards  
- 15 Wonder Cards  
- 1 Starter Token  
- 40 Coin Tokens (standard coins)  
- 10 Gold Coin Tokens (each = 3 coins)  
- 20 Technology Tokens  
- 16 Worker Tokens (4 per player)  
- 4 Star Tokens (1 per player, for scoring track)  
- 9 Event Tokens  

*Only for Scenario Mode:*  
- 8 Special Action Cards  
- 6 Command Cards  
- 3 Special Event Tokens  

3. Setup  
1. Each player chooses a color.  
2. Assemble scoring track from two cards. Each player places star token on "0".  
3. Shuffle Wonder deck and reveal 3 + number of players cards to form the wonder market.  
4. Shuffle Civilization deck and reveal 5 cards to form the civilization market. Remaining cards form a draw pile.  
5. Shuffle the 8 Action Cards and place them in a row to form the time track. Place the Permanent Action Card over the first three action cards (covering Wonder, Research, Exploit).  
6. Place all tokens (coins, tech, workers) in supply within reach.  
7. Each player takes:  
   - 1 Technology Card  
   - 3 Coin Tokens  
8. Determine starting player (e.g., most history books). They take the Starter Token.

*Optional Variants:*  
- **Innovation**: Reveal 1 + number of players Special Technology Cards as tech market.  
- **Changing World**: Shuffle 9 Event Tokens, draw 3, place face up above last 3 action cards.  
- **Drafting**: Deal 7 Civilization Cards to each player. Draft them leftward. After selecting 5, discard remaining 2 to form the market.

4. Gameplay Flow  
The game lasts 6 rounds. Each round:  
1. Starting player takes a turn; play proceeds clockwise.  
2. After all players complete their turn, the round ends.  
3. At end of rounds 1–5:  
   a. Pass Starter Token counterclockwise.  
   b. Shift Permanent Action Card one slot right (hiding one action, revealing a new one).  
   c. Check upper-left icon of newly revealed action card:  
      - War (sword): Player(s) with least military strength sacrifice 1 worker.  
      - Plague (meeple): All players sacrifice 1 worker.  
   (In Changing World variant: resolve Event Token first if present.)

5. Core Mechanics  

**Turn Structure (3 Steps):**  
1. **Rise, Annex, or Skip**  
2. **Take Actions or Rest**  
3. **Resolve Workers**  
Then: Activate "Every turn end" abilities.

*Step 1: Rise, Annex, or Skip*  
- **Rise (Round 1 mandatory)**  
   - Choose 1 civilization from market.  
   - Place it in front of you (main civilization).  
   - Take bonus coins on card (if any).  
   - Place 1 coin on each remaining market card (bonus coins).  
   - Draw new civilization card to refill market.  
   - Reset worker count to population value of new civilization.  
   - Previous civilizations become declined (only legacy abilities remain active).  

- **Annex (Rounds 2–6)**  
   - Choose 1 civilization from market.  
   - Place it *under* your current main civilization.  
   - Gain its legacy ability permanently.  
   - Take bonus coins on card (if any).  
   - Place 1 coin on each remaining market card.  
   - Draw new civilization card to refill market.  
   - Gain 1 worker (if under 4).  

- **Skip**  
   - Do nothing in this step. Proceed to actions or rest.  

*Limit:* Maximum 3 civilization cards per player.

*Step 2: Take Actions or Rest*  
- **Available Actions:**  
   - Always: Wonder, Research, Exploit (on Permanent Action Card).  
   - Current Round: 3 actions directly under Permanent Action Card.  
   - Additional: Action slots granted by civilizations or technologies.  

- **Take Actions:**  
   - Assign workers to action slots (1 worker per slot).  
   - Pay required cost (if any).  
   - Resolve effect immediately.  
   - Some actions require sacrifice (red slots).  

- **Rest:**  
   - Assign no workers.  
   - Gain 2 coins and 1 worker (if under 4).

*Step 3: Resolve Workers*  
- Workers on **white** action slots or unassigned: return to player.  
- Workers on **red** action slots: sacrificed (returned to supply).

*End of Turn:*  
- Activate "Every turn end" abilities (in any order).  
- Proceed to next player.

**Action Summary (Effects & Costs):**  
- **Wonder**: Pay $7, score 3 VP, choose 1 Wonder card. Worker sacrificed.  
- **Research**: Pay $4, score 1 VP, gain 1 Technology (following requirement arrows).  
- **Exploit**: Gain $2. Worker sacrificed.  
- **Culture**: Pay $4, score 2 VP.  
- **Build**: Assign 2 workers, pay $2, score 2 VP.  
- **Conquest**: Score 2 VP *if* you have highest (or tied) military strength.  
- **Hunt**: Gain $1, score 1 VP *only if* you have no technologies.  
- **Fish**: Gain $1.  
- **Trade**: Gain $2. All other players gain $1.  
- **Farm**: Assign 2 workers, gain $3.

**Technologies**  
- Start with access to Writing, Sailing, Mining (no prerequisites).  
- Gain technologies by placing tech token on chosen tech (must meet at least one prerequisite).  
- Each tech grants abilities. Some have multiple.  
- Example:  
   - **Science**: When gained, score 1 VP per upcoming action card (including Plague).  
   - **Education**: Every turn end, may perform Culture (pay $4, no worker).  
   - **Babylon**: Every turn end, gain 1 tech (if requirements met).  

**Civilizations**  
Each has:  
- **Main Ability**: Active only when civilization is current (on top).  
- **Legacy Ability**: Always active once civilization is acquired.  
- **Population**: Determines worker count when risen.  
- **Bonus Coins**: Coins on card when selected.

Examples:  
- **Arabia**: Every turn end, gain 1 coin per 2 technologies (round down).  
- **China**: When performing Farm, gain 1 worker (if under 4).  
- **Maya**: May perform actions from future (unavailable) action cards.  
- **Vikings**: Others cannot use Trade action; you gain 1 military strength.

**Military Strength**  
- Total number of shield symbols from civilizations and technologies.  
- Used in:  
   - War events (lowest must sacrifice 1 worker)  
   - Conquest action (must have most to score)  
   - Some abilities (e.g., Persia gains coins during war)

6. Scoring & End Game  
- Game ends after round 6 (Permanent Action Card reaches end).  
- Each player activates "Game end" abilities (e.g., from wonders or civilizations).  
- Total VP from:  
   - Actions performed  
   - Technologies  
   - Wonders  
   - Civilizations (main and legacy)  
   - End-game abilities  

- **Winning**: Highest VP wins.  
- **Tiebreakers**:  
   1. Most workers  
   2. Most coins  
   3. Shared victory

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I exceed 4 workers?**  
  Yes, only via Annex or abilities (e.g., China). Max cap is 4 workers unless otherwise specified.  
- **Do I pay for actions even if ability reduces cost to $0?**  
  Yes, if the action requires payment, you must pay unless explicitly reduced.  
- **Can I perform an action not on the time track?**  
  Yes, if granted by a civilization or technology ability (e.g., Maya, India).  
- **Can I rise/annex if market is empty?**  
  No, unless using Drafting variant (then use hand).  
- **Do legacy abilities stack?**  
  Yes. All legacy abilities from all civilizations are active.  
- **Is Research required to gain tech via ability?**  
  No. Abilities like Babylon’s let you gain tech without performing Research.  
- **What happens if no one has military strength in a war?**  
  All players have 0 → all sacrifice 1 worker.  
- **Can I perform multiple actions in one turn?**  
  Yes, assign workers to multiple available action slots.
