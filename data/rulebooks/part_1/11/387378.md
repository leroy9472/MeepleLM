## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
In the year 2075, cats have taken control of Earth and now seek cosmic dominance. Players lead rival Cat Aeronautics and Space Agency centers, competing to send astronauts into space to explore planets, moons, and deep space. The goal is to score the most points through exploration, completing goals, and fulfilling special conditions. The game ends when one player places all 8 of their astronauts in space or after 11 rocket failures. The player with the most points wins.

### 3. Setup  
1. Place the **cosmic mat** in the center of the table.  
2. Place the **4 goal tokens** next to the mat.  
3. Form a **supply pile** of all point tokens, accessible to all players.  
4. Place the **cosmic failure token** on the first space of the cosmic failure track.  
5. Each player takes:  
   - A player board  
   - 8 astronaut tokens of one color  
   - 8 identical-function astronauts (no differentiation by player)  
6. **Optional Variants Setup:**  
   - **Exploration:** Shuffle 20 exploration tokens; place 5 face-up on designated spaces of the cosmic mat.  
   - **Secret Missions:** Deal 4 secret mission tokens to each player; each chooses 3 to keep (secret), returns 1.  
   - **UFO:** Shuffle 14 expedition tokens into a face-down pile. Place the **UFO marker** on space 25 (+ symbol). Place UFO tokens in a stack beside the mat, value 5 on top.  

The player who most recently piloted a rocket starts first.

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
The game consists of **cosmic expeditions**, each led by a starting player (commander). Expeditions repeat until the game ends.

#### Cosmic Expedition Phases:
1. **Takeoff Phase**  
2. **Travel Phase(s)** (repeated until crash or disembarkation)  
3. **Landing Phase** (after each non-crashing travel)  
4. **End of Expedition**  

After each expedition, the **rocket board passes clockwise** to the next player.

---

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Takeoff Phase  
- The starting player places the **rocket marker** on space 0 of the cosmic mat.  
- They choose one of their astronauts and place it on the **first seat** of the rocket board.  
- Remaining players, in clockwise order, each place one astronaut on the next available seat.  
- The starting player becomes the **commander** until their astronaut disembarks or the rocket crashes.  

> **Astronaut Ability Note:** If the first astronaut has the satellite-start ability, the rocket starts at space 3 instead of 0.

#### Landing Phase  
Occurs after **every non-crash travel phase**, **only if rocket is on space 6 or higher**.

Each player, starting with the commander and proceeding **top to bottom** on the rocket board, chooses:  
- **Stay in rocket**, or  
- **Disembark** at current location.

##### Disembarking Options:

**On a Moon:**  
- Must be adjacent to current space.  
- Place astronaut on **unoccupied moon farthest from rocket** (highest value).  
- **Only one astronaut per moon.**  
- Immediate points: value shown on moon (1, 3, 5, or 10).  
- If all moons occupied â†’ no one can disembark here.

**On a Planet:**  
- Must be adjacent to current space.  
- Place astronaut on planet. **No limit** on astronauts per planet.  
- **Points scored at end of game** based on majority control.  
- Stack astronauts **in order of disembarkation** (bottom to top).  
- Same-color astronauts: place side-by-side in a row.

**In Deep Space (space 25):**  
- **All astronauts must disembark.**

#### Change of Commander  
- If the **current commander disembarks**, the **next astronaut on the rocket** becomes new commander.  
- New commander inherits **unused dice** and leads future rolls and decisions.  
- Rocket board remains with original holder (for turn order clarity).

---

#### Goals  
First player to meet a condition during a **landing phase** takes the corresponding **goal token** (only one player per goal):  
1. **4 astronauts on 4 different planets**  
2. **3 astronauts on 1 planet**  
3. **4 astronauts on 4 different moons**  
4. **2 astronauts in deep space** (comet or galaxy)  

Each goal token is worth **5 points** at endgame.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  

**Q: Can the rocket start at space 3 more than once?**  
A: Yes, if the starting astronaut has the satellite ability, the rocket always starts at space 3, regardless of previous expeditions.

**Q: What happens if all dice are unusable but an afterburner astronaut is present?**  
A: If the current space allows movement with value 1 and an afterburner astronaut is in the rocket, the commander may use it as a die â†’ no crash.

**Q: Can a player use multiple afterburner astronaut abilities in one travel phase?**  
A: Yes. Each afterburner astronaut can be used once per expedition. Commander may use multiple in one phase if desired.

**Q: What happens if the space-time tunnel moves the rocket onto another exploration token?**  
A: Immediately activate the new token. Repeat if chain continues.

**Q: Can a player complete a secret mission after placing the astronaut?**  
A: Yes, as long as the astronaut is placed on the correct moon or planet (matching the space number on the token), the mission is fulfilled when the condition is met during a landing phase.

**Q: Do planet points depend on order of placement?**  
A: Only for tie-breaking. Majority is based on count; order breaks ties.

**Q: Can a player disembark on a moon thatâ€™s not adjacent?**  
A: No. Only moons adjacent to the rocketâ€™s current space are accessible.
