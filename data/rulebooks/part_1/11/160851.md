## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
In *Lanterns: The Harvest Festival*, players are artisans decorating the palace lake with floating lanterns in preparation for a grand festival. The goal is to earn the most Honor by claiming Dedication Tokens through strategic tile placement and lantern card management. The player with the highest Honor at the end of the game wins.

---

### 2. Components  
- **36 Lake Tiles** (including 1 starting tile with a boat)  
- **56 Lantern Cards** (8 cards each in 7 colors: red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple, black)  
- **30 Dedication Tokens** (in 3 types: Four of a Kind, Three Pair, Seven Unique), each stack ranked by point value  
- **3 Generic (Grey) Dedication Tokens** (each worth 4 Honor)  
- **20 Favor Tokens**  
- **1 Start Player Marker** (wooden boat)

---

### 3. Setup  
1. **Place Starting Tile**: Put the Lake Tile with the boat face down in the center. Flip it face up and rotate so each player faces a different edge. The player facing the red edge becomes the starting player.  
2. **Deal Lake Tiles**: Shuffle the remaining 35 Lake Tiles and deal 3 face-down to each player. These are kept secret.  
3. **Form Draw Pile**:  
   - 2 Players: 16 tiles  
   - 3 Players: 18 tiles  
   - 4 Players: 20 tiles  
4. **Lantern Card Supply**:  
   - 2 Players: 5 cards per color  
   - 3 Players: 7 cards per color  
   - 4 Players: 8 cards per color  
   Form 7 face-up stacks by color.  
5. **Dedication Tokens**:  
   - Sort into 3 stacks by type:  
     - Four of a Kind  
     - Three Pair  
     - Seven Unique  
   - Arrange each stack in descending order of value (highest on top).  
   - Adjust by player count:  
     - 4 Players: Use all tokens  
     - 3 Players: Remove all tokens with 4 dots  
     - 2 Players: Remove tokens with 3 or 4 dots  
   - Set aside 3 grey (generic) Dedication Tokens (worth 4 Honor each).  
6. **Distribute Starting Cards**: Each player takes one Lantern Card matching the color of the edge they face on the starting tile. These are placed face-up in front of them.  
7. **Start Player**: The player facing red receives the Start Player Marker.

---

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
On a playerâ€™s turn, actions must be taken in this order:

#### 1. Exchange a Lantern Card (Optional)  
- Once per turn, spend **2 Favor Tokens** to exchange 1 Lantern Card from your collection for 1 of any color from the supply.  
- Cannot be done on the first turn (no Favor Tokens available).  
- Must be done before any other actions.

#### 2. Make a Dedication (Optional)  
- Once per turn, return a valid set of Lantern Cards to the supply to claim the top Dedication Token of the corresponding type:  
  - **Four of a Kind**: 4 cards of the same color  
  - **Three Pair**: 2 cards each of 3 different colors (6 total)  
  - **Seven Unique**: 1 card of each of the 7 colors  
- If the stack is empty, take a **grey Dedication Token (4 Honor)** instead.  
- **Mandatory if holding >12 Lantern Cards**: Before placing a tile, you must make a dedication or discard down to 12.  
- Must be done before placing a tile.

#### 3. Place a Lake Tile (Mandatory)  
- Place one Lake Tile from your hand face up, adjacent to at least one existing tile (edge-to-edge, not diagonal).  
- **Matching Bonuses (Active Player)**:  
  - For each **matching color** on adjacent edges, gain 1 Lantern Card of that color (max 4).  
  - For each **Platform** on a tile involved in a color match (including the newly placed tile), gain 1 Favor Token.  
- **Orientation Bonuses (All Players)**:  
  - Starting with the active player and proceeding clockwise, each player gains 1 Lantern Card matching the color on the side of the newly placed tile they are facing.  
  - If a color is out of supply, that player receives nothing.  
- **Draw Phase**: After placement, if tiles remain in the draw pile, draw 1 Lake Tile to replenish hand to 3.

---

### 5. Core Mechanics  
- **Hand Limit**: If you have **more than 12 Lantern Cards** at any point before placing a tile, you **must** either make a dedication or discard down to 12.  
- **Tile Placement**: Must be adjacent to at least one tile. Orientation matters for color distribution.  
- **Favor Tokens**:  
  - Unlimited supply. If supply runs out, use any substitute.  
  - Earned by matching adjacent tiles that have Platforms.  
- **Dedication Tokens**:  
  - Unlimited via grey tokens. If a stack is empty, replace it with a grey token (4 Honor). If claimed, replace again.  
- **Starting Tile**: The boat on the starting tile is **not** a Platform.  
- **Turn Order**: Always clockwise. Start Player Marker passes implicitly.

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
- **End Condition**: Game ends when the last Lake Tile is placed.  
- **Final Turns**: After the last tile is placed, each player takes **one final turn** (in clockwise order, starting with the original start player) to:  
  - Exchange Lantern Cards (if possible)  
  - Make a Dedication (if possible)  
  - **No tile placement or drawing** (hands may be empty).  
- **Scoring**:  
  - Sum the Honor points from all Dedication Tokens claimed.  
- **Tiebreakers (in order)**:  
  1. Most Favor Tokens  
  2. Most Lantern Cards remaining  
  3. Shared victory (officially allowed)

---

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Are Dedication Tokens unlimited?**  
  Yes. If a stack is empty, place a grey token (4 Honor) in its place. Replace it again if claimed.  
- **Are Favor Tokens unlimited?**  
  Yes. Use substitutes if supply runs out.  
- **How many Lake Tiles?**  
  36 total. The starting tile is included in this count.  
- **Is the boat on the starting tile a Platform?**  
  No. It does not trigger Favor Token gains.  
- **Does the Start Player Marker have other uses?**  
  No official function beyond turn order. Some players use it to mark the last-placed tile for reference.  
- **Does hand limit affect turn order?**  
  No. Discarding or dedicating to meet the 12-card limit is part of your turn and does not alter turn sequence.  
- **What if a Lantern Card color runs out?**  
  That color is temporarily unavailable. Players due that color receive nothing until cards are returned to supply.
