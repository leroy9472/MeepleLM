1. Lore & Objective  
The Roman Empire has fallen, and Europe enters a transformative era. As lords, players compete to build the most prosperous and glorious medieval domain in Iberia. The goal is to construct cities with buildings, walls, and infrastructure while managing resources, disasters, and rivalries. The player with the highest score at game end wins.

3. Setup  
- Form the supply: Sort buildings by type, walls by length, dice by color.  
- With 2 players: Return 1 of each building type and 1 die of each color to the box.  
- Place tracking tokens next to supply:  
  - 4 players: Use all 5 tokens  
  - 2‚Äì3 players: Use 3 tokens (return 2 to box)  
- Each player takes:  
  - 1 player board, 1 screen, 6 resource pegs  
  - 3 yellow dice, 1 gray die  
  - 1 Keep, 3 Longhouses, 1 Farm, 3 walls (length 4)  
  - Scorched areas:  
    - 2 players: 2 each  
    - 3 players: 1 each  
    - 4 players: 0  
- Set pegs on resource track:  
  - 0 Trade Goods, 1 Stone, 2 Wood, 3 Food  
  - 0 Culture, 0 Disaster  
- Players secretly arrange their starting domain (Keep, Farm, Longhouses, Walls, Scorched Areas) behind screens.  
- Reveal domains.  
- Each player rolls gray die; highest swords becomes starting player and places black peg in (Roll) spot on their board.

4. Gameplay Flow  
The game is played in rounds of 6 steps. Each step is completed by all players before moving to the next.  
- Step 1: ROLL ‚Äî Simultaneous  
- Steps 2‚Äì6: Turn-based, clockwise from starting player  

Steps:  
1. ROLL  
2. COLLECT  
3. FEED  
4. DISASTERS  
5. BUILD  
6. EXTORT  

At end of round:  
- Check if game end condition is met (see Scoring & End Game).  
- If not, starting player passes black peg to left; new starting player places it in (Roll) spot.  

5. Core Mechanics  
**Dice Rolling (Step 1)**  
- Roll all dice simultaneously behind screen.  
- First roll: Set aside any skulls (cannot be rerolled).  
- Optional second roll: Reroll any non-skull dice.  
- Set aside new skulls.  
- Optional third roll: Reroll any non-skull dice (including previously set-aside non-skull dice).  
- After final roll, reveal all dice.  
- White dice showing feather: May be rerolled openly in player order (can use another die, even with skull).  

**Collect (Step 2)**  
- Add resources (trade goods, stone, wood, food) and culture shown on dice.  
- Culture only from white and blue dice.  
- Add:  
  - 1 food per Farm  
  - 2 wood per Lumber Mill  
- Excess beyond track capacity is lost.  
- If culture peg exceeds track: Record ‚Äú+25‚Äù on score sheet, reset peg to 0.  

**Feed (Step 3)**  
- Subtract 1 food per die in pool.  
- For each die not fed: Advance disaster track 1.  
- If disaster track exceeds capacity: Record ‚Äú‚Äì25‚Äù on score sheet, reset peg to 0.  

**Disasters (Step 4)**  
- If any skulls rolled, resolve one disaster (total skulls determine effect).  
- Resolve in clockwise order starting with starting player.  
- Effects (from Appendix):  
  - 1 skull: Remove 1 resource of your choice  
  - 2 skulls: Gain disaster points equal to number of clustered buildings (orthogonally adjacent)  
  - 3 skulls: Give 1 scorched area to each opponent  
  - 4+ skulls: Remove 1 building (chosen by player) and return to box  

**Build (Step 5)**  
- Starting player first, then clockwise.  
- 1 build per hammer (ü™ì) rolled.  
- Spend required resources to build 1 wall or 1 building.  
- Structures must be available in supply.  
- Walls: Any length; buildings: As listed.  
- Placement: Any orientation, must fit.  
- Walled Building: Fully enclosed by orthogonally adjacent walls and/or keeps.  
- Diagonally adjacent structures are not adjacent for adjacency or clustering.  
- If last building of a type is taken: Flip a blank tracking token to X side.  
- Benefits are cumulative (e.g., 2 Monasteries = set 2 dice).  

**Extort (Step 6)**  
- Starting player first, then clockwise.  
- Demand 1 resource (food, wood, stone, trade good) from each player with fewer swords (‚öîÔ∏è) showing.

- If target cannot or refuses to pay: Advance disaster track 2 per unpaid resource.

6. Scoring & End Game  
**Game End Condition**  
- Game ends at end of round if:  
  - 4 players: 5 building types exhausted  
  - 2‚Äì3 players: 3 building types exhausted  
- Or, in solo: After 8 rounds.  

**Scoring**  
- Buildings: Score base points. Walled buildings worth double.  
- Bonus Points:  
  - Market: 10 pts if all 8 orthogonally adjacent spaces are empty  
  - Guildhall: 10 pts if adjacent to 4 different building types  
  - University: 5 pts per die in your pool  
  - Cathedral: 10 pts per die in your pool  
  - Harbor (Collector Set): 4 pts per burgher (blue) die  
  - Great Hall (Collector Set): 4 pts per noble (gray) die (must be completely walled when built)  
- Culture: 1 pt per culture point. Player(s) with most (‚â•1) gain 5 bonus pts.  
- Walled Area: Player(s) with largest total enclosed area gain 10 bonus pts.  
  - Count interior spaces and building-occupied spaces (except keeps) fully enclosed by walls.  
  - Scorched areas not counted.  
- Disasters: ‚Äì1 pt per disaster point on track.  

**Winning**  
- Highest score wins.  
- Tiebreakers:  
  1. Most trade goods  
  2. Most stone  
  3. Most wood  
  4. Most food  
- If still tied, share victory.  

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can a building be both walled and adjacent to a scorched area?** Yes, as long as fully enclosed by walls.  
- **Do keeps count toward enclosed area size?** No, but spaces they occupy are counted.  
- **Can you build on a scorched area?** No.  
- **Can walls be placed diagonally?** Yes, but diagonal walls do not enclose.  
- **Can you reroll a white die with feather after resolving all steps?** No ‚Äî reroll immediately after final dice reveal, before resolving feathers.  
- **Can you extort from a player with same number of swords?** No ‚Äî only from those with fewer.  
- **Do skulls from Sir Philippe affect you in solo?** No ‚Äî only your own skulls trigger disasters affecting you.
