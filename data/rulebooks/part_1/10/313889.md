# HOPLOMACHUS VICTORUM – COMPREHENSIVE REFERENCE MANUAL

## 1. Lore & Objective  
Hoplomachus Victorum is a tactical combat and campaign game where players travel across a map, engage in various events, and ultimately face powerful Primus and Scion bosses. The overarching goal is to grow your hero and troop through strategic combat, manage resources, and defeat the Scion in the final battle. Victory is achieved by defeating the Scion in combat. Failure occurs if your hero is defeated without a Blessing to save them.

## 2. Components  
- Hero Tracking Pad (includes Tactic Capacity, Blessings, Prowess, HP)  
- Unit Chips (Hero, Rival, Arena, Opportunity)  
- Health Chips  
- Tactic Chips  
- Tribune Chip  
- Flag Chip  
- Arena Reference Cards  
- Event Cards (Sport, Bloodshed, Opportunity, Primus, Scion)  
- Leadership Tracker  
- Week Tracker  
- Scion Influence Tracker  
- Draw Bag (for units and banes)  
- Prowess Cards  
- Banes (Bag, Unit, Camp)

## 3. Setup  
### Campaign Start  
- Place hero in starting location.  
- Begin with initial units and tactics as per scenario.  
- Set Scion Influence to 0.  
- Mark Week 1.

### Event Setup  
1. **Draw Event Card** – Reveal during Event Phase.  
2. **Identify Event Type**:
   - **Opportunity**: Draw 2 cards, choose 1 to add to camp, discard the other.  
   - **Sport**: Non-lethal for your units. Setup varies by subtype.  
   - **Bloodshed**: Lethal for your units.  
   - **Primus/Scion**: Follow specific setup rules (see below).  
3. **Deploy Rivals**:
   - Rival units deploy to lowest-numbered vacant deployment hexes.  
   - Apply Act-based modifiers (Act II+, see below).  
4. **Place Special Markers**:
   - **Tribune Chip**: In Sport events, mark the 1st unit (Spar) or choose one (other types).  
   - **Flag Chip**: In Capture the Flag, place on Rival Deployment Hex 1.  
5. **Arena Setup**: If arena has an Arena Turn, place arena chips as specified.

## 4. Gameplay Flow  
### Phase 1: Travel Phase  
Choose one:  
1. Move to adjacent map location.  
2. Cross harbor spot to another location without using harbor.  
3. Use harbor: travel to any harbor, disembark to adjacent location.  
- Cannot backtrack, skip, or enter a capital unless ready to fight its Primus.  
- Mark off 1 week (unless Spectating).

### Phase 2: Event Phase  
1. **Draw Event Card** – Reveal and resolve.  
2. **Accept or Spectate**:
   - **Accept**: Proceed to combat setup.  
   - **Spectate**: Discard event, draw 3 cards from current deck, place on top in any order, increase Scion Influence by current Act number, fully heal hero, skip Cleanup, return to Travel Phase.  
   - *Mandatory Spectate*: If you fail to reach a Primus capital by Week 12, you must Spectate until you do.

3. **Combat Sequence** (if accepted):  
   - **Rival Turn**  
   - **Player Turn**  
   - **Arena Turn** (if applicable)  
   - Repeat until win/loss/surrender.

4. **End of Combat Triggers**:
   - Combat ends immediately if:
     - Goal is met (e.g., flag returned, 6 points in King of the Hill).  
     - All enemy units defeated (except Tribune in Spar).  
     - Hero defeated (campaign lost unless Blessing used).  
     - Surrender declared.

### Phase 3: Cleanup Phase  
1. Mark off 1 week.  
2. Collect rewards or punishments.  
3. Bag rivals and return HP to supply.  
4. Bag drawn bag/unit banes (camp banes stay).  
5. Return all Arena and Tactic chips to supply (except those in camp).  
6. Lethal defeated units go into draw bag; Non-Lethal defeats are restored.  
7. Return hero and surviving units to camp with current HP.  
8. Discard event card.

## 5. Core Mechanics  
### Tactic Capacity  
- All heroes have a base Tactic Capacity of 3.  
- Prowess cards may increase this.  
- If exceeding capacity, discard down to limit.

### Unit Descriptors  
Used to determine targets for skills and effects:
- **Local**: Same faction as arena.  
- **Strongest/Weakest**: Most/least HP.  
- **Closest/Farthest**: Least/most movement required.  
- **Deadliest/Least Deadly**: Most/fewest attack dice.  
- **Fastest/Slowest**: Highest/lowest Movement.  
- **Outnumbered**: Troop has fewer units in combat.  
- **Isolated**: No allied units in combat.

### Combat Sequence (Sport/Bloodshed/Primus)  
**Rival Turn**  
a. **Deployment**: Place next rival unit on lowest-numbered vacant hex.  
b. **Place Tactic**: Play next tactic on lineup using Tactic Assignment Table (based on unit’s first class).  
c. **Movement**: Move toward highest-priority reachable target (minimal movement).  
d. **Abilities**: Activate A-skills in any order.  
e. **Engage**: Use Special Skill S; if not possible, attack highest bolded priority in range.

**Player Turn**  
a. **Score Points** (King of the Hill only): 1 point per unit on key hex. Win at 6.  
b. **Deployment**: Deploy 1 unit to vacant deployment hex (if allowed).  
c. **Place Tactic**: Play 1 tactic on non-elite unit (Tactician removes adjacency requirement).  
d. **Movement**: Move units in any order. No moving through or ending on blocked hexes.  
e. **Abilities**: Trigger A-skills in any order.  
f. **Engage**: Attack or use Special Skill S in any order. Check modifiers.

**Arena Turn** (if applicable)  
- Follow Arena Reference Card.  
- For Pluto’s Refugees:  
  1. Movement  
  2. Abilities  
  3. Engage  
- Arena units follow same rules as rivals but are not considered rival units.

### Defeated Units  
- **Hero**: Must use Blessing to survive. If not, campaign lost.  
- **Arena Unit**: Return to supply.  
- **Rival Unit**: Place face down in rival camp.  
- **Your Unit**: Place face down in your camp.  
- **Health Chips**: Return to supply.  
- **Tactics**: Return to supply.  
- **Tribune Chip**: Return to supply. Defeating Tribune reduces hero to 1 HP immediately.  
- **Flag**: Dropped on hex where unit was defeated.

### Surrendering  
- May surrender at start of any turn (yours or rival’s).  
- Combat fails immediately. Proceed to Cleanup Phase.  
- **Cannot surrender** in Primus or Scion combats.

### Act-Based Modifiers (Non-Primus/Scion)  
- **Act I**: No modifiers.  
- **Act II**: +1 unit to end of rival lineup.  
- **Act III**: +1 unit to end of rival lineup. Rival deploys 2 units on first turn.  
- **Act IV**: +1 unit to end of rival lineup. Rival deploys 2 units with +2 HP on first turn.

### Primus Events  
- **Setup**: Use Primus-specific lineup and HP (from heart icon on chip). Ignore printed HP.  
- **Defeat Consequences**: Lethal.  
- **Rewards**:
  1. Fully heal hero.  
  2. Gain 1 Hero Prowess card of choice.  
  3. Recruit random units until troop has 5 total (including hero).  
- **Combat Modifiers**: Defeating a Primus grants +1 Combat Primus Modifier (cumulative, not applied in Scion/Primus fights).

### Scion Event  
- **Location**: Vesuvian Capital only.  
- **Setup**:
  1. Cross off all remaining Blessings. Remove one bane from camp or bag per Blessing.  
  2. Set up per Scion Reference Sheet.  
- **Rules**:
  - Battle to the death.  
  - No lineup; unit cap applies.  
  - All your units may be deployed over time.  
  - Defeated units are lethal.  
  - Scion has special triggers and effects.

**Scion Combat Sequence**  
1. **Rival Turn**  
   a. **Deployment**: Scion deploys first, dazed (skips first Scion Activity Step).  
   b. **Scion Activity Step**:
      i. Roll all attack dice shown.  
      ii. Resolve effects top to bottom.  
      iii. Scion skips other steps unless other rival units present.  
   c. **Movement/Abilities/Engage**: For other rival units (if any).

2. **Player Turn**  
   a. Score Points (if applicable)  
   b. Deployment  
   c. Place Tactic  
   d. Movement  
   e. Abilities  
   f. Engage

3. Repeat until:
   - Scion defeated → Victory.  
   - Goal on Scion sheet completed → Victory.  
   - Hero defeated → Campaign lost.

**Resolving Scion Dice Effects**  
- Resolve in order from top to bottom.  
- **Single die effects**: Trigger if that die rolls a hit.  
- **Total hits**: Sum all hits, apply effect once.  
- **For each hit**: Effect triggers once per hit.  
- **For each pair of hits**: Effect triggers once per matching pair.  
- **Multi-color effects**: Combine hits from specified colors.

## 6. Scoring & End Game  
### Event Victory Conditions  
- **Capture the Flag**: Retrieve flag and return to your deployment hex.  
- **King of the Hill**: Have units on key hexes at start of your turn; earn 1 point per unit. Win at 6.  
- **Spar**: Defeat all rival units except Tribune.  
- **Bloodshed**: Defeat all rival and arena units.  
- **Primus/Scion**: Defeat target or complete printed goal.

### Rewards  
- **Bloodshed Win**:
  - Choose one: +1 Leadership, +1 Hero HP (fully heal), or upgrade/gain 1 die.  
- **Sport Win**:
  - Choose one: Recruit 1 rival unit (return one to bag if over capacity), or gain tactics up to Tactic Capacity.  
- **Primus Win**:
  - Fully heal hero.  
  - Gain 1 Hero Prowess card.  
  - Recruit until 5 total units.

### Punishments  
- **Failed Non-Scion Event**: Gain Scion Influence equal to current Act. No rewards.

### Final Victory  
- Defeat the Scion in combat.  
- Final difficulty is reduced by number of unused Blessings.

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I use a Blessing outside combat?**  
  No. Blessings can only be used during combat for Survive or Reinforce.

- **What happens if I defeat the Tribune in a Sport event?**  
  Hero is reduced to 1 HP immediately, even if not in combat.

- **Can I surrender a Primus or Scion fight?**  
  No. Surrender is not allowed in Primus or Scion combats.

- **Do Act modifiers apply to Primus fights?**  
  No. Use Primus-specific setup instead.

- **What happens to tactics on a defeated unit?**  
  Return to supply.

- **Can I voluntarily drop the flag?**  
  No. Flagholder cannot unequip the flag.

- **When is the flag returned?**  
  When a unit carrying the flag moves onto one of your deployment hexes.

- **Can I have more than 5 units after a Primus win?**  
  No. Recruit only until you have 5 total units.

- **Do Combat Primus Modifiers apply to Scion fights?**  
  No. They are not applied in Scion or Primus combats.
