# Clinic: Deluxe Edition – Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
Clinic: Deluxe Edition is a competitive strategy game in which players build and manage their own private clinics. The goal is to earn the most Popularity by treating patients efficiently, constructing specialized medical facilities, managing staff, and fulfilling expansion-specific objectives. Players must balance income, expenses, time, and patient care while adapting to evolving conditions and expansions.

The game ends after 6 rounds (7 with the "R7" expansion). Final scoring determines the winner based on Popularity earned from staff, buildings, treated patients, and special conditions.

## 2. Components
- Main board with Appointment Notepad, University, and Parking Lot
- Player boards (4×3 or 3×3 per floor)
- Floor extension boards (Floors 1–4, Basement)
- Module tiles: Service Hubs, Treatment Rooms, Supply Rooms, Special Modules (e.g., Operating Room, Lab, ICU, Outpatient Services)
- Staff: Doctors (white, yellow, orange, red, Workaholic), Nurses, Orderlies, Janitors, CEO, Genetic Counselor, etc.
- Patients: Cubes in white, yellow, orange, red, critical, disgruntled, morbidly obese, etc.
- Cars, Parking tiles, Conveyors
- Action tiles, Bonus tiles, Urban Design cards
- Doctor and Patient Pool bags
- Time track, Popularity track, Turn order track
- Expansion-specific components: Blood tokens, Fire Extinguishers, Zombies, Satellite Dishes, Cryotanks, etc.

## 3. Setup
### General Setup
1. **Module Displays**: Place stacks of Service Hubs, Treatment Rooms, Supply Rooms, and Special Modules beside the main board. Flip top tiles face up.
2. **University**: Fill University spaces with Doctors from the bag, sorted by experience level. Only one Workaholic Doctor allowed at a time; return extras.
3. **Appointment Notepad**: For each row (per player count), place one patient in the rightmost space.
4. **Parking Lot**: Dump all Cars into the Parking Lot.
5. **Expansion Boards**: Set up boards for active expansions (e.g., Virus, ICU, Pharmacy, Urban Design, etc.).

### Player Setup
1. **Player Board**: Choose 4×3 (beginner) or 3×3 (expert) side. Add Floor 2–4 or Basement boards as needed.
2. **Initial Clinic**: Construct:
   - 1 Psychiatry Service Hub
   - 1 Treatment Room
   - 1 Supply Room (must be on Ground Floor)
   - 1 Entrance (must connect to Service Hub)
   - Modules may be placed on higher floors if supported.
3. **Starting Staff**: Place one white Doctor (from supply) at the starting Entrance. Park their Car on a border space.
4. **Bonus Tile Draft**: Reveal one more Bonus tile than player count. In reverse turn order, draft one tile. Apply effects:
   - Tile 1: Start with Nurse + Car
   - Tile 2: Start with Orderly + Car
   - Tile 3: Start with Garden
   - Tile 4: Start with white Psychiatric Patient in Pre-Admissions + Car
   - Tile 5: Start with 2-space Parking tile
   - Tile 6: Start with yellow Doctor instead of white
   - Tile 7: Start with $20 instead of $15
5. **Urban Design (if used)**: Draft one Easy, one Medium, one Hard card. Choose one as goal; set others aside.
6. **Other Expansions**:
   - Hospice: Fill Hospice board with 4 Elders
   - Obstetrics: Place Pregnant Women on Obstetrics board
   - Blood Transfusion: Include Blood tokens
   - Zombies: Place Zombie tokens nearby

## 4. Gameplay Flow
Each round consists of three phases, repeated in order:

### Phase 1: Actions (Repeated 3 Times)
All players simultaneously choose one of three actions using action tiles. Resolve in order: **Build → Hire → Admit Patients**. After each action resolution, refill new patients.

#### Build
- Perform up to 2 builds.
- Pay cost based on floor level.
- Construction Rules:
  - Support: Modules above Ground Floor must have a module directly below.
  - No Matches: Same-type modules (including color) cannot be orthogonally or vertically adjacent.
  - No Obstructions: Cannot build where cars are parked or objects occupy the space.
  - Separate Buildings: Once built, a separate building (connected only via Gardens, Parking, etc.) cannot later be merged via modules.
  - Service Hub Limit: One per floor per building.
  - Special Module Limit: One per player per round.
- Module-Specific Rules:
  - **Service Hub**: Must be on Ground Floor initially; adjacent Treatment Rooms treat matching patients.
  - **Treatment Room**: Must be adjacent (same floor) to a Service Hub and adjacent (any floor) to a Supply Room to function.
  - **Supply Room**: Supports adjacent Treatment Rooms; one per Supply Room.
  - **Operating Room**: Adjacent to Service Hub on same floor; treats one patient.
  - **Outpatient Services**: Any Doctor can treat any patient.
  - **Lab**: Doctor inside levels up twice during Admin.
  - **Triage**: Reduces time spent by 3 (min. 0).
  - **Garden**: Ground Floor or atop another Garden; no car obstruction.
  - **Helipad**: Must be above a module; nothing may be built above it.
  - **Conveyor**: First build requires two orthogonally aligned stops; later stops must align with network.
  - **Extinguisher**: Built on non-treatment modules; adds $1 cost to enter.
  - **ICU**: Adjacent to Service Hub; treats critically ill patients.
  - **Blood Transfusion**: Employee lies in empty Service Hub to donate blood (3 Time); Nurse carries Blood token to Treatment Room (0 extra Time).
  - **Workaholic Doctor**: Treats multiple patients without Nurses; costs $8 to hire and maintain.
  - **Zombies**: Formed from two yellow patients in a Treatment Room with no staff; kill anyone in same space at Move end.

#### Hire
- Hire:
  - 1 Doctor (cost based on University position), OR
  - 1 Janitor, OR
  - 1 Capsule (Pharmacy)
- AND
  - 1 Nurse (from current round’s column), OR
  - 1 Orderly, OR
  - 1 Secretary (Se)
- Place new hires at any Entrance or Helipad. Park their Car.
- Workaholic Doctors cost $8.
- Capsule allows Nurse to treat patients without Doctor (only if no Doctor is present).

#### Admit Patients
- Queue Points (QP) = 1 + number of Entrances + Helipads
- Use QP to:
  - Swap orthogonally adjacent patients on Notepad (1 QP)
  - Move rightmost (red column) patient to your Pre-Admissions (1 QP + park Car)
- Appointment queues auto-shift right to fill gaps.
- After resolving all Admit actions, refill empty rightmost spaces with new patients from bag.

### Phase 2: Business
#### Patient Care & Income
- Treat patients in Treatment Rooms, Operating Rooms, ICU, Outpatient Services, etc.
- Requirements:
  - Doctor must match patient color or have Nurses bridging color difference (1 Nurse per level difference).
  - Overqualified Doctors do not need Nurses.
  - Workaholic Doctor needs no Nurses; treats up to 4 white, 3 yellow, 2 orange, or 1 red patient.
  - Capsule + Nurse can treat patient if no Doctor is present.
  - Blood token allows Nurse to improve patient by one level.
- Income: Based on patient color ($40 red → $8 white). ICU improves by two levels.
- Bonuses:
  - Adjacent Garden: +$2
  - Adjacent Extinguisher: +$3
  - Satellite Dish above: +$3

#### Expenses
- Pay all employees (Doctors $4–$1, Nurses $3, Orderlies $2, etc.; Workaholic $8)
- Module upkeep: $1 per module (reduced $3 per Orderly in Supply Room, min $0)
- Operating Room used: +$2
- Medical Scanner: +$2
- Conveyors: $1 + highest floor + number of treatable buildings (reduced $3 per Janitor)
- Unspent blue capsules (LBP): $1 each
- VIP Parking: Pays all players based on limos present

#### Gain Popularity
- Buy Popularity: $3 per point (varies by expansion)
- Surgeons: +2 Popularity each
- Treated lively elders (LBP): +1 Popularity
- Cured zombies: +$32, +1 Popularity per cured zombie
- Used condoms (CVM): +3 Popularity each

### Phase 3: Administration
1. Advance round marker.
2. Reset action marker to 1.
3. Return patients under red X on Notepad to Patient Pool bag.
4. Level up University doctors; refill and sort.
5. Remove current round’s nurses from University.
6. Level up doctors in Labs twice (+1 Popularity each).
7. Level down all other doctors (except Workaholics, Surgeons, etc.).
8. Worsen untreated patients (white → yellow → orange → red → die; -5 Popularity).
9. Flip top Service Hub and Special Module tiles if stack lacks face-up tile.
10. Reset turn order:
    - Rank by Popularity (lowest first)
    - Tiebreaker: Player who reached score first goes later
    - If order unchanged, rotate: last player becomes first

## 5. Core Mechanics
- **Buildings**: Group of adjacent modules containing at least one Service Hub. Outpatient Services alone is not a building.
- **Movement**:
  - Time cost depends on distance and terrain.
  - Free movement before entry: between Entrances/Helipads.
  - Conveyors: 0 Time between aligned stops.
  - Triage: -3 Time (min 0).
- **Staff Management**:
  - Excess Orderlies (more than Supply Rooms) are automatically fired.
  - Workaholics never leave unless lying down at round end.
- **Patient Flow**:
  - Patients enter via Pre-Admissions or directly to Treatment Rooms.
  - Must be treated or will worsen and die.
  - Died patients go to supply (unless morgue available: +$32, no Popularity loss).
- **Expansions**:
  - **Blood Transfusion**: Blood donation in Service Hub; Nurses deliver to Treatment Rooms.
  - **Zombies**: Two yellow patients → Zombie; 1 Doctor + 1 Zombie in Lab → cure for $32.
  - **ICU**: Treats critical patients; adjacent to Service Hub.
  - **Urban Design**: Score bonus for matching grid patterns.
  - **Basement**: Special modules like Locker Room (level up Doctor), Funkenstein Lab (worsen patients).

## 6. Scoring & End Game
Game ends after 6 (or 7) rounds. Score Popularity:

- **Staff**: +5 (red Doctor) down to +1 (white Doctor); Nurses +3, Orderlies +2
- **Buildings**: +8 for each additional building that can treat patients (requires Service Hub + Treatment Room + Supply Room adjacency)
- **Treatment Rooms**: +2 × floor number (if adjacent to Service Hub and Supply Room)
- **Sperm Bank (TSB)**: +4 × floor number (if Treatment Room half adjacent to Supply Room)
- **Basement Modules**: +4 each
- **Patients**: -8 (red) to -2 (white) for untreated patients
- **Time Spent**: Penalty based on position on Time Track
- **Cryogenics (Cryo)**: Match Doctor to cryopreserved patient; earn $48–$12; unmatched → -5 Popularity
- **Zombies**: +9 Popularity each if still in clinic
- **Urban Design**: Fulfill secret card goal for bonus
- **Solitaire Mode**: Complete specific goals (e.g., ≤12 Time, 7 Doctors on board)

Final Popularity determines winner. Tiebreaker: Fewest patients in clinic.

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Patient Pool empty?** Only possible in last round; skip refill if needed.
- **Doctor Pool empty?** Skip refilling University if bag is empty.
- **Can I build on higher floors during setup?** Yes, as long as construction rules are followed.
- **Does Outpatient Services count as a building?** No — requires a Service Hub.
- **Can I move staff between Entrances before entry?** Yes — 0 Time.
- **Do Ghosts (G) cost extra Time?** Yes — +1 Time to pass through.
- **Can Workaholic Doctors level up?** Only if standing in Lab; they do not level down.
- **Can I use Capsule and Blood in same room?** Yes — but Nurse cannot carry both.
- **Do treated patients take Cars?** Yes — unless Elder (H), who leaves without car.
- **What happens to fired Orderlies?** Remove from game; return Car to Parking Lot.
