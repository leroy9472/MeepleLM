### 1. Lore & Objective
In *Abomination: The Heir of Frankenstein*, players are scientists in 1819 Paris competing to reanimate a monster from body parts, continuing the work of Victor Frankenstein. A mysterious benefactor (the Creature) sponsors the competition, while Captain Walton seeks to destroy it. The game ends when either a player successfully brings their monster to life (all 6 parts alive) or the Captain reaches the final space of the story track. The player with the most victory points wins.

### 3. Setup
1. Place Paris Board in center; Event Board above it.
2. Sort and place cadaver decks:
   - Morgue: face-up on Morgue location
   - Cemetery: face-down on Cemetery
   - Hospital: draw face-up stack equal to player count
   - Public Square: off-board, face-down
3. Shuffle and deal:
   - 3 Scoundrel Cards face-up at Docks
   - 2 Research Cards face-up at Academy
   - 2 Humanity Cards face-up at Saint-Roch
4. Form event deck: 6 random Event + 4 random Encounter cards, add 2 Event cards to top, cover with Event Cover Card.
5. Place Captain meeple on first space of story track.
6. Place appropriate Bribe & Bump Track for player count.
7. Randomly select and place 4 of 6 Bonus Objective Markers.
8. Create supply piles: Materials, Francs, Markers, Leyden Jars, Ice Blocks, Police Markers, Shock Dice, Monster Parts.
9. Each player takes:
   - 1 Character Card
   - 1 Laboratory Board
   - 1 Anatomy Card
   - 1 Player Reference Card
   - Francs = player count
   - 4 Scientist Meeples, 3 Assistant Meeples, 1 Score Marker (matching lab color)
10. Set attribute dials: Humanity = 0, Reputation = 1, Expertise = 1.
11. Place 1 Scientist and 2 Assistants on character card; rest aside.
12. Place score marker on 0 of score track.
13. Randomly determine First Player; give them First Player Creature Meeple.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
Each round has 4 phases:
1. **Event Phase**: First Player draws and resolves top event/encounter card.
2. **City Phase**: Players take turns placing meeples on locations (Paris or Lab), resolving effects.
3. **Lab Phase**: All players simultaneously:
   - Build Monster Parts (multiple times)
   - Throw the Switch (once)
   - Preserve Materials (multiple times)
4. **Reset Phase**:
   - Decompose materials (unless ice block present)
   - Refresh cards (Hospital, Docks, Academy, Saint-Roch)
   - Return meeples to character cards
   - Advance Captain meeple 1 space (end game if reaches last space)

#### Building Monster Parts
1. Choose part (max 1 Head, 1 Torso, 2 Arms, 2 Legs).
2. Meet Expertise requirement.
3. Discard required materials (note worst decomposition stage).
4. Place muscle-side token on operating table (or flip to skin side if completing).
5. Gain 1 Expertise and VPs based on worst material stage, minus penalties for animal parts.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
Game ends when:
- Captain reaches final story track space, or
- One or more players have all 6 monster parts alive at end of Lab Phase.

**End Game Scoring**:
1. **Alive Points**: VP for each part with âŠ™ marker (from Anatomy Card).
2. **Bonus Objectives**: 10 VP each for achieved objectives.
3. **Reputation & Expertise Dials**: VP equal to highest value reached.
4. **Humanity Dial**: VP equal to highest or lowest value reached (player chooses gain or loss).
5. **Captain End**: If game ends via Captain, player with highest positive Humanity gains 5 VP (all tied players gain 5).

**Tiebreakers**:
1. Most alive monster parts
2. Most Francs
3. Turn order (First Player, then clockwise)

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Downgraded Parts**: Do not lose EXPERTISE or VPs gained when built; can gain again if rebuilt.
- **Bumping**: Can bump own meeples for 0 cost if Bump Track has space.
- **Dice Rolling**: Must roll all dice at once; cannot roll one at a time.
- **Humanity at -10**: Cannot gain/lose Humanity; can still gain benefits from actions that would cost Humanity.
- **Reversing Dial Effects**: If dial moves past a space in opposite direction, undo the effect (e.g., lose Assistant if Reputation drops below 7).
- **Encounter Cards**: Must be resolved when triggered; if forgotten, discard without effect.
- **Animal Parts**: Can substitute for most materials, but not for level 3/4 blood. Penalizes VPs when building.
- **Preserved Materials**: Can only be sold at Market; cannot be used to build parts.
