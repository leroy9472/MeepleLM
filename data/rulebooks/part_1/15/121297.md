# Fleet – Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
In the remote Ridback Bay in Nunavut, Canada, previously inaccessible waters have opened to fishing. Players become entrepreneurs, acquiring licenses, launching boats, and harvesting fish to build the most profitable fleet. The objective is to earn the most Victory Points (VP) through licenses, launched boats, fish crates on boats, and end-game bonuses.

## 2. Components
- **License Cards (58 total):**
  - Standard (40): Cod, Shrimp, Lobster, Tuna, Processing Vessel, Char, Salmon, Swordfish, Pacific Oyster
  - Premium (18): King Crab, Fishermen's Pub, Nunavut Weather Authority, Wharfside Casino, Canadian DFO, Captain's Club, Marina
- **Boat Cards (176):** Matching standard license types; used as boats or cash.
- **Dock Cards (48):** Artisan Fisherman, First Mate, House Boat, Inuit Fisherman, Research Vessel, Salty Captain, Tug Boat
- **Bay Cards:** Crabs, Crewman, Gone Fishin', Salvage Yard, Trophy Cards
- **Fish Crates:** 150 cubes (1 per crate)
- **Starting Player Marker**
- **Fleet Die** (for Nunavut Weather Authority and Wharfside Casino)

## 3. Setup
1. **Select Licenses:** Choose 5 Standard and 2 Premium license types for the game. All cards of selected types are included.
2. **Adjust for Player Count:**
   - 4 players: Set aside 6 Premium and 8 Standard licenses. Remove none. Shuffle 6 Premium into remaining Standard deck, place 8 Standard on top.
   - 3 players: Set aside same, remove 2 Premium and 2 Standard. Shuffle 4 Premium in, place 6 Standard on top.
   - 2 players: Set aside same, remove 3 Premium and 6 Standard. Shuffle 3 Premium in, place 4 Standard on top.
3. **Place Auction Market:** Reveal 1 license per player.
4. **Prepare Special Cards:**
   - Salty Captain & Inuit Fisherman: Place 2 per player face up.
   - Salvage Yard: Mix 4 into Standard licenses.
   - Gone Fishin', Crabs, Trophy Cards: Set up as specified.
   - Crewman: Insert Hire Crewman cards into deck piles during setup.
5. **Starting Hands:** Each player gets 1 of each boat type in play.
6. **Fish Supply:** 25 crates per player (50, 75, 100 for 2/3/4 players).
7. **Starting Player:** Randomly determine.

## 4. Gameplay Flow (per round)
1. **Auction Licenses:** Players bid one at a time. Winner pays and takes license. Repeat until all pass or buy. Refresh market to player count.
2. **Launch Boats & Hire Captains:**
   - Launch: Pay to place boat from hand (matching owned license). Max 1 unless bonus.
   - Hire: Place boat card face down on boat as captain. Max 1 unless bonus.
   - Dock Cards (e.g., Inuit Fisherman, Salty Captain) may be purchased here.
3. **Fishing:** Each captained boat gains 1 fish crate (unless modified).
4. **Processing & Trading:**
   - Process: Move up to 1 fish per boat to Processing Vessel license.
   - Trade: Trade 1 processed fish for 1+ boat cards (based on PV licenses).
5. **Draw:** Draw 2, keep 1 (modified by Tuna, etc.).
6. **End of Round Effects:** Activate Nunavut Weather Authority or Wharfside Casino in turn order.

## 5. Core Mechanics
- **Transactions:** Pay using boat cards (cash value), license discounts (Shrimp), or processed fish ($1 each).
- **Bonuses by License:**
  - **Shrimp:** $1 off per license (stacking).
  - **Cod:** Launch 2 boats + draw 1–4 cards (based on count) if any launched.
  - **Lobster:** Hire 2 captains + draw bonus based on total captained boats.
  - **Tuna:** Enhanced draw (keep all, discard any).
  - **Processing Vessel:** Process 1 fish/boat; trade 1 processed fish for 1–4 cards.
  - **King Crab:** End-game VP based on criteria (e.g., number of captains).
  - **Fishermen's Pub:** 10 VP at game end.
  - **Nunavut Weather Authority:** Roll die at round end to harm others (lose fish or boats).
  - **Wharfside Casino:** Gamble fish to potentially gain more.
  - **Salvage Yard:** Discard boat card to draw cards (scaled by number owned).
- **Dock Cards:**
  - **Inuit Fisherman:** $4, counts as launch, no license/captain needed, fishes immediately.
  - **Salty Captain:** $4, replaces captain, gives 2 fish/turn and 8 capacity.
- **Processing:** Processed fish can be used as $1 or traded for cards, but not scored.

## 6. Scoring & End Game
### End Game Triggers (complete round, then score):
- Cannot refill auction market.
- Fish supply depleted (players credited for fish due).

### Scoring:
- VP on purchased licenses.
- VP on launched boats.
- 1 VP per fish crate on boats (not processed).
- Bonus VP from licenses (e.g., King Crab, Wharfside Casino, Captain's Club).
- Promo cards: Inuit Fisherman (boat + captain), Nunavut Weather Authority (7 VP), Wharfside Casino (7 VP + 1 per fish on it).

### Tiebreaker:
1. Most launched boats.
2. Most fish crates on boats.
3. Shared victory.

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Overpaying:** Allowed; no change given.
- **Processing Limit:** 1 fish per boat, not per player.
- **Salty Captain:** Gives 2 fish/round, 8 capacity, counts as captain.
- **Inuit Fisherman:** Counts as boat and captain, no license needed.
- **Salvage:** Costs a launch, can be combined with normal launch if bonus allows.
- **Nunavut Weather Authority:** Can sink boats; fish lost are removed from game.
- **Wharfside Casino:** Can gamble existing processed fish; fish on it not spendable.
- **Crewman:** Immediate bonuses when license purchased; Launch Two Boats Crewman held and used later.
- **Trophies:** Awarded during game or at end; worth 5 VP each.
