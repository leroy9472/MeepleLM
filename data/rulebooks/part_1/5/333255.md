## PART 1 — Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
- The players control monster clans defending their dungeon from invading heroes.  
- The objective is to survive two waves of hero invasions without allowing the heroes to plunder the Treasure with value 4.  
- Victory is achieved by surviving the second wave. Immediate defeat occurs if Treasure 4 is looted.

2. Components  
- Monster Clan cards and matching meeple figures  
- Tactics cards (unique per clan)  
- Loot cards: Beasts, Potions, Equipment, Scrolls, Gadgets  
- Guild deck: Hero cards (Warrior, Rogue, Mage, Archer) and Scenario cards  
- Dungeon rooms: Vault, Library, Sewers, Graveyard, Workshop, Forge, Study, Apothecary, Beast Trainer, Guardians, Laboratory, Builder, Cells  
- Tokens: Coins, Books, Frogs, Bones, Traps, Treasure tokens (values 1–4), Wound tokens  
- Portal tokens (incomplete and completed)  
- Difficulty Level tile (Family, Challenging, Hardcore)  
- Active Player token

3. Setup  
- Choose a scenario from the Dungeon Book and build the dungeon accordingly.  
- Shuffle all Loot cards (Beasts, Potions, Equipment, Scrolls, Gadgets) to form the Loot deck. Reveal top 5 cards.  
- Shuffle Treasure tokens face down and place randomly in designated rooms.  
- Create the Guild deck by combining Hero and Scenario cards as specified by the scenario.  
- Each player chooses a Monster Clan:  
  - Take matching Clan card, Tactics cards, and meeple figures.  
  - Place monsters in starting rooms as indicated on the Clan card (e.g., Imps: 4 meeple in "Swords" rooms).  
  - Remaining monsters go to the Lair.  
- Shuffle Tactics deck and draw 5 cards.  
- Place the Guild deck on the chosen Difficulty Level tile:  
  - Family: Wave 1 = 1 card, Wave 2 = 2 cards  
  - Challenging: Wave 1 = 2 cards, Wave 2 = 2 cards  
  - Hardcore: Wave 1 = 2 cards, Wave 2 = 3 cards  
- The player with the fewest monsters takes the Active Player token.

4. Gameplay Flow  
1. Play Cards from Hand:  
   - Play cards one at a time; resolve actions in any order.  
   - Actions may be split among monsters.  
   - If deck is empty, shuffle discard pile to form new deck.  
2. Refresh Loot (Optional):  
   - Discard a Tactics card to replace 1–5 revealed Loot cards.  
   - May be done multiple times per turn.  
3. Clean Up and Upkeep:  
   - Move played cards to discard pile.  
   - Draw cards to refill hand to 5. Shuffle if needed.  
4. Heroes Invasion:  
   - Draw and resolve Guild cards based on difficulty and wave.  
   - Fully resolve each card before drawing the next.  
   - If deck is exhausted during Wave 1, reshuffle and begin Wave 2.  
5. End Turn:  
   - Pass Active Player token to the left.

5. Core Mechanics  
- Actions:  
  - Move: Move monster to adjacent room; carry one resource/trap.  
  - Melee Attack: Damage hero in same room.  
  - Ranged Attack: Damage hero in adjacent connected room.  
  - Draw: Draw one card.  
  - Activate: Use clan ability, activate room, complete portal.  
  - Place Resource: Coin, Book, Frog, Bone in monster’s room.  
  - Trap: Place trap token; deals 1 damage when hero enters.  
  - Portal: Place completed portal token.  
  - Ambush: Summon monsters in matching room types.  
  - Choose Wisely: Choose one side of split actions.  
  - Surge: Perform right action if left is completed.  
  - Swarm: All monsters in room perform action.  
  - Reinforce: Reveal top 3 deck cards; keep matching, discard rest.  
- Portals:  
  - Complete by using Activate in rooms with incomplete portals.  
  - Once two rooms have completed portals, they are adjacent for movement.  
  - Monsters use 1 Move to travel between portal-connected rooms.  
- Traps:  
  - Placed via Workshop room or Tactics cards.  
  - Triggered when hero moves into or is summoned to the room.  
  - Discarded after dealing 1 damage.  
- Dungeon Rooms:  
  - Vault: Activate to place Coin.  
  - Library: Activate to place Book.  
  - Sewers: Activate to place Frog.  
  - Graveyard: Activate to place Bone.  
  - Workshop: Activate to place Trap.  
  - Forge: Spend Coin to acquire Equipment.  
  - Study: Spend Book to acquire Scroll.  
  - Apothecary: Spend Frog to acquire Potion.  
  - Beast Trainer: Spend Bone to acquire Beast.  
  - Guardians: Spend Trap to acquire Gadget.  
  - Laboratory: Activate to send hero to Cells and acquire Tactics card.  
  - Builder: Spend Coins to build new room with matching Treasure.  
  - Cells: Once per turn, draw Guild card; if hero, place in Cells exhausted and draw 3 cards. Mage frees all imprisoned heroes.  
- Hero Resolution (per card):  
  A. Spawn hero in matching room type.  
  B. “Show Off”: Activate hero ability (Archer ranged attack, Warrior discards resources, Rogue removes trap, Mage frees Cells).  
  C. Check for traps: deal 1 damage, discard trap.  
  D. Inspire: Reactivate exhausted heroes in room.  
  E. Attack: Deal 1 damage to weakest monster.  
  F. Plunder: If active heroes ≥ treasure value, exhaust to loot.  
  G. Charge: Move toward Vault if no monsters or treasure.  
  H. Exhaust hero after actions.

6. Scoring & End Game  
- No scoring system.  
- Win: Survive the second wave (i.e., draw and resolve final card of second wave without Treasure 4 being looted).  
- Lose: Heroes plunder Treasure 4 at any time.  
- Scenario-specific loss conditions may apply (e.g., Tomb of the Necromancer).

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- Can I play cards after sending a hero to Cells?  
  Yes, “throwing a hero to Cells” can be done at any point during your turn.  
- What happens if I draw a Mage to Cells?  
  Draw 3 cards, then immediately resolve Mage’s ability: reactivate all heroes in Cells. Resolve them before continuing your turn.  
- Can heroes use Portals?  
  No, heroes ignore portals and follow standard passageways.  
- Can I choose not to use all actions on a card?  
  Yes.  
- What if a required hero token is not available?  
  Reactivate all exhausted heroes of that type and any in the same rooms.  
- Can I use another clan’s Tactics card acquired via Laboratory?  
  Yes. Resolve actions using your own monsters.  
- How are ties resolved when heroes charge?  
  Players collectively decide which path to take.  
- Can traps be moved?  
  Yes, monsters can carry traps when moving.
