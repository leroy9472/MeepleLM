## Ticket to Ride: Marklin — Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
Ticket to Ride: Marklin introduces passengers and merchandise tokens to the classic train route-building gameplay. Players build rail networks across Germany, complete destination tickets, and move passengers to collect valuable tokens. The goal is to earn the most points through completed tickets, moving passengers, and bonuses.

---

### 2. Components  
- Marklin Train Card deck (including Locomotive and +4 Locomotive cards)  
- Player train pieces (45 per player)  
- Passenger pieces (3 per player)  
- Merchandise Tokens:  
  - 1 stack of Black (7,6,5,4)  
  - 6 stacks of Red (4,3,2)  
  - 12 stacks of Yellow (3,2,1)  
  - 16 white tokens (value 2)  
- Destination Tickets: Short (blue) and Long (brown) decks  
- Scoring marker and score track  
- "Most Completed Tickets" bonus tile (+10 points)

---

### 3. Setup  
- **Player Count**: 2–5 players.  
- **Train Cards**: Each player receives 4 train cards.  
- **Destination Tickets**:  
  - Draw 4 tickets (any mix of short and long), keep at least 2.  
  - Return unkept tickets to the bottom of their respective decks.  
- **Passengers**: Each player takes 3 passenger pieces.  
- **Merchandise Tokens**:  
  - Create stacks by color and place them on matching city spaces.  
  - Stacks are placed in descending order (highest value on top).  
- **Card Pool**: Reveal 5 face-up train cards. If 3 Locomotives appear, discard and replace all 5.  
- **Bonus Tile**: Place the "+10 Most Completed Tickets" bonus nearby.

---

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
On your turn, perform **one** of the following actions:

#### 1. Draw Train Cards  
- Take 2 train cards:  
  - From the face-up display (refill immediately), OR  
  - Blindly from the deck.  
- **Locomotive Rule**:  
  - If you take a face-up **Locomotive**, you may not take a second card.  
  - If you draw a Locomotive **blindly**, you may take a second card.  
- **+4 Locomotive Cards**:  
  - Treated as regular Locomotives.  
  - Can **only** be used to claim routes of length 4 or more.  
  - If drawn face-up, you **may** still take a second card.  
- **Refresh Rule**: If 3 Locomotives (regular or +4) are face-up, discard all 5 and replace.

#### 2. Claim a Route  
- Play train cards matching the route’s color and length.  
- Grey routes: Use any single color.  
- Place your trains on the route.  
- Score points:  
  | Length | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | 5 | 6 | 7 |  
  |--------|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|  
  | Points | 1 | 2 | 4 | 7 |10 |15 |18 |  
- **Passenger Placement**: After claiming, optionally place **1 of your passengers** on **either city** connected by the route.  
  - Only **one passenger per city** allowed (any player).  
  - You may place on a city already occupied by another player's passenger, **but only if you remove theirs first**.

#### 3. Draw Destination Tickets  
- Draw **4 tickets** (any mix of short and long).  
- Keep **at least 1**, return the rest to the **bottom** of their respective decks.  
- No hand limit.

#### 4. Move a Passenger (Only 3 per game)  
- Move **one** of your passengers along **your own continuous routes**.  
- **Start**: From any city where your passenger is placed.  
- **Movement**:  
  - Move city to city along your routes.  
  - Collect the **top Merchandise Token** from each city **entered** (not starting city).  
  - Cannot backtrack or reuse any route segment.  
- **Using Opponent Routes**:  
  - To cross an opponent’s route, **discard 1 Passenger card** per crossing.  
  - You may cross multiple opponent routes in one move.  
- **End**: Stop at any point.  
  - Score points equal to the sum of collected tokens.  
  - Remove the passenger from the game.  
- Each passenger can be moved **only once** (3 total moves per player).

---

### 5. Core Mechanics  
- **Double/Triple Routes**:  
  - In 2–3 player games: Only **one** route per multi-route can be claimed.  
  - In 4–5 player games: All routes available.  
- **Passenger Cards**:  
  - Drawn like train cards.  
  - Used **only** to cross opponent routes during passenger movement.  
- **Route Claiming with +4 Locomotives**:  
  - Usable only on routes of length 4+.  
  - Count as one card when played.  
- **Token Collection**:  
  - Collected during passenger movement.  
  - Only one token per city visit.  
  - Stacks are public and deplete over time.

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
- **End Game Trigger**: When any player has **2 or fewer trains left**, all players (including the trigger) get **one final turn**.  
- **Scoring**:  
  - **Completed Destination Tickets**: Positive points.  
  - **Uncompleted Tickets**: Negative points.  
  - **Passenger Movement**: Points from collected tokens.  
  - **Bonus**:  
    - **+10 points** to player with **most completed tickets**.  
    - **Tiebreaker**:  
      1. Most completed tickets.  
      2. Highest total Merchandise Tokens collected.  
- **No Longest Route bonus** in Marklin edition.

---

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I place a passenger on a city with an opponent's passenger?**  
  Yes. Remove the opponent’s passenger and return it to their supply.  
- **Can I move a passenger through a city with another passenger?**  
  Yes, but you cannot stop there unless it’s your own passenger and you’re ending the move.  
- **Can I use Locomotives to claim any route?**  
  Yes, including +4 Locomotives (only on routes ≥4).  
- **What happens if the train deck runs out?**  
  Reshuffle the discard pile to form a new deck.  
- **Can I move a passenger without collecting any tokens?**  
  Yes, but you gain no points.  
- **Do unused passengers score?**  
  No. Only moved passengers score via tokens.  
- **Can I use a Passenger card to cross my own route?**  
  No. Passenger cards are only for crossing **opponent** routes.  
- **Can I move a passenger through a city I’ve already collected a token from?**  
  Yes, but you **do not** collect another token.
