## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
Dungeon Lords is a strategy board game where players take on the role of evil dungeon overlords, building and expanding their underground lairs to attract and defeat bands of adventurers. The goal is to maximize your score through dungeon construction, monster deployment, capturing adventurers, and earning prestigious titles.

Victory is determined by end-game scoring based on dungeon layout, imprisoned adventurers, monster count, room bonuses, and titles. The player with the highest score wins.

---

### 2. Components  
- **Central Board**: Tracks resources and events.  
- **Progress Board**: Tracks game progression through seasons.  
- **Distant Lands Board**: Supplies adventurers, rooms, and monsters.  
- **Player Boards**: Represent individual dungeons.  
- **Minions**: Worker tokens used to claim actions.  
- **Order Cards**: Action cards used each season.  
- **Tunnel Tiles & Room Tiles**: Dungeon expansion components.  
- **Monster Figures**: Creatures used in combat.  
- **Trap Cards**: Defensive mechanisms.  
- **Food, Gold, and Imp Tokens**: Resources.  
- **Evil Counter & Evilometer**: Track player evilness.  
- **Combat Cards**: Determine battle conditions.  
- **Event Tiles**: Trigger special effects each season.  
- **Adventurer Tiles**: Represent incoming parties.  
- **Damage Counters**: Track adventurer health.  
- **Progress Marker**: Tracks game phase.  

*Expansion Components (if used):*  
- Festival Season (FS), Dungeon Setup (DS), Minions Bearing Gifts (MBG)  
- Unique Opportunity Boards (FS)  
- Improved Tunnel Tiles, Monster Expertise Tiles, Pet Cards (FS)  
- Item Tokens (MBG)  
- Expansion Tiles (Solo variant)

### 3. Setup  

#### Central Board  
- Place Food, Gold, Imp, and Tunnel Tokens on designated spaces.  
- Place 3 Trolls with the Imps.  
- Shuffle **First Year Combat Cards**; randomly select 4 and place face-down on the town space. Return the rest to the box.  
  - *(PS expansion)*: Select 5 Combat Cards instead.  
- Shuffle all **Trap Cards** and place face-down on the Trap spot.  
- Place **Paladins** in upper left; darker tiles on top.  
  - *(FS)*: For multiple Paladins per year, place weaker on top of stronger.  
- Each player places an **Evil Counter** on the starting space of the Evilometer.  
  - Also place a counter for each unused player.  
  - *(FS)*: Shuffle **Unique Opportunity Boards** and place face-down near the Central Board.

#### Progress Board  
- Shuffle **Event Tiles** and place one face-down on each marked space.  
- Place the **Progress Marker** next to the first space.  
  - *(FS)*: Attach the **Progress Board Extension**.

#### Distant Lands Board  
- Separate **Adventurer**, **Room**, and **Monster Tiles** into two groups by back color (gold/silver).  
- Place each group of Adventurer Tiles on indicated spaces.  
- Place **gold-backed Monster and Room Tiles** on their spots; place **silver-backed** tiles on top.  
- Shuffle **Second Year Combat Cards**; choose 4 randomly and place on the indicated space.  
  - *(FS)*: Choose 5 Combat Cards.  
- Place **1 Event Card on top** and **1 underneath** the Combat Cards.  
  - *(FS)*: Place **2 Event Cards on top** and **2 underneath**.  
- Place **Damage Counters** in designated space.  
  - *(FS)*: Set up **Far Distant Lands Board** nearby.  
    - Place **Improved Tunnel Tiles**, **Monster (and Ghost) Expertise Tiles**, and shuffled **Pet Cards** in their spots.

#### Player Setup  
- Each player chooses a color and takes:  
  - Player Board  
  - Minions  
  - Order Cards  
  - Overview Cards  
  - *(FS)*: Attach **Player Board Extension**.  
- *(DS)*: Shuffle **Dungeon Setup Deck**; deal 2 cards to each player.  
  - Simultaneously choose 1 card, place it face-down, return the other to the top of the deck. Reveal all simultaneously.  
- Set up initial tunnels and gather starting resources:  
  - **Normal**: 3 Tunnels (in a straight line from entrance), 3 Food, 3 Gold, 3 Imps.  
  - *(DS)*: Resources and tunnels as shown on Dungeon Setup Card.  
- Randomly draw 3 **Order Cards**; choose 2 to place in **Inaccessible Orders** spaces.  
- **Non-Player Dungeon Boards (for solo/variants)**:  
  - For each non-player:  
    - Randomly place 3 Order Cards in Inaccessible Orders.  
    - Place remaining Orders in specified space.  
    - Place 3 Minions near the board.  
    - Assign one Non-Player Board to a player; return unused components to box.  
- *(MBG)*: Place 5 **Item Tokens** near your Player Board.  
- *(MBG)*: Each player takes the 4 magic items for their non-player color (excluding the Empty Token).

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
The game lasts **2 years**, each divided into **4 seasons** (Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall). Each season follows this sequence:

1. **Orders Phase**  
   - Players simultaneously assign Minions to Order Cards.  
   - Actions are resolved in turn order.  
   - Some actions require payment (Food, Gold, Evil, Traps).  
   - Players may discard an imprisoned adventurer if no unconquered tiles remain.

2. **Event Phase**  
   - Reveal and resolve the current Event Tile.

3. **Adventurers Phase**  
   - Draw and assign Adventurer Tiles based on Evilometer position and turn order.

4. **Production Phase**  
   - Activate rooms (e.g., Magic Room produces Imps, Workshop draws Trap Cards).

5. **Combat Phase** (after Spring and Fall)  
   - Flip Progress Board; reveal Combat Cards in order.  
   - Combat lasts **5 rounds** or until all adventurers are eliminated.  
   - Each round:  
     a. **Planning Phase**: Choose an unconquered tile near entrance.  
        - Tunnel: Play 1 Trap and 1 Monster  
        - Room: Play 1 Trap (costs 1 Gold) and up to 2 Monsters  
        - Unlimited Ghosts may be played  
        - No play allowed? Discard an imprisoned adventurer.  
     b. **Reveal Combat Card**  
     c. **Battle Phase** (in player order):  
        - **Bard Step**: Bards give Courage (Gold Tokens) to adventurers (front to back, skip Paladin).  
        - **Trap Step**: Reveal and pay for Trap; apply effect. Thieves reduce damage (protect front first). Discard Trap.  
        - **Fast Spell Step**: If Combat Card has fast spell and party has magic points, resolve.  
        - **Monsters & Ghosts Step**: Activate monsters in chosen order. After attack, monster is knocked out (face down) for the year.  
        - **Slow Spell Step**: Resolve slow spell if applicable.  
        - **Healing Step**: If at least one Monster or Ghost attacked, heal 1 Damage Counter per symbol (front first).  
        - **Conquering Step**:  
           - Remove Courage Tokens.  
           - Apply Fatigue (damage from Combat Card) to first adventurer. Overflow to next if eliminated.  
           - If at least one adventurer survives, conquer the tile: flip it over and move Evil Counter down 1 on Evilometer.  
        - **End of Battle Step**: Apply poison damage (e.g., Cockatrice).

6. **End of Season**  
   - Reset Minions.  
   - Replace used Monster/Room tiles.  
   - Advance Starting Player Token.  
   - Prepare for next season.

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Monster Abilities (Key Examples)  
- **Cockatrice**: Attack anyone for 1; +1 poison damage at end of battle.  
- **Demon**: Attack anyone for 7; party skips conquering/fatigue; discard a monster when hired.  
- **Dragon**: 2 damage to all; party skips Healing Step.  
- **Elderbeast**: Skip Fast Spells step; costs 1 Imp.  
- **Evil Eye**: Hypnotize any adventurer to front; attack for 3.  
- **Ghost**: Not a monster; cannot attack first adventurer; attacks for 2.  
- **Goblin**: Standard attack (2); if kills leader, attacks next for 1.  
- **Golem**: Standard attack (4); returns to lair face up (not knocked out).  
- **Slime**: Either 1 damage to all OR prevent conquering/fatigue (no attack).  
- **Troll**: Grants Troll Token (can substitute for Imp, except in tunneling/gold mining).  
- **Vampire**: Attack for 3, or 2 and return to lair face up. Cannot attack priests.  
- **Witch**: Either 1 attack for 4, or 2 attacks for 1 each (same or different targets).

#### Combat Rules  
- **Standard Attack**: First adventurer takes damage.  
- **Attack Anyone**: Choose target.  
- **Attack Everyone**: All adventurers take damage.  
- **Priests**: Cannot be attacked by most monsters.  
- **Ghost**: Cannot attack first adventurer.  
- **After Attack**: Most monsters are knocked out (face down). Golem and Vampire return face up.  
- **Party Effects**: Some attacks cause party to skip conquering, fatigue, or healing.

#### Room Scoring Bonuses  
- **Cafeteria**: 1 point per Troll, Goblin, Slime, Witch.  
- **Chapel**: 2 points per Vampire, Ghost.  
- **Hall of Fame**: +2 points per exclusive title (1 in 2–3 player games).  
- **Pandemonium**: 2 points per Golem, Dragon, Demon.  
- **Violet Lounge**: 2 points per Elderbeast, 1 per Cockatrice or Evil Eye.  
- **Anti-Magic Room**: Skip Fast/Slow Spell steps.  
- **Dark Room**: Vampires and Witches get +1 damage.  
- **Labyrinth**: 2 Traps allowed (no gold cost).  
- **Training Room**: Trolls and Goblins get +1 damage.

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
Game ends after **8 seasons** and **2 combat rounds**.

#### Scoring  
- **+2 points** per **unconquered room**  
- **+1 point** per **monster in lair** (ghosts do not count)  
- **+2 points** per **imprisoned adventurer**  
- **+5 points** per **imprisoned Paladin** (type affects base: Dwarf=6, Elf=4, Normal=5)  
- **–2 points** per **conquered tile** (room or tunnel)  
- **–3 points** per **red mark in Dead Letter Office**  
- **+ points** from scoring rooms (e.g., Chapel, Pandemonium)  
- **+ points** from Pets (if MBG used)  
- **+1 point** per **unused (face-down) Pet**

#### Titles (3 points if exclusive, 2 if shared)  
- **Lord of Dark Deeds**: Highest on Evilometer  
- **Lord of Halls**: Most rooms (conquered + unconquered)  
- **Tunnellord**: Most tunnels  
- **Monsterlord**: Most monsters (ghosts excluded)  
- **Lord of Imps**: Most Imps  
- **Lord of Riches**: Most Gold + Food + unplayed Trap Cards  
- **Battlelord**: Fewest conquered tiles

*In 2–3 player games: 2 for exclusive, 1 for shared.*

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Troll Token**: Can substitute for Imp in most costs, but **not** for tunneling or gold mining.  
- **Ghost**: Not a monster — doesn’t count toward monster limit in battle or scoring.  
- **Paladin**: Cannot be attacked by Vampire, Demon, etc. If captured, worth bonus points.  
- **Tiebreakers**: Use turn order (starting player first).  
- **No Valid Orders**: Must take 1 damage counter per unplayable order.  
- **Dead Letter Office**: Red marks from unpaid costs (e.g., monster upkeep).  
- **Solo Play**: Uses d8 die and Evil Counter simulation; non-players do not score.  
- **Expansion Tiles (Solo)**: Affect adventurer strength; scored at end based on variety and quantity.
