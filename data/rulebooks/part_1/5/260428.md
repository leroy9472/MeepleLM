# Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
At the height of its power, the Roman Empire spanned vast territories and influenced major advancements in engineering, architecture, science, art, and literature. By the 5th century, political corruption, economic crisis, and military overextension weakened the Empire, enabling aggressive barbarian tribes to invade. In *Pandemic: Fall of Rome*, players take on roles within the Roman Empire, working cooperatively to defend cities, raise armies, and forge alliances with invading tribes.

**Objective**: Prevent the fall of Rome by either forging alliances with or eliminating all five barbarian tribes (Anglo-Saxons/Franks, Vandals, Huns, Visigoths, Ostrogoths) before the Empire collapses.

Players win if all five tribes are neutralized‚Äîeither through alliance or complete removal from the board. The game ends in loss if:
- Roma is sacked.
- The Decline marker reaches the 8th space.
- A player cannot draw 2 Player cards.
- There are insufficient barbarian cubes to place during an invasion or revolt.

## 2. Components
- **1 Board** depicting Roman cities and migration paths.
- **7 Pawns** and **7 Role Cards** (Consul, Magister Militum, Mercator, Praefectus Classis, Praefectus Fabrum, Regina Foederata, Vestalis).
- **70 Player Cards**:
  - 49 City Cards
  - 14 Event Cards
  - 7 Revolt Cards
- **49 Barbarian Cards**:
  - 5 red-bordered Roma cards
  - 9 gold-bordered setup cards
  - 35 standard invasion cards
- **100 Barbarian Cubes**:
  - 20 Anglo-Saxons/Franks (black)
  - 22 Vandals (red)
  - 20 Huns (green)
  - 24 Visigoths (yellow)
  - 14 Ostrogoths (blue)
- **16 Legions**
- **6 Forts** (1 starts in Roma)
- **5 Alliance Tokens** (one per tribe)
- **3 Battle Dice**
- **1 Invasion Rate Marker**
- **1 Decline Marker**
- **7 Reference Cards**

## 3. Setup
### 1. Prepare Board and Pieces
- Place the board centrally.
- Place 1 fort in Roma; others near the board.
- Place legions in the supply.
- Place barbarian cubes and alliance tokens in their designated supply areas.
- Place Invasion Rate and Decline markers on space 1 of their tracks.

### 2. Resolve Initial Invasion
- Place the 5 red-bordered Roma cards faceup in the Barbarian discard pile.
- Set aside the 9 gold-bordered Barbarian cards.
- Shuffle the remaining Barbarian cards and place them facedown as the Barbarian deck.
- Shuffle the 9 gold-bordered cards and:
  1. Flip 3: Add 3 matching barbarians to each city shown.
  2. Flip 3: Add 2 matching barbarians to each city shown.
  3. Flip 3: Add 1 matching barbarian to each city shown.
- Place these 9 cards faceup on top of the Roma cards in the discard pile.

### 3. Prepare Player Components
- Give each player 1 random Role card, matching pawn, and Reference card.
- Return extras to the box.
- Separate Player cards: City, Event, Revolt.
- Set aside Revolt cards.
- Add Event cards to City cards based on player count:
  - 2 players: 2 Event cards
  - 3 players: 3 Event cards
  - 4 players: 4 Event cards
  - 5 players: 5 Event cards
- Shuffle and deal starting hands:
  - 2 players: 4 cards each
  - 3 players: 3 cards each
  - 4‚Äì5 players: 2 cards each
- If playing with Vestalis, form an Event deck from remaining Event cards.
- Each player places their pawn and 2 legions in a city matching a City card in hand. If none, choose any city.

### 4. Prepare Player Deck
- Choose difficulty:
  - Introductory: 5 Revolt cards
  - Standard: 6 Revolt cards
  - Heroic: 7 Revolt cards
- Return unused Revolt cards to the box.
- Divide remaining Player cards into piles equal to the number of Revolt cards (as evenly as possible).
- Shuffle 1 Revolt card into each pile.
- Stack piles (smallest on bottom) to form the Player deck.

### 5. Begin Play
- The player with the City card farthest from Roma (highest "days" value) takes the first turn.

## 4. Gameplay Flow
Each player turn has 3 steps:
1. **Do 4 Actions**
2. **Draw 2 Player Cards** (and resolve Revolts)
3. **Invade Cities**

After step 3, the turn ends. Play proceeds clockwise.

## 5. Core Mechanics

### Actions (Choose up to 4)
- **March**: Move to an adjacent city. Take up to 3 legions.
- **Sail**: Discard a City card matching the destination port‚Äôs color to move there. Take up to 3 legions.
- **Fortify**: Discard a City card matching your city to place a fort (max 1 per city). If no forts available, move one from another city.
- **Recruit Army**: Add legions equal to the current Invasion Rate. Requires a fort in your city.
- **Battle**:
  - Roll up to 1 die per legion (max 3 dice).
  - Must have at least 1 barbarian in city.
  - Results:

    - ‚öîÔ∏è+üé≤: Remove 1 of each
    - ‚öîÔ∏è√ó2 + üé≤: Remove 2 barbarians, 1 legion
  - Remove as many barbarians as possible; choice of color.
- **Plot**:
  - If another player is in your city, exchange a matching City card (give or take).
  - If hand exceeds 7 cards, discard or play an Event card.
- **Forge Alliance**:
  - Discard required cards of a tribe's color:
    - Vandals: 5 cards
    - Visigoths: 5 cards
    - Huns: 4 cards
    - Anglo-Saxons & Franks: 4 cards
    - Ostrogoths: 3 cards
  - Must have at least 1 barbarian of that tribe in your city.
  - Place alliance token in bottom-left corner.
- **Enlist Barbarians**:
  - Discard a card matching an allied tribe‚Äôs color.
  - Remove all barbarians of that color from your city.
  - Add up to that many legions from supply.

### Role Abilities
- **Consul**:
  - As an action: Add 1 legion to any city with a fort.
  - As an action: Add 1 legion to your city.
  - Passive: Start with +1 legion in your city.
- **Magister Militum**:
  - Battle: Lose 1 fewer legion.
  - Passive: Remove 2 barbarians from your city.
- **Mercator**:
  - Once per turn: Give/take a matching-color card to/from another player in your city.
  - Can forge alliance without matching barbarian in city.
  - Passive: Remove 1 barbarian and 1 legion from your city.
- **Praefectus Classis**:
  - As an action: Sail between any ports (no card discard needed). Take up to 3 legions.
  - If in a port: Discard matching-color City card to add up to 2 legions.
  - Passive: If in a port, remove 1 barbarian from your city.
- **Praefectus Fabrum**:
  - As an action: Remove 2 legions to build a fort in your city.
  - As an action: Discard a City card to move:
    - From a fortified city to any city, or
    - From any city to a fortified city.
    - Take up to 3 legions.
  - Passive: If in a city with a fort, remove 2 barbarians.
- **Regina Foederata**:
  - When Marching or Sailing: Move up to 3 barbarians and/or legions with you (max 3 of one color per city).
  - Moving barbarians does not trigger defense.
  - Once per turn: Do Enlist Barbarians without discarding a card.
  - After Battle: Remove 1 barbarian; if you do, add up to 1 legion.
- **Vestalis**:
  - Setup: Unused Event cards form a facedown Event deck.
  - At any time: Discard a City card matching your city‚Äôs color to draw 1 card from the Event deck.
  - Draw 3 cards during Draw step; return 1 to the top of the deck.
  - Passive: Remove 1 legion from your city.

### Draw 2 Player Cards
- Draw top 2 cards from Player deck.
- If fewer than 2 cards remain, players lose immediately.

### Revolts
If a Revolt card is drawn:
1. **Increase**: Move Invasion Rate marker right 1 space.
2. **Revolt**: Draw bottom card from Barbarian deck. Add 3 matching barbarians to the pictured city.
   - Defended city: Add fewer cubes (see "Defending a City").
   - Undeclared city with 4+ cubes: Fill to 3, then sack.
3. **Intensify**: Shuffle all cards in Barbarian discard pile and place on top of Barbarian deck.
- Return Revolt cards to box after resolution.
- If 2 Revolt cards drawn: Resolve both fully (steps 1‚Äì3 for first, then 1‚Äì3 for second). Event cards may be played between.

### Hand Limit
- Max 7 Player cards (City + Event).
- Excess must be discarded or reduced via Event card play.

### Invade Cities
- Flip over number of Barbarian cards equal to current Invasion Rate.
- For each card:
  - Check if pictured city can be invaded:
    - If it has at least 1 matching barbarian, or
    - The previous city on migration path has one.
  - If not, follow migration path backward until a valid city is found.
  - If no city can be invaded, invade the city adjacent to the barbarian supply.
  - Add 1 matching barbarian cube.
    - If city has 3 of that color: **Sack the city** instead.

### Defending a City
- **Ambush** (no fort or pawn): Remove all legions.
- **Attack** (has fort or pawn): Remove 1 legion.
- No barbarian is added in either case.

### Sacking Cities
- Triggered when adding a 4th barbarian of a color.
- Move Decline marker down 1 space.
- Return fort (if present) to supply.
- Add 1 barbarian of that color to each adjacent city.
  - Legions defend normally.
  - If adjacent city already has 3 of that color: **Chain reaction sacking**.
    - Move Decline marker down again.
    - Add barbarians to adjacent cities (skip already-sacked cities from this card).
- If Roma is sacked or Decline reaches space 8: players lose.

## 6. Scoring & End Game
### Winning
Players win **immediately** when all five tribes are neutralized:
- Forged alliance with the tribe, **or**
- No barbarians of that tribe remain on the board.

### Losing
Game ends in loss if:
1. Roma is sacked.
2. Decline marker reaches space 8.
3. A player cannot draw 2 Player cards.
4. Cannot place required barbarians (insufficient supply).

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Migration Path Order**: Always check pictured city first, then move backward along path.
- **Revolt Card Draw**: Draw from **bottom** of Barbarian deck.
- **Intensify Step**: Shuffle only the **discard pile**, place on top of existing deck.
- **Event Cards**:
  - Can be played anytime except between drawing and resolving a card.
  - Not an action.
  - "Corrupt" option moves Decline marker down 1 before resolving.
- **Sacking & Defense**: Legions defend against cubes added during sacking/revolt.
- **Chain Reaction Sacking**: Do not sack a city twice from the same Barbarian card.
- **Role Sharing (Solitaire)**: 3 roles share one hand; use green pawn to track turn order.
- **Treasury (Solitaire)**: Unlimited storage; Plot allows deposit/withdraw of matching City cards.
- **Roma Caput Mundi Variant**: Legions cannot enter Roma. Adding a barbarian to Roma moves Decline marker instead.
