## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
PARKS celebrates America’s national parks, featuring art from the Fifty-Nine Parks Print Series. Players take on the roles of hikers traveling through four seasons, visiting scenic trail sites, collecting resources, taking photos, and visiting national parks. The goal is to score the most points by visiting parks, taking photos, fulfilling personal year-end goals, and holding the first player marker.

### 2. Components  
- 1 Tri-fold board  
- 2 token trays (hold all tokens and photos)  
- 48 Park cards (Tarot-sized)  
- 10 Season cards  
- 12 Year cards  
- 36 Gear cards  
- 15 Canteen cards  
- 9 Solo cards (for solo mode)  
- 10 Trail Sites (5 basic, 5 advanced)  
- 1 Trailhead tile, 1 Trail End tile  
- 10 Hikers (2 per player)  
- 5 Campfires (1 per player)  
- 1 Camera token  
- 1 First Hiker Marker  
- Resource tokens:  
  - 16 Forest  
  - 16 Mountain  
  - 30 Sunshine  
  - 30 Water  
  - 12 Wildlife (wild token)  
- 28 Photo tokens (each worth 1 point)

### 3. Setup  
**General Setup:**  
1. Place the board in the center. Position token trays to either side.  
2. **Parks Deck**: Shuffle and place face down. Reveal 3 face-up Park cards.  
3. **Gear Deck**: Shuffle and place face down. Reveal 3 face-up Gear cards.  
4. **Canteens**: Shuffle and deal 1 to each player. Remaining deck goes to top-left area.  
5. **Year Cards**: Shuffle and deal 2 to each player. Each chooses 1 to keep (face down), discards the other to the bottom of the deck.  
6. **Season Deck**: Shuffle and place on board. Reveal top card.  
7. **Trail Setup**:  
   - Place Trailhead tile on far left.  
   - Add 5 Basic Sites (without ★).  
   - In 4–5 player games, include the ★4+ Site (Sunshine + Water).  
   - Shuffle 1 Advanced Site (with ★) into the Basic Sites.  
   - Shuffle and lay out trail tiles left to right.  
   - End with Trail End tile (non-solo side).  
8. **Weather Pattern**: Starting from the 2nd trail site, place tokens as shown on the Season card, repeating pattern to the end.  
9. **Player Setup**:  
   - Each player takes 2 hikers and a matching campfire.  
   - Place hikers on Trailhead.  
   - First Hiker Marker goes to player who most recently went hiking.  
   - Camera goes to player on their right.  

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
- **Turn Order**: Player with First Hiker Marker starts. Turns proceed clockwise.  
- **On a Turn**:  
  1. Move one hiker forward to an unoccupied trail site (or use Campfire to share).  
  2. Collect any weather token on the site.  
  3. Perform the site’s action.  
- **Trail End**: When a hiker reaches Trail End:  
  - Relight Campfire.  
  - Choose one action: Reserve a Park, Buy Gear, or Visit a Park.  
- **End of Season Trigger**: When only one hiker remains on the trail, that player must move to Trail End and take an action. This ends the season.  
- **Season Reset**:  
  1. Player with Camera may spend 1 token to take a photo.  
  2. Empty all canteens (return water to supply).  
  3. Return all hikers to Trailhead.  
  4. Pick up trail sites, add 1 Advanced Site, shuffle, and rebuild trail.  
  5. Reveal next Season card, apply weather pattern.  
  6. Player with First Hiker Marker starts next season.  
- Game lasts 4 seasons.

### 5. Core Mechanics  
**Trail Sites (Basic):**  
- Forest: Gain 1 Forest  
- Mountain: Gain 1 Mountain  
- Valley: Gain 2 Sunshine  
- Ocean: Gain 2 Water  
- Vista: Draw a Canteen card OR spend 2 tokens to take a photo and gain the Camera  
- Waterfall (4+ players only): Gain 1 Sunshine and 1 Water  
- Wildlife: Spend 1 token to gain 1 Wildlife token  

**Advanced Sites:**  
- Lodge: Spend 1 token to gain any 1 token (not Wildlife). May do a second time.  
- Lookout: Reserve or Visit a Park OR Buy a Gear card  
- River: Spend 1 Water to copy action of another occupied site  

**Canteens:**  
- Fill with Water gained *on that turn* to activate.  
- Water remains until end of season.  
- Action counts toward season effects.  

**Camera & Photos:**  
- Spend 2 tokens at Vista to take a photo and gain the Camera.  
- With Camera, photos cost 1 token.  
- At season end, player with Camera may take 1 photo for 1 token.  
- Photos are worth 1 point each.  

**Reserve a Park:**  
- Take 1 of 3 face-up Parks or top of deck.  
- Place horizontally in play area.  
- First player to do this at Trail End gains First Hiker Marker.  
- Park is replaced immediately.  

**Buy Gear:**  
- Pay Sunshine cost.  
- First player (or first two in 4–5 player games) gets 1 Sunshine discount.  
- Gear has ongoing abilities and possibly an instant action.  

**Visit a Park:**  
- Pay exact tokens shown on card (Forest, Mountain, Sunshine, Water).  
- Wildlife can substitute any token (except for filling canteens).  
- Place card vertically in score area.  
- Draw replacement.  

**Campfire:**  
- Flip to extinguished to share a site with another hiker.  
- Relights when any of your hikers reaches Trail End.  

**Token Limit:**  
- Maximum of 12 tokens in supply.  
- Discard down at end of turn if over limit.  

**Gear Cards:**  
- Ongoing abilities remain active.  
- Instant actions resolve upon purchase.  
- Some reference specific trail sites; these activate when camping at that site.  

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
Game ends after 4th season. Score:  
1. **Parks**: Sum of points on visited Park cards.  
2. **Photos**: 1 point per photo.  
3. **Year Card**: Points for meeting personal goal (e.g., visit X parks with Mountain, take X photos). Only unique Year cards scored.  
4. **First Hiker Marker**: 1 point.  

**Tiebreaker**:  
- Most visited parks wins.  
- If still tied, game is shared.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I visit a site without taking its action?** No. You must be able to perform the full action.  
- **Can weather tokens be used for site actions?** Yes, immediately upon collection.  
- **Can I use Wildlife to fill a canteen?** No.  
- **Does 1→1×2 count toward season effects?** Yes. Each token gain triggers season effects.  
- **Do canteen actions count toward season effects?** Yes.  
- **Can multiple discount Gear cards stack?** Yes. Effects are cumulative. No token cost reduced below zero.  
- **Can I copy a site action with River if it’s occupied?** Yes, as long as a hiker is on it.  
- **Can I use the Camera at a park or campsite that allows a photo?** Yes—pay 1 token of any type.
