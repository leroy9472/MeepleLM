# TRICKERION: LEGENDS OF ILLUSION — COMPREHENSIVE REFERENCE MANUAL

### 1. Lore & Objective
In the mystical city of Magoria, the legendary illusionist Dahlgaard the Magnificent has vanished—until now. He returns to host a seven-week contest among four master illusionists, each vying to inherit the Trickerion Stone, a relic that grants supernatural intellect, speed, and perception. The player who earns the most fame through magical performances becomes the next Legend of Illusion.

**Objective**: Acquire the most fame points over seven rounds. Fame is earned primarily through performing tricks in the Theater, with additional points awarded at game end.

### 2. Components
- **Main Game Board**: Central board with Locations (Downtown, Market Row, Workshop, Dark Alley, Theater), Fame Track, Turn Counter, and Prophecy Orb.
- **Player Game Boards**: Personal boards for each player, serving as their Workshop and headquarters.
- **Specialist Board Extensions**: Mini-boards for Manager, Assistant, and Engineer, adding special abilities.
- **Wooden Character Disks**: Represent Magicians, Apprentices, and Specialists.
- **Trick Cards** (48): Divided into 4 categories (Mechanical, Spiritual, Escape, Optical), 12 per category, 3 levels (Fame Threshold: 1, 16, 36).
- **Assignment Cards**: Permanent (10 per player) and Special (12 per Location) used to assign Characters to Locations.
- **Performance Cards** (40): Represent shows in the Theater; include Riverside Theater, Grand Magorian, Magnus Pantheon.
- **Components** (96): Basic (1 Coin), Advanced (2 Coins), Superior (3 Coins).
- **Coins**: 28 × 1 Coin, 16 × 5 Coins.
- **Trickerion Shards** (50): Resource used to buy Action Points or score end-game fame.
- **Trickerion Stone**: Turn counter.
- **Magician Cards & Posters**: Player identity and advertising tools.
- **Trick & Symbol Markers**: Used to prepare and perform tricks.
- **Downtown Dice** (6): Determine available actions in Downtown each turn.
- **Turn Setup Cards** (12): Used in Duel of Magicians variant.
- **Magician Power Cards** (90): Used in Magician Powers variant.
- **Prophecy Tokens** (27): Optional, used with Dark Alley.
- **Magician Workbooks**: Reference sheets.

### 3. Setup
#### General Setup
1. Place Main Game Board in center.
2. Sort Trick cards into 4 face-up decks by category; place above Dahlgaard Residence in Downtown.
3. Fill Market Row Buy Area with 1 Wood, 1 Metal, 1 Glass, 1 Fabric.
4. Create Performance deck: 2 Riverside, 2 Grand Magorian, 2 Magnus Pantheon (bottom to top); place face down.
5. Fill Theater with (player count − 1) face-up Riverside Theater cards.
6. Place all resources (Components, Shards, Coins) in supply.
7. Shuffle 4 Special Assignment decks; place in Dark Alley.
8. Place Trickerion Stone on Turn I.

#### Player Setup
1. In clockwise order, each player:
   - Chooses a Magician with unselected Favorite Trick category.
   - Takes matching Player Game Board, Magician card, Poster, and components.
   - Receives:
     - 1 Level 1 Trick from Favorite category, marked with Symbol marker.
     - Components totaling 2 Coin value.
     - Starting hand of Assignment cards:
       - Without Dark Alley: 3 Theater, 2 Workshop, 2 Market Row, 2 Downtown.
       - With Dark Alley: 2 Theater, 1 each of Workshop, Market Row, Downtown, Dark Alley.
     - 1 Magician disk + 1 Apprentice disk on board.
     - 1 Specialist (Manager, Assistant, Engineer) + extension + bonus:
       - **Manager**: +2 Coin value Components on Manager’s extension (counts as +1 each).
       - **Engineer**: +1 Level 1 Trick on Engineer’s extension (gains +1 Trick marker when prepared).
       - **Assistant**: +1 Apprentice on Assistant’s extension (no wage).
     - 1 Trickerion Shard.
     - Starting Coins: 10/12/14/16 based on Initiative Order.
2. Place player markers on Fame Track at 5.
3. Randomly assign Initiative Order (1st–4th).
4. With Dark Alley: draw 3 random Prophecies; place on Fortune Teller orb.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
Each of the 7 rounds follows this sequence:
1. **Roll Dice**: Roll 6 Downtown dice to determine available Tricks, Characters, and Coins.
2. **Set Initiative Order**: Reorder players from lowest to highest Fame (reverse ties).
3. **Advertise**: Optional—pay 1–4 Coins (based on Initiative) to gain 2 Fame.
4. **Assignment Phase**: Simultaneously place Assignment cards face-down under Characters.
5. **Place Characters Phase**: Reveal cards; place Characters one at a time in Initiative Order.
   - Characters gain Action Points (base + slot modifier).
   - May spend 1 Shard for +1 Action Point (except Theater).
   - Take Actions at assigned Location.
6. **Performance Phase**: In Thursday → Sunday order:
   - Players with Magician on Performance slot choose a Performance card with their Trick marker.
   - Perform all Tricks on the card.
   - All Trick owners gain Yields.
   - Performer gains bonuses.
7. **End Turn Phase**:
   - Pay Wages: 1 Coin per non-Idle Apprentice, 2 Coins per non-Idle Specialist.
   - Return Characters and Posters.
   - Orders Arrive: Move ordered Components to Buy Area.
   - Clear Quick Order slot.
   - Rotate Performance cards clockwise; discard last, add new from deck.
   - Advance Trickerion Stone.
   - With Dark Alley: Move Prophecies, discard used Special Assignment cards.

### 5. Core Mechanics
#### Action Points
- **Base AP**:
  - Apprentice: 1
  - Specialist: 2
  - Magician: 3½
- **Modifiers**:
  - Slot bonuses/penalties on Main Board.
  - +1 AP for 1 Shard (except Theater).
- AP must be spent immediately.

#### Locations & Actions
**Downtown**:
- **Learn Trick (3½)**: Choose Trick matching die or Favorite category. Must meet Fame Threshold (pay difference in Coins). Set die to X.
- **Hire Character (3½)**: Place Character matching die on Inn. Set die to X.
- **Take Coins (3½)**: Take Coins equal to die roll. Set die to X.
- **Reroll Die (1½)**: Reroll one die.
- **Set Die (2½)**: Set one die to any face.

**Market Row**:
- **Buy (1½)**: Buy 1–3 of one Component type. Pay cost (1/2/3 Coins).
- **Bargain (1½)**: Reduce total Buy cost by 1 Coin per use (min 1 Coin).
- **Order (1½)**: Place Component in Order Area (arrives next turn).
- **Quick Order (2½)**: Place Component in Quick Order slot (available this turn, +1 Coin cost).

**Workshop**:
- **Prepare Trick (1–3½)**: Spend AP equal to cost on card. Place Trick markers if Components are met and no markers on card. Components not spent.
- **Move Trick (1½)**: Move Trick to/from Engineer’s slot.
- **Move Components (1½)**: Move Component to/from Manager’s slot.
- **Move Apprentice (1½)**: Move Apprentice to/from Assistant’s slot.

**Theater**:
- **Set Up Trick (1½)**: Place Trick marker on Performance card. Category symbol must be in Link circle. No duplicate color+symbol.
- **Reschedule (1½)**: Move own Trick marker (no Link bonus).
- **Perform (Magician only)**: Magician on Performance slot triggers Perform in Performance Phase.

**Dark Alley**:
- **Draw First Card (1½)**: Draw 2 Special Assignment cards, keep 1.
- **Draw Further Cards (2½)**: Draw additional card (same as above).
- **Fortune Telling (1½)**: Rotate Pending Prophecies clockwise.

#### Trick Links
- When two Trick markers of same category are adjacent in a Link circle, a Link is formed.
- Creator gains bonus based on level of Trick placed:
  - Level 1: 1 Fame or 1 Coin
  - Level 2: 2 Fame or 2 Coins
  - Level 3: 3 Fame or 3 Coins
- If Link circle has Shard symbol, each player with a marker in the Link gains 1 Shard (owner gains only 1 even if both are theirs).

#### Performance Phase
- In Thursday → Sunday order:
  - Performer chooses a Performance card with at least one of their Trick markers.
  - All Trick owners gain Yields from their Tricks.
  - **Yield Modifiers**:
    - Thursday: −1 Fame, −1 Coin per Trick
    - Sunday: +1 Fame, +1 Coin per Trick
    - If owner has no Theater presence, use Performer’s modifier.
  - **Performer Bonuses**:
    - 1 Fame per Link in Performance
    - Specialist Bonuses: Assistant (+2 Fame), Manager (+3 Coins), Engineer (+1 Shard)
    - Performance Card Bonus (printed on card)
- Return all Trick markers to owners’ supplies.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
#### Base Game (5 Rounds)
- Game ends after 5 turns.
- Final Scoring:
  - 1 Fame per Trickerion Shard
  - 1 Fame per 3 leftover Coins (round down)
  - 2 Fame per Apprentice
  - 3 Fame per Specialist
- Max 20 Fame per category.

#### With Dark Alley (7 Rounds)
- Game ends after 7 turns.
- Final Scoring:
  - 1 Fame per Trickerion Shard
  - 1 Fame per 3 leftover Coins
  - 2 Fame per unused Special Assignment card
  - Bonus Fame for Level 3 Tricks if condition met and Components owned.
- Max 20 Fame per category.

#### With Magician Powers
- Trickerion Shards do not score Fame.
- Other scoring as per Dark Alley.

**Tiebreaker**: Player ahead in Initiative Order wins.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Can I prepare a trick without Components?** No. Components must be present.
- **Are Components spent when preparing?** No. Components are reused.
- **Can I have more than 3 of a Component?** No. Maximum 3 per type.
- **Can I return Tricks?** Yes. Return to supply; all markers return to owner.
- **Can I perform a trick I didn’t prepare?** No. Must be prepared (have markers).
- **Can I place multiple Characters on different weekdays?** No. All Characters must be on the same weekday.
- **Can I use Special Assignment card for any action?** Only the enhanced action (or forfeit for +1 AP).
- **What if I can’t pay Wages?** Lose 2 Fame per unpaid Coin.
- **Can I prepare a trick that already has markers?** No. Must have zero markers.
- **Can I use Engineer’s bonus on a trick not on his slot?** No. Only tricks on Engineer’s slot gain +1 marker.
