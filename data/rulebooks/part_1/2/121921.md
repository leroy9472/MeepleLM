## Robinson Crusoe: Adventures on the Cursed Island  
Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective
Robinson Crusoe: Adventures on the Cursed Island is a cooperative survival game where players take on the roles of castaways stranded on a mysterious, dangerous island. Players must work together to survive the harsh elements, fend off threats, and complete scenario-specific objectives before the final round.

**Victory Condition:**  
- Fulfill the scenario‚Äôs objective before the final round ends.  
- All characters must survive until the objective is achieved (except in scenarios 1, 3, and 4, which do not require survival to the end of the round).

**Defeat Conditions:**  
- Any character dies (reaches 2 Wounds).  
- Scenario objective is not completed by the end of the final round.

### 3. Setup
1. **Select Scenario:** Choose a scenario card and follow its specific setup instructions.
2. **Board Setup:**
   - Place Morale marker at 0.
   - Place Weapon marker at 0.
   - Place 9 starting and 5 random Invention cards on the board (Invention side up).
   - Shuffle remaining Inventions into a deck.
   - Place Island tile 8 on the board; place Camp token (Camp side up) on it.
   - Place "Food Crates" Wreckage card on the rightmost Threat Action space.
3. **Player Setup:**
   - Each player chooses a character (except Soldier in 1- or 2-player games).
   - Take matching Character card, 2 pawns, and Wound marker.
   - Place Wound marker on the leftmost space of life track.
4. **Neutral Characters:**
   - **Friday:** Included in 1- or 2-player games; white pawn and Wound marker placed.
   - **Dog:** Included in solo or 3-player games; pink pawn placed.
5. **Decks:**
   - Shuffle and place Adventure decks (Building, Gathering, Exploration).
   - Shuffle Beast deck and place nearby.
   - Shuffle Mystery deck and place nearby.
   - Shuffle Starting Items; draw 2, place face up; return rest to box.
   - Create Event deck: half Book-icon cards, half Adventure-icon cards (number based on scenario rounds, rounded up).
6. **First Player:** Youngest player takes First Player token.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
Each round consists of six phases, in order:

#### 1. Event Phase (Skip Round 1)
- Draw top card from Event deck.
- If Adventure or Mystery card, resolve effect, discard, draw again.
- If Event card:
  - If ? icon, place matching ?-token on corresponding Adventure deck.
  - If Book icon, resolve scenario-specific effect.
  - Place card on rightmost Threat Action space; push existing cards left.
  - If a card is pushed off, resolve its Threat effect and discard.

#### 2. Morale Phase
- First Player gains or loses Determination tokens based on Morale track.
- At maximum Morale, First Player may choose to gain 2 Determination or heal 1 Wound.
- If unable to discard required Determination, take 1 Wound per missing token (Unfulfilled Demand).
- **Solo Play:** Increase Morale by 1 before resolving.

#### 3. Production Phase
- Gain 1 resource per source (food or wood) from the island tile under Camp.
- Place resources in Available Resources space.
- Sources can only be used once per round.

#### 4. Action Phase
- **Plan:** All players assign pawns to actions simultaneously.
- **Resolve:** Actions resolved in order from 1 to 7.

##### Action Types:
1. **Threat Action (1+ pawns):** Resolve a card in Threat Action field. May be repeated if multiple cards.
2. **Hunting (2 pawns):** Draw top Beast card. Resolve:
   - Take Wound for each Weapon level below Beast strength.
   - Decrease Weapon level by amount shown.
   - Gain food and fur.
   - Resolve special effects.
   - Discard Beast card.
3. **Building (1 or 2 pawns):**
   - **Shelter:** Pay cost in wood or fur (based on player count). Flip Camp token to Shelter side. Shelter is permanent.
   - **Roof/Palisade:** Requires Shelter. Pay cost to increase level by 1.
   - **Weapon:** Pay 1 wood to increase Weapon level by 1.
   - **Items:** Meet prerequisites (resources, terrain, other items) to build Inventions. Place in Future Resources.
4. **Gather Resources (1 or 2 pawns):**
   - On adjacent or 2-tile-away explored island tiles.
   - 1 pawn: roll dice; 2 pawns: automatic success.
   - 2 tiles away: requires 2 pawns (roll) or 3 pawns (auto-success).
   - Gain 1 resource per source. Source used only once per round.
5. **Exploration (1 or 2 pawns):**
   - On unexplored spaces adjacent or 2 tiles away.
   - 1 pawn: roll dice; 2 pawns: automatic success.
   - 2 tiles away: 2 pawns (roll) or 3 pawns (auto-success).
   - Draw island tile, place on space.
   - Mark Inventions with matching terrain.
   - If Beast icon: shuffle 1 Beast card into Hunting deck.
   - If Mystery icon: follow scenario rules.
   - Draw Discovery tokens as indicated.
6. **Arranging the Camp (1 pawn):**
   - Gain 2 Determination tokens and increase Morale by 1.
   - **4 players:** Choose between 2 Determination or +1 Morale.
7. **Rest (1 pawn):** Heal 1 Wound.

##### Action Resolution Rules:
- Actions resolved in order 1 to 7.
- Multiple players may take same action (except Threat and Hunting limited by cards).
- Supporting pawns: only the main player is affected by success/failure.
- Resources used must be in Available Resources at planning.
- Determination gained during phase can be used immediately.
- After resolution:
  - Return pawns.
  - Move resources from Future to Available Resources.
  - Flip built Inventions to Item side and place on board.
  - Apply Item effects.

#### 5. Weather Phase
- Roll weather dice as specified by scenario and round.
- Resolve in order:
  1. **Winter Clouds (‚ùÑ):** Discard 1 wood per ‚ùÑ.
  2. **Rainy Clouds (‚òÅ):** Total all ‚òÅ (dice and tokens). Subtract Roof level. For each excess, discard 1 food and 1 wood.
  3. **Hungry Animals Die:** 
     - Discard 1 food (all players take Wound if unable).
     - Decrease Palisade by 1 (all players take Wound if unable).
     - Fight Beast of strength 3 (all players take Wound for each Weapon level below 3).
  4. **Storm Token (‚ö°):** Decrease Palisade by 1 (Unfulfilled Demand if impossible).
- Discard all Weather tokens.

#### 6. Night Phase
1. **Eat:** Discard 1 food per player. If insufficient, players choose who goes unfed; unfed players take 2 Wounds.
2. **Move Camp (Optional):** Move to adjacent tile.
   - If **built Shelter:** Halve Roof and Palisade (round up).
   - If **natural Shelter (tile icon):** Lose Roof and Palisade.
   - **Shortcut:** Lost; card flipped back to Invention side.
   - Move +1 resource tokens with Camp.
3. **No Shelter:** Each player takes 1 Wound.
4. **Discard Perishable Food:** All food except nonperishable (e.g., from Dam) is discarded.
5. **Reset:** Remove skill usage markers, advance round, pass First Player token.

### 5. Core Mechanics

#### Unfulfilled Demand
- If a player cannot fulfill a requirement (discard resource, decrease level, etc.), they take 1 Wound per missing unit.
- If effect applies to all players (e.g., Weather), each player takes 1 Wound per Unfulfilled Demand.

#### Determination Tokens
- Gained from Morale, Rest, successful actions, skills.
- Spent to activate character skills or re-roll dice.
- Cannot be shared.
- Gained during a phase can be used immediately.

#### Wounds and Morale
- Each Wound moves marker right on life track.
- Passing a Morale decrease symbol (‚ñΩ) lowers Morale by 1.
- Healing does not raise Morale.
- 2 Wounds = death = game loss.

#### Friday
- Not a player; controlled by First Player.
- Can act alone or support.
- Does not eat, need shelter, or suffer Weather.
- On ? roll, takes 1 Wound instead of drawing Adventure card.
- Can spend 2 Determination to re-roll any die.

#### Dog
- Only for Exploration or Hunting.
- Supports player pawns; cannot act alone.
- Cannot be used with Friday.

#### Discovery Tokens
- Gained during Exploration.
- Some provide immediate resources; others are abilities.
- Examples:
  - +1 Palisade, +1 Weapon
  - Heal 2 Wounds (with Pot)
  - Draw Treasure
  - +1 food/wood source

### 6. Scoring & End Game
- **Win:** Complete scenario objective before final round ends.
- **Lose:**
  - Any character dies (2 Wounds).
  - Objective not completed by end of final round.
- **Scenario-Specific Survival:** Scenarios 2, 5, and 6 require surviving to the end of the round in which the objective is met.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Can you move Camp if all characters are outside?** Yes.
- **Does Fire Invention give +1 Palisade without Shelter?** No. Shelter required.
- **Can +1 Weapon token be used as reaction?** Rules unclear; thematic interpretation suggests planning required (like Defense Plan). German edition allows immediate use.
- **Can Friday use Dog?** No. Dog only for player pawns.
- **Can you Gather from Camp tile?** No. Only adjacent or 2-tile-away tiles.
- **Is there a limit to Roof/Palisade levels?** No. Unlimited.
- **Can you build Shelter on non-Camp tiles?** Only on Camp tile.
- **Can Determination from failed action be used to re-roll?** Yes, immediately.
- **Does Rest heal special wounds?** Yes, unless specified otherwise.
