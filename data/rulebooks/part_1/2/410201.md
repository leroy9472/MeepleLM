## Comprehensive Reference Manual

### 1. Lore & Objective  
You are an amateur dracologist in the world of Wyrmspan, where dragons roam the skies. Your goal is to excavate a hidden labyrinth beneath your land and entice dragons to roost in your sanctuary of caves. Over four rounds, you build your cave system, attract dragons, and earn victory points (VP) through various actions and end-game scoring. The player with the most VP at the end of the game wins.

### 3. Setup  
1. **Central Setup:**  
   - Place the Dragon Guild board in the center. Randomly select and place one Dragon Guild tile face up (side depends on player count: 2â€“3 or 4â€“5 players).  
   - Place the card display board nearby. Shuffle all dragon cards and cave cards separately. Place 3 face-up dragons and 3 face-up caves on the display. Place remaining decks face down.  
   - Place the round tracker board with the round marker on Round 1. Shuffle the objective tiles and place 4 at random on the board, random side up. Return unused tiles to the box.  
   - Place all coins, eggs, and resource tokens (meat, gold, crystal, milk) in a public supply.

2. **Player Setup (per player):**  
   - Take 1 player mat and all player pieces in one color.  
   - Place adventurer meeple in Base Camp on your player mat.  
   - Place guild token on the "START" space of the Dragon Guild board.  
   - Gain:  
     - 6 coins (place to the left of your mat)  
     - 1 egg (place on one of the two nest icons on your mat)  
     - 3 dragon cards and 3 cave cards (drawn from respective decks)  
   - Choose any 4 cards (dragons and/or caves) to keep; discard the other 2 face-up to respective discard piles.  
   - Choose 3 resources (any combination of meat, gold, crystal, milk) and place them in your supply.

3. **Start Player:**  
   - Randomly determine the first player and give them the start player marker.

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
- The game lasts **4 rounds**.  
- In each round, players take turns in clockwise order, beginning with the start player.  
- On your turn, choose **one** of three actions: **Excavate**, **Entice**, or **Explore**.  
- After taking an action, your turn ends. You must then:  
  - Discard down to **9 coins, 9 cards, and 9 resource tokens**.  
  - Return your adventurer to Base Camp (if exploring).  
- Players may pass at any time. Once passed, they cannot take more turns that round but may still benefit from othersâ€™ actions.  
- When all players have passed, the round ends and **Round End/Upkeep** begins.

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### **Excavate**  
- Select the **leftmost empty space** in one of your three caves (Crimson Cavern, Golden Grotto, Amethyst Abyss).  
- Pay:  
  - 1 coin (base cost)  
  - 0 eggs (for 2nd column), 1 egg (3rd), or 2 eggs (4th) â€” paid from any nest on your mat or dragons.  
- Play a cave card from your hand to that space.  
- In any order, optionally activate:  
  - **"When Played"** ability on the cave card (if you decline, opponents still may activate their part).  
  - **"When Covered"** ability on the mat space (only in 4th column: spend 3 items â€” resources, cards â€” to gain 1 coin).  
- Cave card color is purely aesthetic; any card fits any cave.  
- *Free Excavation:* If an ability lets you excavate for free, skip all costs.

#### **Entice**  
- Select a dragon card from your hand.  
- Pay:  
  - 1 coin (base cost)  
  - Resource cost (gold, crystal, meat, milk) shown on card; some require extra coin, egg, or milk.  
  - Use **Resource Exchange** at any time: 2 resources â†’ 1 of another type.  
- Play the dragon to your mat:  
  - Must go into an **excavated**, **unoccupied** space.  
  - Must match at least one **preferred habitat** (shown on left of card).  
  - Must be placed **left to right** within a cave.  
- Gain the **"When Played"** ability (if any).  
- Note: Max 12 dragons on mat. Tucked cards do not count as dragons.

#### **Explore**  
- Choose one cave (Crimson Cavern, Golden Grotto, or Amethyst Abyss) to explore.  
- Pay cost:  
  - 1st time per cave per round: 1 coin  
  - 2nd time: 1 coin + 1 egg  
  - 3rd time: 1 coin + 2 eggs (max 3 visits per cave per round)  
  - Eggs paid from nests on mat or dragons.  
- Place paid coin and egg(s) on your mat to track visits.  
- Move adventurer **left to right** along the path.  
- Activate **"if activated"** abilities (ðŸŸ¥ icon) as you pass:  
  - You may activate each ability when you pass it.  
- Your turn ends when you reach a **Stop icon** (empty space). Return adventurer to Base Camp.

#### **Special: Hatchlings**  
- All hatchlings require **1 egg + 1â€“3 milk** to play.  
- Three-part ability:  
  1. **"If activated"**: When adventurer walks onto it, may cache milk/meat or tuck a card.  
  2. **"Any time"**: Whenever you cache or tuck, gain a benefit (regardless of trigger).  
  3. **"Once per game"**: On the **third** cache/tuck, gain regular benefit + bonus (hatchling "grows up").

- Most hatchlings live in **Amethyst Abyss**.

#### **Resource Exchange**  
At any time, exchange **2 resources** (any combo of meat, gold, crystal, milk) for **1 of another type**.

#### **Card Acquisition Terms**  
- **Gain a card**: Take from face-up display **or** top of deck.  
- **Gain from display**: Must take a face-up card; if none, cannot gain.  
- **Draw a card**: Take only from top of deck.  
- **Reveal a card**: Show top card; follow ability or return.

#### **Capacity Limits**  
At end of your turn, discard down to:  
- 9 coins  
- 9 cards (dragons + cave cards combined)  
- 9 resource tokens  
Excess gained during othersâ€™ turns is kept until your next turn.

  - Playing certain dragons/cave cards  
  - Exploring caves (some spaces trigger advancements)  
- Each advancement: move guild token **1 space clockwise**, gain benefit on landing space.  
- If you pass multiple benefits, gain all.

  - Place 1 player marker on an empty square of the Dragon Guild tile (max 1 per square, except bottom-right rectangle â€” unlimited).  
  - Gain the benefit shown.

- Multiple players on brown space same turn? Resolve in **clockwise order**, starting with active player.

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
Game ends after **Round 4**. Score VP as follows:  
1. **Dragon Guild Markers**: Score VP based on end-game criteria on each square where you placed a marker.  
2. **Dragon VP**: Sum VP values on **visible** (not tucked, not in hand) dragons.  
3. **End Game Abilities (ðŸŸ©)**: Score all end-game abilities on visible dragons.  
4. **Eggs**: 1 VP per egg on dragons or mat.  
5. **Cached Resources**: 1 VP per resource cached on dragons.  
6. **Tucked Cards**: 1 VP per tucked dragon card (under dragons).  
7. **Public Objectives**: Score VP from round objectives (on round tracker).  
   - Ties are **friendly**: all tied for 1st get 1st-place VP; next gets 2nd, etc.  
   - If you have **zero** toward objective, place marker on "all others" (0 VP).  
8. **Excess Items**:  
   - 1 VP per **remaining coin**.  
   - 1 VP per **4 leftover items** (resources, dragon cards, cave cards â€” combined, round down).

**Winning:**  
- Highest VP wins.  
- Tie? Player with **most visible dragons** wins.  
- Still tied? Shared victory.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I play a dragon without excavating?** No. All spaces must be excavated first.  
- **Do tucked cards count as dragons?** No. For abilities or objectives requiring number/type of dragons, only visible dragons count.  
- **Can I cache resources from supply?** Yes, unless specified otherwise.  
- **Can I use resource exchange during payment?** Yes, at any time.  
- **What if display runs out of cards?** Refill only at end of each playerâ€™s turn. During turn, "gain" abilities may still draw from deck.  
- **Can I explore a cave more than 3 times?** No. Max 3 times per cave per round.  
- **Do "once per round" abilities trigger if I pass?** Yes. All players activate them during Round End, even if passed.  
- **Can I lay an egg if no nest is empty?** No. Each nest (on dragons or mat) holds only 1 egg.  
- **Can I pay eggs from any location?** Yes, unless ability specifies (e.g., "pay from this cave").  
- **Does a dragon count itself for ability checks?** Yes. If an ability checks for "playful dragons in this cave," it includes itself if applicable.
