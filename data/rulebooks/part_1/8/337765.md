## Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
In Brian Boru, players strive to unite Ireland under their rule by gaining control of towns, forming political marriages, repelling Viking invasions, and earning the Church’s favor. The goal is to earn the most victory points (VP) through regional dominance, alliances, and endgame scoring. The game reflects historical strategies used by Brian Boru, a High King of Ireland, who combined military strength, diplomacy through marriage, and church support to consolidate power.

2. Components  
- Game board depicting 8 Irish regions, each with towns connected by roads  
- 125 wooden player discs (25 per player in 5 colors)  
- 30 Viking raider tokens  
- 10 Viking control tokens  
- 16 monastery tokens  
- 60 coins  
- 14 renown tokens  
- 1 Active Town Marker  
- 25 Action Cards (7 red/Battle, 7 blue/Church, 7 yellow/Courtship, 4 white/Royal)  
- 9 Marriage Cards (including Princess of Denmark)  
- 7 Viking Cards (indicating invasion strength)  
- 8 Claim Tokens (with region thresholds and VP values)  
- 5 Reference Cards (player aids)  
- Score, Marriage, Church, and Battle tracks on board

3. Setup  
- Place board in center; place Claim Tokens face down in their slots.  
- Set up supply: coins, renown tokens, Viking raiders, monasteries, and Viking control tokens.  
- Shuffle Viking deck and place face down next to Battle Area.  
- Prepare Marriage Deck: place Princess of Denmark on bottom; place 2 cards (3 players) or 3 cards (4–5 players) face down on top; discard rest.  
- Each player receives:  
  • All discs of one color  
  • 3 coins  
  • 1 renown token  
  • 1 reference card  
- Each player places:  
  • 1 disc on space 1 of Marriage Track  
  • 1 disc on 10 of Score Track  
- Randomly choose first player; give them Active Town Marker.  
- In clockwise order, each player places 1 disc on a town in an empty region (no two players may start in same region).

4. Gameplay Flow  
The game lasts 3 rounds (3 players) or 4 rounds (4–5 players). Each round has 4 phases:

1. Preparation  
- Reveal top Viking card: place number of Viking raider tokens shown in Battle Area; discard card.  
- Reveal top Marriage card: place at top of Marriage Track.  
- If revealed card is Princess of Denmark, this is the final round.

2. Draft  
- Shuffle all Action Cards.  
- Deal:  
  • 3 players: 8 cards each  
  • 4 players: 6 cards each  
  • 5 players: 5 cards each  
- Discard remainder face down.  
- Draft:  
  • Each player selects 2 cards to keep, places face down.  
  • Pass remaining cards to left.  
  • Repeat until all cards are distributed.  
  • Add kept cards to hand.

3. Action  
- While players have >1 card: play tricks.  
- Trick:  
  • Active player places Active Town Marker on unoccupied town.  
  • Must play card matching town color or white (Royal).  
  • Others play any card.  
  • Winner: highest value card matching town color or white.  
- Action Step:  
  • Players resolve actions in order from lowest to highest card value.  
  • Winner takes Primary Action (top of card).  
  • Losers choose one Secondary Action (bottom of card).  
  • Discard played cards.  
- After final trick, discard last card unplayed.

4. Upkeep  
Resolve in order:

a. Marriage Step  
- Player highest on Marriage Track:  
  • Takes Marriage Card, gains benefit, moves disc to bottom.  
  • If Princess of Denmark: choose Military Support, Establish Trade, or Spurn Her (+4 VP).  
- All other players: gain benefit shown on their current space (place disc, gain coin, gain renown).  
- If all on bottom, discard Marriage Card.

b. Battle Step  
- If Viking raiders remain:  
  • Player with fewest Viking raiders loses 1 town (chosen by player with most raiders).  
  • If tie for fewest: all tied lose a town.  
  • If tie for most: losing player(s) choose town.  
- Remove all Viking raiders from Battle Area.  
- Player with most Viking raiders:  
  • Gains 1 renown, scores 1 VP per renown, returns all raiders.  
  • If tied: skip.  
- Player(s) with most after above: gain 1 VP, return 1 raider.  
  • If tied: all tied gain reward.

c. Church Step  
- Player with most discs in Church Area:  
  • Places 1 monastery on own town (max 1 per town), takes Active Town Marker, retrieves all discs.  
  • If tied: skip.  
- Player(s) with most after above: gain 1 VP, retrieve 1 disc.  
- Each player with ≥4 discs in Church Area: may place 1 monastery, then retrieve all discs.  
  • Resolve clockwise from Active Town Marker.

d. Claim Regions Step  
- Flip face-down Claim Token face up if region’s total towns (players + Vikings) ≥ threshold.  
- For face-up Claim Tokens:  
  • Award to player with most towns in region.  
  • If tied: token remains on board.  
  • If Vikings have most: return token to board unless player has Military Support.  
- Monasteries count as 2 towns.

5. Core Mechanics  
- Trick-Taking: players play cards; winner determined by highest value matching town color or white.  
- Area Majority: control of regions based on number of towns.  
- Card Drafting: passing and selecting cards in rounds.  
- Action Selection: primary (winner) vs. secondary (losers) actions.  
- Domain Tracks: Marriage, Church, and Battle tracks provide ongoing benefits.  
- Monasteries: double a town’s influence in region scoring.  
- Vikings: periodic threats that can seize towns; managed via Battle Area.  
- Royal Cards (white): wild; can be used for any town color.  
- Town Expansion: spend 5 coins to claim adjacent town via road.

6. Scoring & End Game  
Final Scoring occurs after last Upkeep Phase.

- Claim Tokens:  
  • Each owned token: score VP shown.  
  • Each face-up token on board: tied players score half VP (rounded down).  
  • Military Support allows counting Viking towns as own.

- Region Presence:  
  • Score VP based on number of regions with ≥1 controlled town:  
    1–2 regions: 0 VP  
    3–4 regions: 1 VP  
    5–6 regions: 5 VP  
    7 regions: 7 VP  
    8 regions: 10 VP  
  • Establish Trade allows counting Viking towns as own.

- Renown Tokens: 1 VP each.  
- Coins: 1 VP for player with most (no tie).  
- Active Town Marker: 1 VP for holder.  
- Princess of Denmark: Spurn Her grants +4 VP.

Winning:  
- Highest total VP wins.  
- Tiebreakers:  
  1. Most Claim Tokens  
  2. Most Marriage Cards  
  3. Shared victory

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- Can you end on same Marriage Track space? No. If two discs occupy same space, the later-moved disc moves down to next empty space.  
- What if no towns available for Marriage Card reward? Effect is skipped.  
- Can you expand without 5 coins? No. Must pay full cost.  
- Do monasteries count during Battle Step? No. Only for Claim Regions and Final Scoring.  
- Can a town have two monasteries? No. Maximum one per town.  
- Can you look at discard pile? No.  
- Can you take actions out of order? No. Resolve all symbols left to right.  
- What if no one has Viking raiders during Battle Step? No town lost; proceed to rewards.  
- Can Royal cards be used in any trick? Yes, as wilds for any town color.
