### 1. Lore & Objective  
The Mad King Ludwig has commissioned the construction of grand castles, and as a renowned master builder, you are tasked with co-designing two of them. You will collaborate (and compete) with other builders to create magnificent structures.  

**Objective:**  
At the end of the game, each castle is scored. Your **personal score** is the **lower** of the two castle scores you helped build.  
The **player with the highest personal score wins**.  

**Tiebreakers (in order):**  
1. Compare the scores of the higher-scoring castles. Higher wins.  
2. Compare the total number of **specialty rooms** (e.g., Tower, Fountain, Royal Attendant, etc.) across both castles. Higher wins.

---

### 2. Components  
- Regular room tiles (Food, Living, Utility, Outdoor, Sleeping, Corridor, Downstairs)  
- Specialty tiles (Throne Room, Tower, Fountain, Grand Foyer, Royal Attendant)  
- Bonus cards  
- Wooden castle tokens (unique per player)  
- Player aid cards  
- Counter trays (Yellow & Red)

---

### 3. Setup  
1. Place the **Yellow and Red counter trays** in the center of the table.  
2. Each player receives:  
   - 1 **Throne Room tile**  
   - 1 **unique wooden castle token**  
   - 1 **Player Aid Card**  
3. Place your **Throne Room tile between the player to your left**.  
4. Place your **castle token beside the Throne Room** to identify it as your castle.  
5. Shuffle all regular room tiles and place them face-down in the trays.  

---

### 4. Gameplay Flow  
The game is played over **2 rounds**. Each round consists of multiple turns until only one tile remains.

#### Round 1:  
1. **Draw:** Each player draws **9 regular room tiles** from the tray.  
2. **Choose:** Secretly select **2 tiles** from your hand to play. Place them face-down in front of you.  
3. **Pass:** Place the remaining **7 tiles face-down under the castle token to your left**.  
   - This signals you are done choosing.  
4. **Reveal:** Once all players have passed, everyone reveals their 2 selected tiles.  
5. **Place:** Discuss with your co-builders (players on either side) and **place one tile into each of your two castles** (the one to your left and the one to your right).  
   - You must place **one tile in each castle**.  
6. **Repeat:**  
   - If there is more than **1 tile** under the castle token to your right, pick them up and repeat the process (choose 2, pass rest left, reveal, place).  
7. **End of Round 1:**  
   - When only **1 tile remains** under a castle token, **discard it face-down** (out of the game).  
   - Begin Round 2.

#### Round 2:  
- Follow the same steps as Round 1, **but pass tiles to the right instead of the left**.  
- That is:  
  - After choosing 2 tiles, place the remaining tiles **under the castle token to your right**.  
  - Pick up tiles from the **left**.

### 5. Core Mechanics  

#### Building Rules:  
- All tiles must be **oriented in the same direction** within a single castle.  
- All tiles must be **adjacent** to at least one other tile in the castle (except the first).  
- Tiles above ground must be **supported** (i.e., have a tile directly below).  
- **Blue-bordered rooms** cannot have any rooms **above** them.  
- **Downstairs rooms** must be placed **below ground level**.  
- Corridor rooms may be placed at any level, including below ground.  
- Food, Living, Utility, Outdoor, and Sleeping rooms may be placed at ground level or above.

#### Room Types & Scoring:  

**Food Room:**  
- Scores **2 points** for each room **directly above and below** it.  
- When you place your **3rd Food room**, immediately draw **5 tiles** from the supply. Choose **1** to place in your castle. Discard the other 4.

**Living Room:**  
- Scores **1 or 2 points** for each room of a specified type adjacent to it (as shown on tile).  
- When you place your **3rd Living room**, immediately:  
  - Place **1 Royal Attendant** in one of the two spots in your Throne Room.  
  - At game end, score **1 point per room** in your castle that has the **wall hanging** shown for that attendant.  
  - Also, immediately place a **Tower Tile**.

**Utility Room:**  
- Scores **1 point** for each room of the same type **connected** to it.  
  - "Connected" means a path can be traced using only that room type.  
- When you place your **3rd Utility room**, immediately:  
  - Draw **3 bonus cards**, choose **1** to keep (place face-up beneath your castle).  
  - Score points at game end as indicated on the card.

**Outdoor Room:**  
- Scores **1 point** for each room of the same type **anywhere** in the castle.  
- When you place your **3rd Outdoor room**, immediately place a **Fountain Tile**.

**Sleeping Room:**  
- Scores **4 points** if your castle contains **all 6 other regular room types** by game end.  
- Otherwise, scores **1 point**.  
  - (Room placement location does not matter.)

**Corridor Room:**  
- Scores **1 point** for each **wall hanging** of the specified type surrounding it (adjacent tiles).  
- When you place your **3rd Corridor room**, immediately place a **Grand Foyer**.

**Downstairs Room:**  
- Must be placed **below ground level**.  
- Scores **1 or 2 points** for each room of a specified type **in the same vertical column** (above or below).  
- When you place your **3rd Downstairs room**, immediately **choose any one bonus** from another room type (e.g., draw 5 tiles, place Tower, etc.).

#### 5th Room Bonus:  
- When you place the **5th tile** of any **regular room type**, immediately:  
  - Choose **any 1 specialty room tile** (Tower, Fountain, Grand Foyer, Royal Attendant) and place it in your castle.  
  - You may place a specialty room **even if you already have one**.

---

### 6. Scoring & End Game  
After Round 2 ends:  
1. **Score each castle individually** using the scoring rules for each room and bonus.  
2. Each player identifies the **two castles they helped build** (the one to their left and the one to their right).  
3. Your **personal score** = the **lower** of the two castle scores.  
4. The player with the **highest personal score wins**.  

**Tiebreakers:**  
1. Compare the **higher-scoring castle** of each tied player. Higher wins.  
2. Compare **total number of specialty rooms** (Tower, Fountain, Grand Foyer, Royal Attendant) in both castles. Higher wins.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can I place a tile directly on top of a blue-bordered room?**  
  No. Blue-bordered rooms cannot have any rooms above them.  

- **Can I place a downstairs room with nothing above it?**  
  Yes, as long as it is **below ground** and **adjacent** to another tile in the castle.  

- **Do I have to use the same orientation for both of my castles?**  
  No. Each castle must have consistent orientation, but the two castles may differ.  

- **Can I discuss tile choices before revealing?**  
  No. Discussion is only allowed **after** tiles are revealed and during placement.  

- **What happens if I place my 3rd room of multiple types in one turn?**  
  Trigger all effects in the order you place them.  

- **Can I place a specialty room from the 5th Room Bonus into either castle?**  
  Yes. You may place it in **either** of your two castles.  

- **Are bonus cards scored per castle or per player?**  
  Bonus cards are tied to the **castle** they are placed beneath.  

- **Can I place a corridor room below ground?**  
  Yes. Corridor rooms can be placed at any level, including below ground.
