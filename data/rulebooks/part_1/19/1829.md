## Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
Risk 2210 A.D. is a futuristic reimagining of the classic Risk board game, set in the year 2210. Players represent global powers competing for dominance across Earth, the Moon, and the oceans. The game spans five years (rounds), with victory determined at the end by total territories controlled, continent/colony bonuses, and influence cards. The ultimate goal is to achieve the highest score through strategic expansion, resource management, and tactical use of commanders and command cards.

2. Components  
- Game board with land, lunar, and water territories  
- MODs (Military Operation Detachments) – infantry units  
- Commander pieces (Land, Naval, Nuclear, Space, Diplomat, Tech)  
- Space Stations  
- Energy tokens  
- Command Cards (by commander type)  
- Turn Order markers  
- Year marker  
- Dice (6-sided and 8-sided)  
- Devastation markers  
- Score Chart  

3. Setup  
- Determine number of players (2–5).  
- Each player receives:  
  - Starting MODs: 35 (2 players), 30 (3 players), 25 (4–5 players)  
  - 3 energy  
  - 1 Land Commander  
  - 1 Diplomat  
  - 1 Space Station  
- Draw 4 random land territory cards; place devastation markers on those territories (remove from play).  
- Roll for turn order; highest roller goes first.  
- Players alternate placing one MOD in an unclaimed land territory until all are occupied.  
- Then place remaining MODs in groups of three across owned territories.  
- Place Land Commander, Diplomat, and Space Station on owned territories.  
- Deal 3 Mission cards to each player (if playing Mission variant).  

4. Gameplay Flow  
Each year consists of:  
1. Bid for Turn Order  
2. Player Turns (in turn order)  
3. End of Year – Advance Year Marker  

Player Turn (in order):  
1. Collect Energy and Deploy MODs  
2. Hire and Place Commanders, Build and Place Space Stations  
3. Buy up to 4 Command Cards  
4. Play Command Cards (may cost energy)  
5. Invade Territories (may gain bonus for conquering 3 occupied territories)  
6. Fortify Position (move units between connected territories)  

5. Core Mechanics  
- **Energy and MODs Collection**:  
  - Collect MODs: 1 per 3 territories owned (round down)  
  - Add continent/colony bonuses  
  - Place 1 MOD on each owned Space Station  
  - Energy collected based on territories and bonuses (see Score Chart)  

- **Commanders**:  
  - Cost 3 energy to hire; can be upgraded to 2.0 for additional energy  
  - Each commander enables specific actions and command cards  
  - Must have commander in play to buy or play their command cards  
  - Commander abilities:  
    - Land: D8 attack/defense to/from land  
    - Naval: D8 attack/defense to/from water; enables sea invasions  
    - Nuclear: D8 attack/defense anywhere  
    - Space: D8 attack/defense to/from lunar; enables moon invasions  
    - Diplomat: D6 attack; special negotiation cards  
    - Tech: D6 attack; draws Advanced Tech card on certain rolls  

- **Space Stations**:  
  - Cost 5 energy; max 4 per player  
  - Can only be built on land  
  - All units in territory defend with D8  
  - Produce 1 MOD each turn  
  - Required for travel to/from Moon  

- **Command Cards**:  
  - Cost 1 energy each; max 4 purchased per turn  
  - Must have corresponding commander to buy/play  
  - Cards have play conditions (e.g., “Before First Invasion”)  
  - Some cards have 2.0 requirement (upgraded commander)  

- **Combat**:  
  - Attacker rolls 1–3 dice (6-sided unless commander grants D8)  
  - Defender rolls 1–2 dice (D8 if commander or Space Station present)  
  - Compare highest dice; defender wins ties  
  - Max 2 units lost per roll  
  - If commander with D8 is used in attack and wins, must move into conquered territory  

- **3-Territory Bonus**:  
  - Conquer 3 occupied territories in one turn → gain 1 energy and 1 command card (type must match existing commander)  

- **Fortification**:  
  - Move units from one territory to another connected via owned territories  
  - Path may include land, sea, and lunar territories if connected via Space Stations and Lunar Landing Sites  
  - Must leave at least 1 unit behind  

6. Scoring & End Game  
- Game ends after Year 5  
- Final scoring:  
  - 1 point per territory (land, water, lunar)  
  - Continent and colony control bonuses  
  - 3 points per Influence Card  
- Winner is player with highest score  
- Tiebreakers: most energy, then most units on board  

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Can you play command cards on other players’ turns?**  
  Yes, if card specifies (e.g., “When Attacked”).  
- **Can you stack Stealth MODs?**  
  Yes, multiple can be played in one defense.  
- **Does Space Station count as a unit for fortification?**  
  No; must leave at least one MOD or Commander.  
- **Can you attack without a commander?**  
  Yes, but only with 6-sided dice.  
- **Can you fortify to/from Moon without Space Commander?**  
  Yes, if you control Space Stations and Lunar Landing Sites; Space Commander only needed for invasions.  
- **What counts as a “contested territory” for 3-Territory Bonus?**  
  Must have at least one enemy unit at start of attack.  
- **Can you play Frequency Jam against Armageddon?**  
  Yes, if played before Armageddon.  
- **Do you need Nuclear Commander to play nuclear cards during Armageddon?**  
  Yes.
