## Comprehensive Reference Manual

1. Lore & Objective  
- Android: Netrunner is a two-player asymmetric card game where one player assumes the role of the Corporation ("Corp") and the other the Runner.  
- The Corp represents a powerful megacorporation trying to advance and score Agenda cards.  
- The Runner is a hacker attempting to infiltrate the Corp‚Äôs servers to steal those Agendas.  
- **Primary Objective**: The first player to score or steal **7 Agenda points** wins the game immediately.  

2. Components  
- Two decks: One Corp deck (R&D), one Runner deck (Stack).

  - Runner: Stack (draw deck), Grip (hand), Heap (discard), Rig (Programs, Hardware, Resources).  
- Cards:  
  - Corp card types: Agendas, Assets, Upgrades, Ice, Operations.  
  - Runner card types: Programs, Hardware, Resources, Events.  

3. Setup  
- Each player:  
  1. Chooses a side (Corp or Runner) and takes their identity card.  
  2. Takes 5 starting credits.  
  3. Shuffles their deck, offers it to opponent for shuffling.  
  4. Draws 5 cards as starting hand.  
- **Mulligan Rule**: First the Corp, then the Runner, may shuffle their hand back and draw 5 new cards (one mulligan only).  
- Place decks:  
  - Corp: R&D (face down), HQ (hand), Archives (empty, face up/down).  
  - Runner: Stack (face down), Grip (hand), Heap (empty, face up).  
- The **Corp takes the first turn**.  

4. Gameplay Flow  
- Players alternate turns.  
- **Corp Turn**:  
  1. **Draw Phase**: Draw 1 card from R&D. If R&D is empty, Runner wins.  
  2. **Action Phase**: Spend 3 clicks on any combination of actions.  
  3. **Discard Phase**: Discard down to max hand size (default: 5).  
- **Runner Turn**:  
  1. **Action Phase**: Spend 4 clicks on any combination of actions.  
  2. **Discard Phase**: Discard down to max hand size (default: 5).  

5. Core Mechanics  
- **Clicks**: Basic action units. Corp has 3 per turn, Runner has 4. All must be used.  
- **Credits**: Currency for playing cards, triggering abilities, or breaking traces.  
- **Installing Cards**:  
  - Corp: Install Agendas, Assets, Upgrades, or Ice from HQ. Cards installed facedown (unrezzed) unless paid to rez.  
    - Ice install cost = number of ice already protecting that server.  
    - Only 1 Agenda or Asset per remote server. Unlimited Upgrades.  
  - Runner: Install Programs, Hardware, or Resources from Grip. Always faceup.  
    - Programs use Memory Units (MU). Runner starts with 4 MU.  
    - If MU exceeded, trash programs until within limit.  
- **Advancing Cards**:  
  - Corp spends 1 click + 1 credit to place an advancement token on a card.  
  - Agendas may be advanced even while unrezzed.  
  - When advancement tokens ‚â• agenda‚Äôs advancement requirement, it can be scored (free action, after an action or before turn).  
- **Scoring Agendas**:  
  - Place scored agenda faceup in score area.  
  - Resolve ‚ÄúWhen you score‚Äù abilities.  
  - Adds agenda points to total.  
- **Runs**:  
  - Runner spends 1 click to initiate a run on a server.  
  - **Phases of a Run**:  
    1. **Initiation**: Declare server. Gain 1 temporary credit per Corp Bad Publicity.  
    2. **Confrontation**: Approach ice one at a time (outermost first).  
       - May jack out (except at first ice).  
       - Corp may rez approached ice or non-ice cards.  
       - If rezzed, Runner encounters ice.  
    3. **Encounter Ice**:  
       - Runner uses Icebreakers to break subroutines.  
       - Icebreaker strength must ‚â• ice strength.  
       - Subtypes: Sentry, Barrier, Code Gate, Trap ‚Äî must match for breaking.  
       - Unbroken subroutines resolve in order. ‚ÄúEnd the Run‚Äù subroutine ends run immediately.  
    4. **Access Phase**:  
       - After passing all ice, Runner may jack out.  
       - Corp may rez remaining cards.  
       - Access cards based on server:  
         - **R&D**: Top card (secret). May steal agenda or trash card if cost paid.  
         - **HQ**: 1 random card. May steal agenda or trash.  
         - **Archives**: All cards (turned faceup). Steal all agendas; cannot trash.  
         - **Remote Server**: All cards in server. Steal agendas, trash cards if cost paid.  
       - All unaccessed/untrashed cards return to original state.  
- **Traces**:  
  - Triggered by card effect.  
  - Corp sets trace strength by spending credits.  
  - Runner sets link strength (from cards/identity) and may spend credits.  
  - If trace > link: trace successful (resolve ‚Äúif successful‚Äù effects).  
  - If link ‚â• trace: trace unsuccessful.  
- **Tags**:  
  - Runner becomes tagged (via card effect).  
  - Corp may spend 1 click + 2 credits to trash a Runner resource.  
  - Runner may spend 1 click + 2 credits to remove 1 tag.  
- **Damage**:  
  - **Meat/Net Damage**: Trash 1 random card from Grip per point.  
  - **Brain Damage**: Trash 1 random card + reduce max hand size by 1 (tracked with brain token).  
  - If Runner must take damage with 0 cards in Grip, or max hand size < 0 at end of turn ‚Üí flatlined (Corp wins).  

6. Scoring & End Game  
- **Win Conditions**:  
  - First player to **7 agenda points** in score area wins immediately.  
  - **Corp loses** if R&D is empty and must draw (Runner wins).  
  - **Runner loses** if:  
    - Takes damage with fewer cards in Grip than damage dealt.  
    - Max hand size reduced below 0 at end of turn.  
- **Scoring Recap**:  
  - Corp scores agendas by advancing and declaring score (free action).  
  - Runner steals agendas during access (automatic, cannot decline).  

7. FAQ or Edge Cases  
- **Rezzing Ice**: Only during a run, when approached.  
- **Recurring Credits**: Refill to full at start of player‚Äôs turn.  
- **Unique Cards (‚ô†)**: Only one copy of same title can be active. Second causes first to be trashed.  
- **Paid Abilities & Timing**:  
  - May be triggered during:  
    - Start of turn (after ‚Äúwhen your turn begins‚Äù).  
    - After each action.  
    - End of discard phase.  
  - Active player goes first, then alternate until both pass.  
- **Memory Units**: Programs must fit within available MU. Trashing programs allowed before installing new ones.  
- **Console Limit**: Only one Console (hardware) may be installed at a time.  
- **Upgrades in Remote Servers**: Can be installed alongside Agenda/Asset. No limit.
