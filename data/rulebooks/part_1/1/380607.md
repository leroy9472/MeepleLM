# Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
In Great Western Trail: New Zealand, players take on the role of runholders (sheep station owners) on the South Island of New Zealand in the early 20th century. The objective is to grow your sheep station by improving your flock, building infrastructure, upgrading harbours, and delivering sheep to trading posts. Victory points are earned through various actions and achievements, with the player having the most victory points at the end of the game declared the winner.

## 3. Setup
1. Place the game board in the middle of the table and the sea routes board above it.
2. Shuffle the 8 harbourmaster tiles and place 5 randomly face-up onto the 5 harbourmaster spaces on the sea routes board. Return the remaining 3 to the box.
3. Place the 8 neutral building tiles face-up (side with • visible) on their designated spaces on the game board.
4. Place the bonus tiles market token on the top dashed circle space in the second row of the bonus tiles market.
5. Prepare the A bag (worker and hazard tiles):
   - Take 1 of each worker type and place them on the indicated spaces in the top row of the Job Market.
   - Draw additional A tiles based on player count: 12 for 2 players, 13 for 3 players, 14 for 4 players.
   - Hazard tiles go to their corresponding sections on the board.
   - Worker tiles go to the highest available space in their respective columns in the Job Market.
6. Prepare the B bag (bonus tiles):
   - Draw B tiles and place them in the bonus tiles market starting beneath the player count sign, filling left to right.
   - Continue until the row before the bonus tiles market token is filled.
7. Fill the 4 foresight spaces in Wellington with 2 A tiles (on A spaces) and 2 B tiles (on B spaces).
8. Shuffle the 37 sheep cards and draw a number based on player count: 9 for 2 players, 11 for 3 players, 14 for 4 players. Display them face-up in the sheep market.
9. Shuffle the 24 objective cards and place them face-down. Draw 4 to create a face-up display.
10. Place the Romney sheep, Sheepdog, Ferry, and Kōtare bird card stacks to the left of the game board with their respective tiles above.
11. Prepare bonus cards:
   - Take 1 card marked with a white star from each of the 10 sets.
   - Randomly draw 4 of these 10 cards.
   - Add cards based on player count: 2 more for 2 players (total 3), 4 more for 3 players (total 5), all 5 for 4 players (total 6).
   - Stack each set face-up in order: lowest numbered set under steering wheel, second lowest under barrel, second highest under bell, highest under compass.
12. Place a number of step tiles equal to player count on the respective space next to the Pathfinder Track.
13. Each player chooses a color and takes:
   - 1 Player Board
   - 1 Player Count Tile (placed according to player count)
   - 1 Fee Tile (placed to the right of Player Count Tile, showing coin side)
   - 10 Buildings (sides randomly chosen by one player, all others match)
   - 10 Storehouses (placed on bottom spaces of Player Board)
   - 16 Discs (placed on disc spaces of Player Board)
   - 1 Certificate Marker (on '0' space of Certificate Track)
   - 1 Gold Marker (on '1' space of Gold Track)
   - 1 Ship (on starting water space)
   - 14 Player Sheep Cards (shuffled into facedown deck)
   - 1 Exchange Token
   - 1 Runholder
14. Randomly determine first player. Players receive starting resources:
   - 1st: £7 & 4 Cards
   - 2nd: £8 & 5 Cards
   - 3rd: £9 & 6 Cards
   - 4th: £10 & 7 Cards
15. Each player discards down to 4 cards, places a white disc on space 0 of the Pathfinder Track, and places their Runholder on any Neutral Building Tile.

## 4. Gameplay Flow
On your turn, carry out the following phases in order:

### PHASE A: Move Runholder
- Move your Runholder at least 1 step forward on the Trail, up to your Step Limit.
- Skip over empty spaces.
- Multiple Runholders can stop on the same space.
- If you pass over green or black hands on Hazard Tiles or other players' Buildings, pay the indicated fee to the supply (for hazards) or to the player (for opponent buildings).
- If you reach Wellington, immediately end this phase.

### PHASE B: Use Location Actions
Use the action(s) of your reached location:

#### On Neutral or Own Buildings:
- Use any of the Tile's Action(s) (and any additional Risk Actions) in any order, or
- Use 1 single Auxiliary Action.

#### On Another Player's Building or Hazard Tile:
- Use 1 single Auxiliary Action only.

#### In Wellington:
Carry out 4 steps, then return your Runholder to the start of the Trail:
1. **Income**: Reveal different types of Sheep Cards from your hand. Calculate total Breeding Value, adding permanent Certificates and/or spending temporary Certificates. Gain this amount in £, then discard revealed cards.
2. **Delivery**: Move one of your Discs from your Player Board to a Trading Post with value ≤ your Breeding Value. Follow any immediate rewards and pay transport costs if applicable.
3. **Foresight A**: Choose one of the two tiles in the left foresight space and move it to its corresponding section.
4. **Foresight B**: Choose one of the two tiles in the right foresight space and move it to its corresponding section.

When moving Bonus Tiles:
- Always place in the row with the Bonus Tile Market Token, left to right.
- If placing a tile in the space with the token, move it down a row.
- If the token passes a yellow arrow, refill the Sheep Market.
- If the token passes a turquoise arrow, flip the 4 Neutral Tiles to their reverse side.
- If the token moves off the bottom, the game ends.

### PHASE C: Draw Cards
Draw up to your hand limit (indicated on your Player Board).

## 5. Core Mechanics

### Ship Movement
- Move your Ship up to a number of steps equal to your Sailors.
- Each Sea Route space can accommodate any number of Ships.
- Once you stop, you forfeit any excess movement.

### Harbour Upgrades
If you stop on a Harbour connection space with movement remaining:
- Use 1 movement step and pay upgrade cost to upgrade the Harbour.

#### Small Harbour
- Pay upgrade cost and place a Storehouse.
- Gain indicated Bonus Card to your discard pile.

#### Large Harbour
- Place a Storehouse (no cost).
- Grants additional option for future Trade or Wool delivery.

#### Medium Harbour
- Pay upgrade cost and place 1 Disc.
- Gain indicated Bonus Card.
- May remove a Worker from the rightmost space of a row to acquire the Harbourmaster Tile, gaining its immediate bonus and keeping it for end game scoring.
- If upgrading at end of a Sea Route, reset your Ship to the starting space.

### Pathfinder Track Advancement
- Spaces 2, 4, and 7: Unlock additional Private Building locations.
- Spaces 3 and 8: Gain 1 Gold.
- Space 11: Choose to flip Step Tile to +2 side or flip Fee Tile to ignore black/green hands.
- Space 4 or 6 (based on player count): Gain a Step Tile (+1 Step Limit).
- Space 16+: Each advancement earns £1.

### Hiring a Worker
- Choose 1 Worker Tile from Job Market.
- Pay hiring cost (with any modifications).
- Place on leftmost available space on your Player Board.
- If it covers an Action, you may perform it immediately or forfeit it.

### Investing in a Bonus Tile
- Choose 1 Bonus Tile from the Bonus Tile Market (not from row with token).
- Pay investment cost (with modifications).
- Place in your player area and gain immediate rewards.

### Sheep Market
- Acquire Sheep Cards by spending £ based on number of Shepherds.
- Each Shepherd may only be used once during this action.
- May use 1 Shepherd to add 2 Sheep Cards to the Sheep Market display.
- Buying a Suffolk Sheep Card also grants 1 Exchange Token.

### Sheep Shearing
- Reveal up to as many different types of Sheep Cards as you have Shearers.
- Sum Wool values, adding permanent Wool.
- Gain this amount in £, then discard revealed cards.
- May place 1 Disc on a Wool Trading Post with value ≤ your total Wool Value.

### Placing a Building
- Choose 1 unplaced Building.
- Place on empty Building space or replace an existing Building.
- Must meet Craftsman requirement and pay indicated £ amount per Craftsman used.
- When replacing, pay only the difference in cost.
- Some Trail spaces require Pathfinder Track advancement (2, 4, or 7) to build.

### Gold and Certificates
- Gold is tracked on Gold Track (separate from £).
- Spend Gold during PHASE A or B to purchase Bonus Cards:
  - Steering Wheel/Barrel: 3 Gold
  - Bell: 4 Gold
  - Compass: 5 Gold

## 6. Scoring & End Game
The game ends when the Bonus Tile Market Token moves off the bottom of the Bonus Tile Market. The triggering player takes the token and gets one final turn. Then each other player gets one final turn. No PHASE C on final turns.

### Final Scoring
- £5 = 1 VP
- Placed Buildings: Sum VP on tiles
- Trading Posts: Sum VP from discs on posts
- Harbours: Sum VP from discs and storehouses on small and medium harbours
- Pathfinder Track: VP for position
- Objective Cards: Fulfill tasks for positive VP, fail for negative VP
- Harbourmaster Tiles: VP from individual tasks
- Workers: 4 VP for each Worker on 5th space of any row
- Storehouses: 2 or 4 VP for clearing corresponding pairs
- Hand Limit Space: 3 VP for clearing
- Bonus Tile Market Token: 5 VP for holder

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Disc Placement**: If you need to place a Disc but can't (no discs or can't pay), remove 1 of your Discs from a medium Harbour of your choice.
- **Disc Types**: White Discs can go on any Trading Post; Black Discs only on Black Trading Posts unless no White Discs remain.
- **Multiple Actions**: When using building actions, you may perform them in any order but cannot combine them.
- **Discarding**: When discarding cards, place them face-up on your personal discard pile.
- **Drawing Cards**: If your draw stack is empty, shuffle your discard pile to form a new draw stack.
- **Hazard Removal**: When removing a hazard, take the associated card (Sheepdog for rockfall, Ferry for flood).
- **Bonus Cards**: Can be played before PHASE A or before/after any single action in PHASE B, but not during movement or in the middle of an action.
- **Tie**: In case of a tie, the victory is shared.
