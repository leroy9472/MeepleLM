### 1. Lore & Objective
The "People of the Sun" are a settlement in the Kingdom Death: Monster universe, centered around sun-related themes and survival against monstrous threats. Their innovations and gear reflect a culture attuned to solar symbolism, survival, and ritualistic practices. The overarching objective is to survive hunts against terrifying monsters, develop the settlement through innovation and resource management, and overcome campaign milestones to progress through the story.

### 2. Components
- Settlement sheet (including population, death count, innovations, principles)
- Survivor sheets (tracking survival, insanity, attributes, gear, injuries)
- Gear cards (weapons, armor, items)
- Resource cards (basic: hide, bone, organ, scrap; strange: lantern tube, cocoon membrane, etc.)
- Innovation deck
- Settlement event deck
- AI deck (monster behavior cards)
- Hit location deck
- Terrain cards
- Endeavors (action points for settlement development)
- Attribute tokens (survival, insanity, strength, accuracy, etc.)

### 3. Setup
1. **Name the Settlement**: Choose "People of the Sun" or a custom name.
2. **Initial Survivors**: Create survivors; upon naming, each gains +1 survival.
3. **Starting Gear**: Equip each survivor with Cloth and Founding Stone.
4. **Innovations**: Begin with the "Language" innovation.
5. **Quarry Selection**: Unlockable monsters include White Lion, Screaming Antelope, Phoenix, and others based on campaign progression.
6. **Settlement Locations**: Start with core locations; others must be built during development.
7. **Resource Pools**: Begin with basic resources based on starting conditions.

### 4. Gameplay Flow
The game progresses through a series of **Settlement Phases**, each followed by a **Hunt Phase** and **Showdown Phase**.

#### Settlement Phase
1. **Survivors Return**: Heal all light and heavy injuries; remove tokens.
2. **Gain Endeavors**: Each returning survivor generates 1 endeavor.
3. **Settlement Event**: Draw and resolve a random event.
4. **Update Timeline**: Advance the lantern year and trigger story events.
5. **Update Death Count**: Increase for each death since last phase.
6. **Check Milestones**: Trigger if conditions met (e.g., 5 innovations → Hooded Knight).
7. **Develop**:
   - **Craft Gear**: Spend resources at settlement locations (e.g., Leather Worker, Blacksmith).
   - **Innovate**: Spend endeavors and resources at the Lantern Hoard.
   - **Build**: Construct new settlement locations.
8. **Prepare Departing Survivors**: Choose gear, update stats, select quarry monster.
9. **Special Showdown**: If triggered (e.g., nemesis encounter), resolve immediately.
10. **Record & Archive Resources**: Store unspent resources and gear.
11. **End Phase**: Lose unspent endeavors.

#### Hunt Phase
1. **Choose Quarry**: Select a monster to hunt.
2. **Set Up Hunt Board**: Place event cards on hunt spaces.
3. **Reveal Events**: Resolve cards on current space in order.
4. **Move**: Advance on hunt track; roll for random events if needed.
5. **End Hunt**: Start showdown if survivors meet monster (ambush rules apply).

#### Showdown Phase
1. **Setup**: Deploy terrain, survivors, and monster; create AI deck.
2. **Monster Turn**: Draw and resolve AI card; perform actions.
3. **Survivors Turn**: Each survivor performs an act (move, attack, survival action).
4. **Repeat**: Alternate turns until monster is dead or survivors perish.
5. **Resolve Outcome**: Survivors return (if victorious) or settlement suffers consequences.

### 5. Core Mechanics
- **Survivor Attributes**:
  - **Survival**: Used for survival actions; lost when reduced to 0.
  - **Insanity**: Affects behavior; 3+ = insane.
  - **Strength, Accuracy, Evasion, Luck, Speed, Movement**: Modify combat rolls.
- **Combat**:
  - **Attack Roll**: (Weapon Speed + Survivor Speed) d10s; hit if (roll + Accuracy) ≥ (Weapon Accuracy + Monster Evasion).
  - **Hit Location**: Draw card for each hit; traps cancel attack.
  - **Wound Roll**: (d10 + Weapon Strength + Survivor Strength) ≥ (Monster Toughness + modifiers).
  - **Critical Wound**: Natural 10 on wound roll; triggers ♦ effects, cancels ♣ reactions.
  - **Perfect Hit**: Natural 10 on attack roll; always hits.
- **Survival Actions** (once per round, non-attacking survivors only):
  - **Dodge**: Spend 1 survival to cancel 1 hit.
  - **Encourage**: Spend 1 survival to stand up a knocked-down survivor (requires Language innovation).
  - **Dash**: Spend 1 survival for +1 move (requires Paint innovation).
  - **Surge**: Spend 1 survival for +1 activation (requires Inner Lantern innovation).
- **Knockdown**:
  - Survivors stand at end of next monster turn.
  - Knocked-down survivors can only dodge.
- **Injuries**:
  - **Light/Heavy**: Temporary effects.
  - **Severe**: Roll d10 on injury chart; may cause permanent injuries (e.g., Dismembered Arm, Blind).
  - **Bleeding Tokens**: 5 tokens = death.
- **Gear Mechanics**:
  - **Frail**: Breaks when wounding super-dense hit locations.
  - **Cumbersome**: Requires spending both ① and ② to activate.
  - **Block X**: Spend activation to ignore X hits next attack.
  - **Sharp**: Add 1d10 strength to wound attempts.
  - **Devastating X**: Inflict X additional wounds when wounding.
  - **Set Bonuses**: Wearing full/partial sets grants bonuses (e.g., Rawhide Set: +1 survival on survival actions).
- **Innovations**:
  - **Language**: Allows encourage action.
  - **Embolden (People of the Sun)**: Survival limit +1; survival actions gain +1 survival.
  - Others unlock gear, locations, or abilities.

### 6. Scoring & End Game
- **Victory**: Defeat the final monster in the campaign (e.g., Sunstalker).
- **Defeat**:
  - Population reaches 0.
  - Survivor dies with no one to carry them back (e.g., Disemboweled).
- **Campaign Progression**:
  - Unlock new monsters, innovations, and story events via milestones.
  - Permanent injuries and disorders accumulate, affecting future hunts.
  - Settlement develops through innovations and built locations.

### 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Can a survivor use survival actions while attacking?** No; attacking survivors cannot perform survival actions.
- **What happens if a trap is drawn?** Cancel remaining hits, resolve trap, end attack.
- **Can Block be used multiple times per attack?** No; only once per attack.
- **How does First Strike work?** Must be resolved first; if multiple, players choose order.
- **What triggers insanity effects?** Insanity 3+ makes a survivor "insane," activating gear like Phoenix Plackart.
- **Can gear be removed?** Cursed gear cannot be removed; otherwise, gear can be changed in settlement.
- **What happens on a Perfect Hit?** Always hits; triggers weapon effects (e.g., Bone Dagger: +1 survival).
