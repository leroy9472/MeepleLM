# Comprehensive Reference Manual

## 1. Lore & Objective
In the Caribbean during the Golden Age of Piracy, players take on the role of either influential merchants or feared pirates. The goal is to earn 10 Glory Points through trade, combat, missions, and exploration. The first player to reach 10 Glory Points wins. In the event of a tie, the player with the most points excluding stashed gold wins.

## 2. Components

- Captain cards

## 3. Setup
1. Place board and shuffle all decks except ship cards.
2. Place one face-up demand token in each port zone.
3. Place one face-down ship modification at each port.
4. Place one merchant token (nationality down) in each sea zone.
5. Place one location token in each sea zone except Caribbean Sea (no two adjacent Trade Stations).
6. Draw two mission cards, read aloud, place markers in listed sea zones.
7. Place Spanish Treasure Galleon in Cartagena.
8. Place storm token in Caribbean Sea.
9. Spin weather spinner to set initial wind direction.

### Player Setup
1. Choose color, take player board, treasure chest, captain board.
2. Randomly draw a captain card (do not reveal).
3. Secretly choose ship (Flute or Sloop).
4. Reveal captain and ship, place ship at home port.
5. Draw one glory card.
6. Place cubes on hit locations (Hull and Masts = Toughness value).
7. Take 10 gold.
8. Determine first player.

## 4. Gameplay Flow
1. **Start of Round:**
   - Spawn NPCs if indicated
   - Redistribute merchants if track is full (8 tokens)
   - Spin weather spinner; storm moves with wind
   - Draw Event Card and resolve

2. **Player Turns (clockwise):**
   - Check for storm damage
   - Check for NPC scouting
   - Optional: Attempt extra action via Leadership test (if Fiercely Loyal)
   - Perform up to 3 actions (Move, Scout, Port)
   - Lose loyalty if started and ended turn in same port

3. **Turn Sequence continues** until all players have acted, then next round begins.

### Combat
**Naval Combat Phases:**
1. **Declaration:** Both players declare action (Shoot, Board, Flee). First round must be Shoot.
2. **Seamanship Contest:** Roll Seamanship. Winner performs chosen action. +1 die if maneuverability is 2+ higher.
3. **Action Resolution:**
   - **Shoot:** Winner hits with all cannons. Loser/tie hits with successes (up to cannon count).
   - **Board:** Crew combat begins.
   - **Flee:** Successful if opponent rolled 0 successes.

**Crew Combat:**

### Bounties
Gained by:
- Starting combat with non-pirate ship
- Defeating naval ship

Effects:
- Cannot enter ports of nation you have bounty with
- Naval ships will attack you
- Pirates will not attack you
- Worth 5g per level to other players

### Locations
- **Pirate Haven:** Free recruit & rumor (pirates only)
- **Missionary:** Remove bounties (3g each)
- **Trade Station:** Buy/sell goods (non-pirates)
- **Natives:** Full repair for 1 cargo
- **Reef:** Gain cargo, weapon, rumor, or crew (risk hull damage)
- **Naval Fort:** Move naval ship
- **Prison:** Hire specialist for 5g
- **Town:** One port activity

## 6. Scoring & End Game
**Glory Points are earned for:**
- Defeating NPC, player, or Spanish Treasure Galleon
- Selling 3+ goods in demand at a port
- Selling 2 contraband
- Plundering 12+ gold from merchant or location raid
- Completing a mission
- Finding a rumor to be true
- Buying a galleon or frigate (once per captain)
- 1 glory point per 10g stashed (max 5)

**Winning:**
- First to 10 glory points triggers final round
- After all players have equal turns, game ends
- Tiebreaker: Most points excluding stash, then most stash

## 7. FAQ or Edge Cases
- **Multiple NPCs in zone:** Randomize battle order
- **NPC movement:** Follows event card direction unless hostile player adjacent/in zone
- **Storm effects:** 3 hits minus Seamanship successes; NPCs unaffected
- **Contraband:** Naval ships scout for non-pirates carrying it but won't hunt
- **Spanish Treasure Galleon:** Moves with first NPC direction on event card; gains 5g per move
- **Retirement:** Can retire in port; follow death rules but keep glory and stash
- **Loyalty track:** Effects are cumulative; Fiercely Loyal allows extra action
- **Favors:** Can be used to re-roll non-combat skills, redraw cards, make NPC re-roll scout, or enter port illegally
